o  Plated Through Hole
o  SMT
o  Integrated nRF24 Wireless Controller 

My goal was to enable placement of a flood light without having to deal with different controllers  or wiring needs.  By using WS2811, place a flood light at the beginning or end of a pixel string and maintain complete control. 

The additional wireless version allows the driver to function as its own controller and communicate via wireless connection using the Komby Protocol.  It contains an ATMEGA328 and necessary circuitry to support the nRF24 and Komby Software.

Allows up to 170 flood lights to be controlled with a single universe.  Design includes 3 variations:<br />o  Plated Through Hole<br />o  SMT<br />o  Integrated nRF24 Wireless Controller <br /><br />My goal was to enable placement of a flood light without having to deal with different controllers  or wiring needs. It contains an ATMEGA328 and necessary circuitry to support the nRF24 and Komby Software. <br /><br />Designed for LED Modules:<br />Model: 10W<br />Color: RGB<br />DC Forward Voltage (VF): Red 6-8V, Green 9-12V, Blue 9-12V<br />DC Forward current (IF): 300MA<br />Out put Lumens: Red 120-150LM, Green 200-300LM, Blue 70-100LM<br />Wave Length : Red 620-625nm , Green 515-520nm, Blue</div>

                    <div class="section section-instructions">
                        <h5>Build instructions</h5>
                        
                        
                            <ul class="post-content section-instructions-list">
                                
                                    <li>
                                        <span class="instruction-number">1</span>
                                        <div class="instruction-list-item"><p>The following tools and materials are required to assemble the PixiFlood Driver:</p><ul><li>Soldering Iron</li><li>Solder</li><li>Flush/diagonal cutters</li></ul><p>Additional tools that might be helpful, but not required:</p><ul><li>Solder Paste</li><li>Hot Air Station or Reflow Oven</li></ul><p>Additional tools and supplies required to install driver into Housing:</p><ul><li>Philips Screwdriver</li><li>Thermal Paste</li><li>Pliers / Wrench to remove wire retainer</li></ul><p>Due to the fact this board has SMD/SMT components a good flux is mandatory. I recommend using a re-flow method for SMT components due to the size of the 74HCT3G04DP which is a TSSOP8 package. I used solder paste and hot air.</p><p>Verify and gather all components associated with the BOM (See Component List)</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1117121426090548564.png"></p><p>Sorry, you may have to sort the 2N3904 transistors from the L78L05.  Both are TO-92 and look identical. Check the the back (Flat) on each, depending on eyesight you may need magnifying glass.  The ratio is 3:1  of 2N3904 to L78L05.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3787581428486197064.jpg"></p><p>If you order Pre-Soldered, the next three steps have already been completed. </p><p> Your first assembly step will begin with (Step 4) the 1N5918 Diodes.</p></div>
                                    </li>
                                
                                    <li>
                                        <span class="instruction-number">2</span>
                                        <div class="instruction-list-item"><p>Solder:  PT4115BE89 (3) to PCB.  Use VERY LITTLE SOLDER ! A fine gauge solder would be helpful and check for bridged solder joints before applying power to the board. </p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7177161428480663348.jpg"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6814691428480808933.jpg"></p><p>There are several tutorials on "Drag" soldering of SMT components available on YouTube.  Recommend practicing on something else if you have never attempted SMT soldering before. The PT4115B ICs can be found on eBay and Alibaba for about .20 a piece but may take up to 6 weeks shipping time.  </p><p>There is a quick tip for checking for bridged connections on the P4115.  Do a continuity check between the ground and the 12v test point.  If it beeps, you have a bridge.  This works on the 3 pins closest to the LED since they are ground and 12v. </p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1414171428483407317.jpg"></p><p>If you encounter issues later, I recommended a simple "touch up" with a soldering iron to ensure proper connection.</p></div>
                                    </li>
                                
                                    <li>
                                        <span class="instruction-number">3</span>
                                        <div class="instruction-list-item"><p>Solder:  WS2811S (IC2) to PCB.  Same warnings as the PT4115B.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1568631428481150411.jpg"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4661131428481165088.jpg"></p><p>That is the last of the SMT/SMD components, everything from this point forward is Plated Through Hole (PTH).</p><p>If you ordered Pre-Soldered your 1st step follows.</p></div>
                                    </li>
                                
                            </ul>
