                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">The overall problem that we are trying to solve is being able to rapidly deploy a system of automated movement.<br /><br />An example mission of this would be for use in natural disaster settings during the humanitarian efforts. The robot modules would be unpacked from a backpack, then configured and linked together to perform a task. Tasks can vary depending on the scenario, such as sorting supplies to go to a specific area, or even digging out areas to let standing water flow away from shelter locations. By having the robots help with tasks the effort is in parallel with the human, freeing up time for the human to do complex decision making jobs.<br /><br />The first step towards working on the problem is to create one of the robot modules. This project is about the RDAS Drive module.</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/8781281439814108940.png"></p><p><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WJ8Wnob40kc" target="_blank">Video:</a></p><p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/WJ8Wnob40kc?rel=0" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe></p><hr><h2>How it works</h2><p>The robot is unfolded from its transportation cube shape. The green pieces will eventually be solar panels to harvest some energy. The sides with the wheels touch down and move the robot. The distance sensor in front detects and obstacles and avoids them. </p><p>Tele-operational control from the human is possible with a hands-free wearable headband with haptic feedback. The headband tracks the movements of the human&apos;s head and moves the robot accordingly.</p><h2>Problem</h2><p>Continuing from the description above, these robots are meant to be able to do tasks in parallel with the human. This way they can focus on more complex jobs.</p><p>Being able to achieve the level of parallelization is a key part to the problem; the robots won&#x2019;t be very helpful if they have to be controlled all the time. They have to be able to do their task as autonomously as possible and when there is human input they have to understand what is trying to be communicated.</p><p>The goal of this is to eventually get the robots out in the field helping. It will take a long time with a lot of failures to get to that point. It&#x2019;s all with another moonshot in mind: if the robots will be good enough for Earth, then what is stopping us from making them good enough for other planets as well. The robots could be tasked with starting to build structures on Mars, or go exploring, try to grow a plant (challenging in difficult environments).</p><h2>Applications<br></h2><p>* Reconstruction after natural disasters</p><p>* Exploration</p><p>* Remote actuated monitoring</p><hr><h2>Concept of Operations</h2><p><a href="https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5641/20650760152_f3277d84bd_k.jpg" target="_blank">Click here</a> for larger version of diagram 1</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/8647311439831960870.jpg"></p><p><a href="https://farm1.staticflickr.com/743/20471962140_4857155108_k.jpg" target="_blank">Click here</a> for larger version of diagram 2</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/693741439832035048.jpg"></p><hr><h2>Systems</h2><p>There are two main systems in RDAS: <br>* Motor system <br>* Sensor system + communications</p><p>Systems diagram <a href="https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5622/20037396354_4b36698b4c_k.jpg" target="_blank">click here</a> for larger version</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1097021439831776778.jpg"></p><p><strong>Example Application Diagram 1</strong></p><p><a href="https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5721/20633704596_d922906dca_k.jpg" target="_blank">Click here</a> for larger version</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4185411439832119209.jpg"></p><p><strong>Example Application Diagram 2</strong></p><p><a href="https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5749/20666659381_f0327b36fa_k.jpg" target="_blank">Click here</a> for larger version</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/2496141439832233078.jpg"></p><h2>Developers</h2><p>Another one of the goals of RDAS is to have groups developing on it, so that the robots could be deployed locally if there is a scenario where the robots can help. The experience could then be shared globally and go to all of the other labs working with RDAS, to improve an action plan for how the robots can be used to help in various scenarios.<br></p><hr><h2>Milestones</h2><p>There are a few milestones to hit before getting RDAS into people&#x2019;s hands:<br></p><p><strong>Generic RDAS Chassis Design</strong></p><p>The first of which would be to create a generic RDAS chassis, based off of the CubeSat dimensions. The work done here with making RDAS Drive module is helpful towards going to this milestone.</p><p><strong>Field Testing</strong></p><p>The ultimate tests will be to see how well the robot modules perform outdoors, in the real environment. It has to be able to work! Trying, failing, fixing things, will be the way this milestone is accomplished.</p><p><strong>Weather Balloon Payload</strong></p><p>Here is a goal along the horizon that I am racing to try to meet. That is evaluating the performance of the robot as used in a weather balloon payload. This would introduce it to different temperatures and conditions, as well as test how the robot can function at a high altitude.</p><h2>Electronics</h2><p>RDAS uses a Motor board and a Sensor board, here are both <a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1T_9r3SQRll0dOZQRWpaxNXqfskCUHnLXMtrZkHam8I0/pubhtml">Bill of Materials</a></p><p>Motor board: </p><p><a href="https://github.com/RobotGrrl/fabacademy/tree/master/motor_board_v1.0">gEDA and Gerbers</a></p><p><a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1LlQ5uIKe1PynVcyj8L-FYj3ALGsRrBvFQ7i7EvaG2nw/pubhtml?gid=0&amp;single=true">Pinout</a></p><p><a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1BuAOsEZYxxoL_0D5qq3omPNUV8JjT5pEpe90uYyg8h0/pubhtml?gid=0&amp;single=true">Connectors</a></p><p>Sensor board:</p><p><a href="https://github.com/RobotGrrl/fabacademy/tree/master/sensor_board_v1.0">gEDA and Gerbers</a></p><p><a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1LlQ5uIKe1PynVcyj8L-FYj3ALGsRrBvFQ7i7EvaG2nw/pubhtml?gid=1124540505&amp;single=true">Pinout</a></p><p><a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1BuAOsEZYxxoL_0D5qq3omPNUV8JjT5pEpe90uYyg8h0/pubhtml?gid=1786503307&amp;single=true">Connectors</a></p><p>Electronics diagram <a href="https://farm1.staticflickr.com/700/20471962770_4c50474707_k.jpg" target="_blank">click here</a> for larger version</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3696601439831862311.jpg"></p><hr><h2>Structure</h2><p>Download the .stl and Autodesk Inventor files <a href="https://github.com/RobotGrrl/fabacademy/tree/master/rdas_v0.2">here</a></p><p>Follow the <a href="http://fabacademy.org/archives/2015/na/students/kennedy.erin/project_bom.html">Bill of Materials here</a> for how many pieces to print and suggestions on how to print</p><p>Assemble the chassis- Scroll down to the &quot;Assembling&quot;
