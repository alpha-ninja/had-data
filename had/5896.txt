                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">In 1879 year Thomas Edison invented electrical incandescent bulb. Until today, every one rejoiced and used them. But nowadays everyone thinks about the ecology and resources and money saving. As one of the biggest spenders of energy is lighting ! Thank goodness because the world has come to save LED technology :) You can think that LED is f**king simple element (and it is so) but normal lighting devices with LEDs, it's become to a  pretty complex device!<br />It is not enough to put electricity to two electrodes and that's all. You need LED drivers, controllers, current sources etc. to drive the LED's. Quite complex and it is not enough for all that - you want to drive them with a smart home automation controller (PLC) solution or control them from smartphone or raspberry pi. Normal chain of the LED lighting looks like this: <br />LED STRIP->LED STRIP DRIVER ->SOME raspberry pi ->SUPPLY<br />In order not to harass people, I've decided to develop a universal "simple" LED lighting controller</div>
                </div>

                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><h2>WHY ?</h2><p><span class=""><span class="hps">Why</span> <span class="hps">I want to</span> <span class="hps">develop</span> <span class="hps">this device? </span></span></p><p>Because currently I am renovating may flat and I am going to mount LED panels and LED strips in ceiling. And I want to control them through smartphone + wall switch.<span class=""><span class="hps"> I could not</span> <span class="hps">find</span> <span class="hps">a normal</span> <span class="hps">solution</span> <span class="hps">which</span> <span class="hps">could satisfy</span> <span class="hps">my</span> <span class="hps"><span class=""><span class="hps">wishes</span></span></span> <span class="hps">+</span> <span class="hps">dim</span> <span class="hps">LED</span> <span class="hps">panels from 0 .. 100% with small steps !</span></span></p><h2>THE MAIN<span id="result_box" class="short_text" lang="en"><span class="hps"> TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS</span></span></h2><ol><li><span id="result_box" class="short_text" lang="en"><span class="hps"></span></span><strong>Drive LED panels</strong><ul><li>Switch them ON/OFF</li><li>Dim them using PWM</li><li>Dim from 0 .. 100% ( step 1% or less)</li><li>LED panel maximum power 25 W</li></ul></li><li><strong>Drive RGB or White LED Strips</strong><ul><li>Switch them ON/OFF</li><li>Control individual channel - 3x channels ( red, green, blue)</li><li>Dim them using PWM</li><li>Dim from 0 .. 100% ( step 1% or less)</li><li>Get <span class="short_text"><span class="hps">at least</span></span> 100W from all channels <span class="short_text"> <span class="hps">together</span></span><span></span></li></ul></li><li><strong>Supply input</strong><ul><li>Use standardised power supplies  </li><li>Supply device from 12 V DC</li></ul></li><li><strong>Functionality</strong><ul><li>The main control possibility must be wall switches</li><li>Additional control possibility must be control via electronic device like - PLC, raspbery pi, smartphone etc.</li><li><span class="short_text"><span class="hps">Devices must act as a</span> <span class="hps">MASTERS</span> <span class="hps">and</span> <span class="hps">SLAVE</span></span>S</li><li>MASTER must control, <span class="short_text"><span class="hps">drive</span> <span class="hps">other SLAVES</span></span></li><li><span class="short_text"><span class="hps"></span></span>1 MASTER creates lighting zona, SLAVES  drive own outputs according to the master output ( similar like when DMX512 two devices has the same address )</li></ul></li><li><strong>Control</strong><ul><li>Using wall buttons, rotary encoder, presence sensor<ul><li>1. ONE position spring tape switch -&gt; on/off</li><li>2. TWO position  spring tape switch Up/Down -&gt; dim up/down</li><li>3. TWO position spring tape switch Up/Down + ONE down spring tape switch -&gt; dim up/down + turn on/off</li><li>4. Rotary knob with encoder and button -&gt; dim up/down + switch on/off</li><li>5. presence sensor -&gt; on/off</li><li>* from 1. to 4. cases presence sensor can be used additionally ONLY to turn lighting OFF if there is no movement more then 30 min. As if you forgot to switch lights off!</li></ul></li><li>Through smartphone<ul><li>Similar functionality like with buttons</li></ul></li></ul></li><li></li></ol><p><br><span class=""><span class="hps"></span></span></p>
