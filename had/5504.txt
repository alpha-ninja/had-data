                            <p><p>Ok, so my quick and dirty capacitive sensor is not very successful. Maybe it is that it needs temperature compensation, maybe I am just measuring noise. The biggest problem is that I do not know.</p><p>So time to collect data. I have bought a ESP8266 board and a sensor version of the <a href="https://www.tindie.com/products/miceuz/i2c-soil-moisture-sensor/" target="_blank">Chirp</a>. So I am probably connecting these as a (temporary?) logging solution and send everything to <a href="https://thingspeak.com/" target="_blank">thingspeak</a> or something similar. If only I had some time the next few weeks to put it together.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/93171437155623888.jpg"></p>

                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/5504/log/19568-capacitive-soil-moisture">Capacitive soil moisture</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">2 months ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5504/log/19568#discussion-list">
                                4 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>Ok i wanted to use the Chirp as a soil moisture sensor but they are out of stock :(</p><p>Than I started thinking, why spend extra money on a seperate sensor? The teensy LC has some very capable capacitive measuring pins on its own.</p><p>So from some left over PCB material, 2 layers of copper, I created a capacitive sensor. I cut a strip of 80mm x 10mm. 1 layer is connected to ground the other to pin 16. Next I wrapped this all in electrical tape. </p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/2325281434393512085.jpg"></p><p>From some quick testing it seems to work. Using touchRead() I get a value of aprox 2200 when it is out of the soil (or very dry) and &gt; 3000 when it is wet.</p><p>So this is going to be tested for the next couple of days (weeks). If everything works I am going to create a PCB version of this sensor. </p>
