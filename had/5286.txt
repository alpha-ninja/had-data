                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Utilise cheap NRF51822 board to develop 9+ DOF inertial measurement unit (IMU) for general motion tracking and dead reckoning. Want to accurately track your movement or log sport activities ? Join the project and help make it a reality!<br /><br />One more thing ... bluetooth enables sports watch / mobile connections. Hence we plan to team up with <a href='http://hackaday.io/project/4510-open-source-sport-watch' target="_blank">http://hackaday.io/project/4510-open-source-sport-watch</a> for a complete solution.</div>

                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p><strong></strong>The basic project breakdown looks as follows:</p><ul><li><strong>Phase 1</strong> - analyse project requirements from functional and design perspective <strong>DONE</strong></li><li><strong>Phase 2</strong> - decide on what components to choose <strong>DONE</strong></li><li><strong>Phase 3</strong> - electrical schematics design <strong>DONE</strong></li><li><strong>Phase 4</strong> - PCB design <strong>DONE</strong></li><li><strong>Phase 5</strong> - component sourcing <strong>D</strong><b>ONE</b></li><li><strong>Phase 6</strong> - PCB soldering (bypasses required) <strong>DONE</strong></li><li><strong>Phase 7</strong> - Waterproof casing design and 3D print <strong>DONE</strong></li><li><strong>Phase 8</strong> - NRF51822 and component validation <strong>DONE</strong></li><li><strong>Phase 9</strong> - complete self-test at boot</li><li><strong>Phase 10</strong> - sensor logging to console and storage</li><li><strong>Phase 11</strong> - download data to computer via external serial connection</li><li><strong>Phase 12 </strong>- real world applications with bluetooth paired mobile phone or smart watch (bike, swim, run etc.)</li></ul><p>We plan to publish all documentation and schematics as we move along.
