                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">"Rooster on Wrist" is the name I chose for this project. Mainly a 1.44" tft lcd + stm32f4 discovery an hc-05 bluetooth module and lots code. The main purpose of the watch is to sync and display Time/Date/Weather and Notifications from the phone. The watch uses the internal RTC on the STM32F4 to keep tracking of the time.<br /><br />So fare I managed to get the first watch face done + the companion app on Android. The watch successfully sync data from the phone and displays it.<br /><br />One aspect was to display the data in an elegant way. From the start I was inspired by the pebble and I took a look at my of the watch faces online to create something similar. <br /><br />Concerning the Android App I always thought I was going to write my own but at the very last moments I discovered App inventor which saved me tons of time !! I still believe I will have to write my own code sooner or later.</div>

                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><h2><br></h2><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/IgfxAfX6bOw" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><h2><br></h2><h2>The Code:</h2><p>Just like many of hobbyists out there I use CooCox with GCC for my projects and that&apos;s a major key factor why I chose ARM over PIC32 MIPS , the free IDE and C compiler.</p><p><span></span></p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4297041437307389454.PNG"><p>The first step was to interface the tft lcd , I looked up online and luckily I found many useful blog posts about using the ili9163 tft lcd. Obviously I could have checked the datasheet and write my own library which I eventually did and managed to get something on the screen (a bitmap of what I imagined)</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6363321437307631029.jpg" width="309" height="413" style="width: 309px; height: 413px;"></p><p>This TFT LCD uses SPI and is interface to SPI3 on the discovery board. I use DMA to make it even faster to loads frames ( thought I did not see much a difference )</p><p>I kept working on the display library because it was a key factor in handling most of the work here. </p><p>Next I wrote the code for the RTC unit, which has two main functions RTC_Configuration() which sets the RTC configurationa and TimeWidget()<span class="redactor-invisible-space"> which constantly keeps track of the time and updates it on the screen.</span></p><h2>The bluetooth module:</h2><p>The hc-05 is connected to the USART1 unit pins and triggers an interrupt which stores the data received from the phone and calls  RTC_Configuration<span class="redactor-invisible-space">()</span> to sync the Time/Date from the phone.</p><h2>The Mobile App:</h2><p>When I first started I had to use an app called BlueTerm on Android, it&apos;s an extremely useful Bluetooth terminal app that allowed me to communicate  with the HC-05 module and send data manually to test my code.</p><p>Later on I learned about App inventor by MIT and it was like few minutes untill I got my first companion app done. It reads automatically the time on the smartphone and syncs it to the device.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7488181437308426691.png" alt="" width="175" height="291" style="width: 175px; height: 291px; margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px; float: left;"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1964781437308436094.png" width="185" height="311" alt="" style="width: 185px; height: 311px; margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px; float: left;"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7150031437308466476.png" width="185" height="308" alt="" style="width: 185px; height: 308px; margin: auto; display: block;"></p><blockquote>android sync app screenshots</blockquote><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/8458771437332697700.PNG" width="284" height="140" alt="" style="width: 284px; height: 140px; float: left; margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px;"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/5279061437332716254.PNG" width="289" height="143" style="width: 289px; height: 143px;"></p><blockquote>appinventor2 screenshots</blockquote><h2>What&apos;s Next ?</h2><h2>From the begging the goal was to create a complete smartwatch with complete functionality. </h2><p>What&apos;s left to go:</p><p>- Improving the software, adding more watch faces, adding apps and notification sync.</p><p>- Improving the android app and sync incoming calls and weather.</p><p>- Build a small PCB for with the STM32F407 or STM32F051, add battery (with voltage regulator and controller ic) , add a SPI 16MB flash memory to store all bitmaps.</p><p>- Use a BLE module instead of the HC-05</p><p>- 3D Modeling a case and manufacturing it.</p><br>
                                
                            </p>

                            <p><h2>1) Added 4 buttons on the side:</h2><p>I soldered 4 of the buttons ( I got some vertical standing ones) on the side of the screen.
