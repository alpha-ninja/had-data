IOTsatcom wants to play a role in the CLIMATE CHANGE research. Low cost, easy to use.

The IOTsatcom is about a telemetry system to be used via Iridium satellite network.
Using the IOTsatcom, robots and IOT sensors can be deployed everywhere in the globe.
The scientists are able to gather the telemetry on the web platform via its API, download
tables or to analyze data with custom graphs.

Two-way communication is provided in order to send remote commands from the WEB
to the remote unit. The remote commands include GPIOs, Serial RX/TX to the sensor or
robot, PWM, ADC readings.

IOTsatcom is optimized to run with a battery recharged by a solar panel.
A programmable sleep status is available and special low power features are implemented.

Satellite traffic cost is low because the smart protocol and timing/grouping strategies.

                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><h1><br></h1><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/s7XHVQwPvZQ" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" width="500"></iframe><h1><br></h1><h1>Why, climate change and a short story..</h1><p>This project began with a problem to solve for my ROV. Last year, I needed a method to connect my robot with a web application in order to receive its telemetry, any radio system was out of my purposes because the planned deployment location was very far from everything, even from the GSM network.I started to think to a satellite uplink.</p><p>After months of development, I created a system able to bridge the data from/to the robot via satellite link but I wasn&apos;t completely satisfied yet. There was another  necessary step to do. I wanted to convert my device into an universal product and I had a thought in constant-looping into my head:</p><p><em></em></p><blockquote><em>&quot;What if we would have a satellite radio device, programmed from a web API, easy to</em><br><em>use, so universal to be connected with any kind of IOT sensor or any robot via serial</em><br><em>port, with low power consumption and cheap in terms of satellite traffic?&quot;</em></blockquote><p><em></em></p><p>I started to work on it further and I was proud about the results but after I realized it could be used for the scientific research as well. A very important topic for me is the climate change, I am a sailor and a scuba diving instructor, environment, the nature, the ocean is my second home...maybe the first..</p><h2>..so this is IOTsatcom today!</h2><p><em>A beautiful place I need to take care..</em><br></p><p><img style="margin: auto; display: block;" alt="" src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7652291434539402097.JPG"></p><h1><br></h1><h1>Addressing a world problem ..the CLIMATE CHANGE</h1><p>Many scientists today need to gather an enormous amount of data, many of them work with a limited budget. They are pushed in their pursuit by their commitment to achieve a scientific result. We need them, we need people who care, we need people with strong motivation in science.</p><p><strong>Scientists  today will provide us the sustainability of life tomorrow.</strong></p><ul><li>How to give them access to cheaper technologies? </li></ul><ul><li>How to help them to focus in their scientific research and spend less time in engineering?</li></ul><p><strong></strong></p><p>Other men want  to have sensors too, control, mobile apps, telemetry everywhere. We see a great amount of IOT sensors on the market today but many solutions can&apos;t be provided yet.</p><p>Often the 4 common problems to face are:</p><ol><li><strong>How to power the far away sensor?</strong></li><li><strong>How to send the data to some remote processing system?</strong><strong></strong></li><li><strong>This has to be cheap!</strong><strong></strong></li><li><strong>Can we change the logic of the IOT sensor/robot from remote?</strong></li></ol><p>These questions rise in the context of the ROVs, with complex robots, with big green-houses, monitoring a forest, a volcano, in the ocean or with autonomous systems and sensors. There is a great amount of use cases still difficult to implement.</p>These problems are even more evident in the case of the study of the <strong>CLIMATE CHANGE.</strong>I did a search on the web and the results are interesting, some URL links:<strong></strong><ul><li><a target="_blank" href="http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/rog.20022/full">A review of global ocean temperature                             <h2><a href="/project/6279/log/20068-server-side-and-web-frontend">Server side and WEB frontend</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">2 months ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6279/log/20068#discussion-list">
                                0 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><blockquote>Update Tue Jun 30 00:31:40 UTC 2015</blockquote><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7900991435427015329.png"></p><p>I am working at the server side technologies, above there is my last conceptual work, some details could change between today and tomorrow but we are almost there. The technologies involved are:</p><ul><li><strong>WAMP on websockets</strong> </li></ul><p style="margin-left: 40px;"> crossbar.io at the early stage of the deployment, after will be more probable switch to something in erlang but still WAMP. It also offers tokens for the authorization before the websocket handshake</p><p style="margin-left: 40px;"><a target="_blank" href="http://wamp.ws">The WAMP architecture</a><br> </p><ul><li><strong>no cookies to keep the session at the browser side</strong></li></ul><p style="margin-left: 40px;">I want to implement something new, the WAMP will provides tools to track the user and his session</p><ul><li><strong>REST API to push telemetry via HTTPS from the IOT sensors or robots</strong></li></ul><p style="margin-left: 40px;">This will be in pure python: falcon or wheezy.web, after I will switch to something in erlang probably. The REST API front end offer a solid PUSH only new data interface and very fast. I calculated to support at least 30K requests for second.<br></p><ul><li><strong>Postgresql </strong></li></ul><p style="margin-left: 40px;">Main DB for the data of sensors, rock solid, proven and becoming very versatile these days, I still have to decide few things but I will play with NOSQL schemaless datastore at first, the data will be stored probably in JSONB. I still have to deeply verify if we can pass JSONB to the javascript in browser. </p><ul><li><strong>Redis as auth. token DB store, it will keep the tokens until the ws is handshaked</strong></li></ul><p style="margin-left: 40px;">This is a security extra layer, before establish the websocket handshake, the browser needs to send a token received at the first contact with the server, REDIS DB will keep temporary the tokens until they get used with the handshake.</p><ul><strong></strong><li><strong>in browser HTML rendering of the templates</strong></li></ul><p style="margin-left: 40px;">This is my challenge, it is more than one year I see new javascript framework to process HTML templates browser side, this means they are not generated server side, the server is just a data streaming backend. It should e useful to keep an impressive amount of requests on.</p><ul><li><strong>Bootstrap responsive theme with HTML5 and CSS/JS</strong></li></ul><p style="margin-left: 40px;">I am evaluating several admin panels based on bootstrap, at the moment the more interesting are:</p><p style="margin-left: 40px;">Ultimo admin <a target="_blank" href="http://canvaskite.com/marketplace/ultimo/index.html">link</a></p><p style="margin-left: 40px;">AdminLTE <a target="_blank" href="https://almsaeedstudio.com/themes/AdminLTE/index2.html">link</a></p><p style="margin-left: 40px;">further templates could be added tomorrow.</p><h1><br></h1><h1>WAMP, it's time to play..</h1><p>I wanted to see how the things work, so after installing <a target="_blank" href="http://crossbar.io">crossbar.io</a>, I began to look at the code of the example project and read the <a target="_blank" href="http://crossbar.io/docs/">online documentation</a>, I changed configuration parameters and code to understand better how it works. There is not to much to see about underneath protocol and WAMP because everything is raised to  an higher level of abstraction and it just works but some hacking is still good...</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3653101435623563463.png"></p><p><a target="_blank" href="http://msgpack.org/"></a></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7479741435623890426.png"></p><p>Now my concern is about the security I could build on top of this system so I am interested to the authorization and authentication features provided by crossbar.io</p><p>The current documentation on github explain about the <a target="_blank" href="http://crossbar.io/docs/WAMP-CRA-Authentication/">CRA authentication method</a>, it is interesting, I could write an authenticator in python able to retrieve the secret from a DB to let crossbar.io to perform the authentication BUT some clever guy on github suggest to implement <a target="_blank" href="https://github.com/tavendo/WAMP/issues/135">WAMP-SCRAM</a> instead because more secure.</p><p>I am exactly there...no documentation yet...reading  the source code to understand how to use it...sure will be a long night again :)....see you tomorrow...cheers<br></p>
                                
                            </p>

                            <p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6428251435270770985.png"></p><p>I want to propose you the idea of a separate programmer for the Freescale CPU, I designed the above block following the schematic of Teensy 3.1 at <a target="_blank" href="http://www.pjrc.com/teensy/schematic.html">the pjrc.com website</a>. I divided the Teensy 3.1 in 2 blocks, programmer and CPU.</p><p><strong>Possible benefits:</strong><br></p><ul><li>To reduce the costs in case you want to have multiple IOTsatcom, you just need one programmer for all of them, in this case IOTsatcom will keep just the Freescale CPU onboard.</li></ul><ul><li>Simplification of the IOTsatcom PCB, an easier board to be more reliable and solid dropping the USB interface and programmer on each board.</li></ul><p>Let me know if this idea could be interesting for you and you think this is a necessary feature for the project.</p><p>Thank you.</p>
