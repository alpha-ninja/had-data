                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">This is a small project I'm realizing with the help of my son. We want to have a droid and BB-8 was the my son's choice. <br />As there are still not too many details about the "real" Star Wars droid, we are designing and building our own version.<br />With an holonomic omniwheel driving system and a magnetic anchoring device for the head, it will be very close to the original.</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>The droid is quite known. So no need to add details.</p><p>Our droid will be 3D printed in ABS using orange, white and grey filament.</p><p>We started by designing and printing the body. </p><p>The second step will be designing and building the inner moving structure and creating the software.</p><p>The last step will add the head.</p><p>Hopefully we will be ready for the movie launch in December 2015.</p><p>The components list will be updated whenever we will add something new to the droid<br></p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>

                            <p><p>I finally received the 25x10 mm magnets from China. They are stronger than I thought.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/9634021439274188493.JPG"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/889291439274269671.JPG"></p><p>The distance between the magnets is 20 mm. From the video below you can see that they hold quite strong.</p><p>In my design the body has a total shell thickness of 12 mm, maybe I can add a couple of mm to get extra stiffness and strength for the sphere.<br></p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/fhVdTsdWTQE" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" width="500"></iframe><p>BTW... the plastic parts are a prototype for the R2D2 rocket boosters, but that&apos;s another story... :)</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1043501439274775842.JPG"></p>

                            <h2><a href="/project/6602/log/20764-structure-strength">Structure strength</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a month ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6602/log/20764#discussion-list">
                                0 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>I have been thinking about the spherical structure strength.</p><p>The original idea was to screw the orange rings to the grey triangles blocking the white circles between them. Moreover the orange rings were also designed to be screwed to the white circles.</p><p>Now that I have the parts printed I'm not so sure the orange rings will be strong enough.</p><p>I'll go back to the design board and modify the inner parts to provide the sphere structure and reduce the orange ring to simple covering without structural functions.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1321981436513126778.JPG"></p>
