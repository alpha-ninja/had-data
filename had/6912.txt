                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">The HDrive is an easy to use, all in one servo motor. It can be used in all applications where you need a high performance servo drive.<br /><br />There are no device drivers, the motor can be configured and controlled by the build-in webserver. Just tell the motor what final position, speed or torque should be reached, the internal motion controller will do the rest for you. <br /><br />Specs:<br />- Closed loop stepper motor (brush less) with field oriented control<br />- Nominal torque: 0.2 Nm<br />- Nominal Speed: 1500 RPM<br />- Position Accuracy: +/- 0.2 degree<br />- Communication Frequency: 0.5 KHz<br />- Communication Interfaces: Ethernet and CAN<br />- Current control frequency 50 KHz, Position/Speed Control 5 KHz<br /><br />inexperienced or professionals, the HDrive brings a simple start or a deeply configurable control system to run all your applications smoothly and reliable. High dynamics super fast communication and clean design will make this motor to your best friend.</div>

                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p><strong>OUR TARGET IS TO MAKE DRIVETRAIN TECHNOLOGY ACCESSIBLE TO EVERYONE</strong><br></p><p><em>To bring motion into an application a lot knowledge and time was mandatory.</em></p><p>Our smart Servomotors makes it possible to clearly distinguish between the drive and the application. Thus you can fully concentrate in the logic - the HDrive will take over the drivetrain technology for you.</p><p>our main benefits are:</p><ul><li>Easy and fast implementation, no expert knowledge mandatory</li><li>Directly driven by Ethernet, no cable adapters nor software drivers are mandatory</li><li>Motor can be controlled with only three lines of Matlab code</li><li>Very high communication frequency up to 500 Hz. This means you can send a new target position or  read out the digital I/O&apos;s every two milliseconds</li><li>Slim design with multiple mounting possibilities</li></ul><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/779551438425009703.jpg"></p><p><strong>Communication</strong></p><p>To set a new target position you  have to send an XML formatted ticket over an TCP Socket connection:</p><p>&quot;&lt;system position=&#x201D;500&#x2033; speed=&#x201D;100&#x2033; torque=&#x201D;50&#x2033;&gt;&quot;  This will command the motor to go to the position 50.0&#xB0; with a speed of 100 RPM and a max torque of 0.5Nm. </p><p>This command can be send with only a few lines of code in most programming languages. The most simple example would be to open a telnet connection and to send the commands in it. </p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/BoEdE9bjhZc" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p><strong>I</strong><strong>ndustrialisation</strong></p><p>We started the development four years ago. Since then we have build lots of prototypes and have tested many different motor and amplifier technologies. Now we have chosen to use a stepper motor with a field oriented control. This kind of control combined with an accurate position measurement is turning the stepper in a high end servo drive. Thus the motor is behaving like a normal DC motor - minimized torque ripple and no jerks as seen in conventional steppers do appear.</p><p><strong>Company funded</strong></p><p>Recently we have funded the company henschel-robotics. Please take a look at our website: <a href="http://www.henschel-robotics.ch" target="_blank">www.henschel-robotics.ch</a></p>
