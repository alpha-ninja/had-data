                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">3D printers use as raw material plastic filament of about 360 m long, coiled. With a coil, we can make some items. But when there remain only a few meters, it is often impossible to use it. This filament is then unusable or discarded. Considering the price, it's infuriating.<br />It also happens that the filament breaks during printing or on the coil. <br />The current size of the filament coils do not allow to print large parts. The best solution would be to link together the filaments of each coil. Thus, the size of the printable parts would be no more limited.<br /><br />From these findings, I have sought to develop a plastic filament welder to:<br />•Print large parts.<br />•Reduce filament waste.<br />•Combine different colors and / or filament materials.<br />•Making life easier for 3D printers.<br />•Saving money.<br /><br />It is called EasyWelder.<br /><br />It is now on Kickstater at : <a href='https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/i3d-inno/easywelder-filament-welding-tool-for-3d-printing' target="_blank">https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/i3d-inno/easywelder-filament-welding-tool-for-3d-printing</a></div>

                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>It is accompanied by a &quot;Holding Plate&quot; that block filaments in position during the welding phase.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1839341437152527514.jpg"></p><p><strong>Features / Operation:</strong><br></p><p>The gripper is equipped with two heating tips. The heating temperature is controlled electronically and adjustable from 100 &#xB0; C to 200 &#xB0; C. An on / off switch is available on the handle of the tool. A LED indicates the set temperature is reached. The heating tips are coated with a ant-adhesive layer. </p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/9758961431279465717.jpg"></p><p>It can weld plastic filament of 1.7mm or 3mm in diameter in less than a minute. The complete intervention (welding + finishing + control) takes few minutes.<br></p><p>Consumption: 230 VAC - 50 / 60Hz - 25W</p><p>The gripper is equipped with a plug with Europeanor US standards. An AC adapter is required for other grids.</p><p>The Holding plate ensures maintenance in the filament position before, during and after welding, to its total cooling. The filament is in the guide grooves to ensure perfect concentricity. The Holding Plate also verifies that the diameter of the weld zone is consistent with the need.</p><p><strong>The EasyWelder is CE certification.</strong></p><p>Our <a href="http://www.i3dinno.com/easywelder-en-p186486.html" target="_blank">website </a>presents in video how to use it.</p><p><strong>The kit :</strong></p><p>EasyWelder + Holding plate + paper</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4877431437154330356.png"></p><p><strong>Maintenance of EasyWelder:</strong><br></p><p>Regular cleaning of the heating part with a soft cloth is sufficient.</p><p><strong>Quality and types of welds:</strong><br></p><p>The EasyWelder can weld the following plastic filaments:</p><ul><li>PLA</li><li>PVA</li><li>ABS</li><li>HIPS</li><li>NinjaFlex and other similar filaments</li><li>PEG</li></ul><p>For other materials, tests are to be performed.</p><p>Each weld requests light work of deburring and diameter control with the Holding Plate.</p><p>O-ring or tori are achievable with the flexible filament like NinjaFlex or rigid filament.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/67631431279575878.jpg"></p><p>O-ring in Ninjaflex filament.</p><iframe width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/Z6-XwgFDcH0" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen="" style="width: 500px; height: 281px;"></iframe><p>Resistance tests to bending and tensile strength were used to verify the quality of the welds. These tests indicate that the weld will resist in a 3D printing.<br></p><p><strong>However, welded filament will never be as strong as a seamless filament.</strong></p><p><strong><br></strong></p><p><strong>It&apos;s possible to weld PE strip and braid.</strong></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;"><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3009381437154521257.png"></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">                                                                             PE braid</p><p><strong><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/9045591437154560457.png"></strong></p><p><strong>                                                                          Plastic strip</strong></p><p><strong><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/9566881437154594862.jpg"></strong></p><p><strong>                                                                          Together</strong></p><p><strong><br></strong></p><p><strong><br></strong></p><p>Here is an example of printed parts with welded PLA filament.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/386251437154404147.png"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/2159041431279777851.jpg"></p>
