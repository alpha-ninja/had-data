                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">A few situations came up in the last few months that all required my presence and were an hour away.<br /><br />1) My mother required help with her Social Security paperwork online.<br />2) My mother required help connecting her laptop to her wireless printer<br />3) My mother-in-law required help putting together an online grocery order for HER parents<br />4) My mother-in-law's parents required help with online services (misc)<br /><br />I was initially looking into VNC but I'm wary of putting software on their normal PC's.  This also assumes that they're always going to have (or even want) a PC, and that they're comfortable at their normal PC.<br /><br />If, however, I were to build a small Raspberry Pi system and hook it up to their TV, the options open up.<br /><br />The aging party can watch what we do, we can control it from our home PC, and they can interact (or not) with a bluetooth keyboard/mouse.</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>The big thing I want is for me to 'drive' and them to watch and then give me verbal feedback (or type in their password), without me having to drive an hour. :)<br></p><p>Some areas I need to investigate:<br></p><p>I need to look into security concerns with an open hole in the firewall for VNC, and the the security of VNC itself.</p><p>I'm excited about the idea that you can turn the HDMI on and off from the PI, which should make it such that I can trigger their TV to turn on and switch to the PI.  I need to look into whether or not I can change it back.  Mabye an IR transmitter or CEC.</p><p>I should also look into gathering feedback from the aging party through their remote using CEC.  It may mitigate the need for a mouse or keyboard.</p><p>I need to look into camera as well.</p><p>Invariably, they're going to point at the screen.  I have no idea how to deal with this yet...<br></p>
                                
                            </p>
