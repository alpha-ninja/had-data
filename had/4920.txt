                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Project to be deployed in the 3rd world or developing nation.<br />To provide power (from wind turbine, delivered down tether), weather data (on-board sensors) and connectivity (on-board base station).<br /><br />The balloon is to be constructed from user-serviceable materials. Inflation using Hydrogen (GASP.. Hindenburg! don't worry, I'll cover this in a subsequent post), which can be generated using electrolysis (water + power) and delivered up the tether. Tether allows for retraction during adverse weather.<br /><br />Power from a wind-turbine mounted on the balloon, delivered down the tether<br /><br />Weather data from an on-board sensor suite (we've all played with arduino and the humidity/temperature sensors). I would like to include a storm-scope, but that would be another project.<br /><br />Connectivity in the form of a GSM nano-base station or WiFi base-station. To assist in tele-medicine.<br /></div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>Underlying principles:</p><p>User Serviceable - As much as absolutely possible should be repairable by an end-user with basic craft skills and basic training. This means that as many components as possible should be considered easily sourced and low-cost, world-wide.</p><p>Deployable to 3rd world or developing nation - No assumed infrastructure. Ideally as a stand-alone device that does not require any existing grid or resources (apart from atmosphere, a little water and something to tie it down to).</p><p>Tethered - Something to strong enough to stop it blowing away, light enough not to drag it down. Conductors to transfer energy down and narrow tube or cavity (can be quite narrow) to bleed hydrogen up.</p><p>Low Cost - Ideally low cost (sub $100-200) with a rapid return on investment and minimal ongoing costs for maintenance.</p>
                                
                            </p>
