                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Clouden is a smart watering solution that constantly monitors your local weather and soil conditions, and optimizes watering schedules to improve plant health and optimize water usage. Whether you&#x2019;re a novice gardener with a few plants in your backyard, or managing a large-scale farm, Clouden will eliminate the guesswork from watering schedules.<br /><br />Inserted in the soil, the Clouden Sensor Box gathers data about weather changes and soil conditions. Using our Clouden App or the Clouden Control System, the data are analyzed and suggestions for improvement are made (through alerts via push notifications, text, or email) to maximize plant health. The Clouden Control System can directly regulate your existing watering system, watering your plants only when needed. <br /><br />With advanced, real-time monitoring technology, and an intuitive control system the Clouden smart watering solution keeps you connected to your garden or farm so you can optimize water usage and grow healthier plants.</div>

                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><h2><strong>INTRODUCTION</strong></h2><p>Currently, most automated irrigation systems operate in an open-loop fashion, controlling watering schedules based on timers that are manually &#x2018;programmed&#x2019; to account for the different calendar seasons&#x2013;a technology developed in the 60s.  Because of the overwhelming amount of evidence showcasing the severeness of the drought in California, the state where more than a third of the US&#x2019; vegetables and two-thirds of its fruits and nuts are produced, there&#x2019;s been a concerted effort to make these systems smarter.</p><p>Over the past few years, a new breed of automated irrigation systems have been developed to include internet connectivity.  The access to the worldwide web allows the controllers to query online APIs (e.g,.<a href="http://www.wunderground.com/weather/api"> </a><a href="http://www.wunderground.com/weather/api">http://www.wunderground.com/weather/api</a>) to determine the probability of rain at its current location.  It also allows users to set or override watering schedules remotely from any connected device.  Although an improvement over the non-connected systems, this solution lacks physical measurements to corroborate the online data.</p><p>In this project, we describe the construction of a data-driven irrigation system comprised by two sub-systems.  One, the <strong>Clouden Box</strong>, is a web-capable irrigation controller where the watering schedules are set and the valves are electronically opened or closed using custom electronic circuitry.  The second, the <strong>Clouden Node</strong>, is a solar-powered, weatherproof array of sensors that measure weather and soil health conditions of the zones controlled by the <strong>Clouden Box</strong>.  These sensor arrays communicate in real-time different environmental parameters to the controller wirelessly using Bluetooth Low Energy.</p><p>The sensor feedback from one or more <strong>Clouden Node</strong> in conjunction with information from online weather APIs are processed and analyzed by the <strong>Clouden Box </strong>controller.  Once a few data points are collected, our machine learning algorithms on the controller will determine the best watering schedules and inform the user about the water usage efficiency of the system on a zone-by-zone basis.</p><p>From novice gardeners with a few plants in their backyards to large-scale farm managers, users of the <strong>Clouden</strong> Irrigation Control System can eliminate the guesswork from watering schedules.</p><h2><strong>MATERIALS AND METHODS</strong></h2><p><strong>Clouden</strong> is a sensor-driven, Wi-Fi-enabled, self-learning, programmable irrigation control system that allows users to optimize water usage, save money and boost the health of their lawns and crops.  The system is comprised by two parts, one that measures several parameters directly from the soil, and another that schedules and drives the irrigation based on the sensor data.  The sensor data can be complimented with online weather information, which can be queried when a wireless or wired internet connection is available.<br></p><p>[More coming soon...]</p>
