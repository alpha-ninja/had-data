                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Must have thing, if you&apos;re a distinct flashlight fan, as well as you&apos;re going to film rapid in dark areas.<br /><br />If you think you&apos;ve spot something near grade, let me look at it closely.<br /><br />Idea came to us when a friend was filming a parrot at 300 fps 180 deg. shutter at his home with  a red epic dragon left mid shift with all guts lying around.<br /><br />Or even earlier, when i decided to make an ultimate filming and working light, that&apos;s so handy while crafting 0.3 pitch stuff for one of projects i felt like spotting for needs, described here on hackaday.</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>Now I&apos;ve come to a point where overall design has been crafted,</p><p>CC boost troubleshooting, burnout stress tests and some hacks are done.</p><p>Mainframe contains 12 rows of 9 leds each, half-cold &#x2013; half-warm.</p><p>Connected in parrallels each circuit tends to have</p><p>around 32V overall voltage drop across each of two</p><p>loops of passively cooled, connected in parallel 9 piece &#x2013; in-series, 3 watt LED stripes driven by</p><p>two independent dimmable boost CC supplies, thy drain out of</p><p>HUGE 12V DC or ACtoDC or lead acid damn heavy source.</p><p>all connected with dead hot wires not to touch without eyeprotection.</p><p>Stay tuned.</p><p>Prepare,</p><p>pray.</p><p>Wach flies dance!</p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>
