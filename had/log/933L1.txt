                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-1926"><p name="df49"><img src="//cdn.hackaday.io/images/4035541398659713843.JPG"></p><p name="e982">Picked up the headset, it's choice was in that it had the most features and was on clearence. I always love it when they offer the warranty though. This guy was particularly insistent on the warranty, and that it would cover anything. When I informed him that I'd be taking it apart, and would probably fry the first one, his reply was a stoic "It might not cover that." lol</p><p name="837e">So the first day we were able to get the headset apart, analyze the buttons and connections.</p><p name="58b0">After taking several measurements and calculating the height with battery, we decided(Much hagling, lol) on a base layout and plan of attack.</p><p name="8cbf">This headsets case actually lends itself to a hack. I'm not sure how to post pics in this post, but we have pics of the disassembly, and template making process.</p><p name="4914">We've also toyed with the idea of doing a "Diresta" esque video and posting it to one of our youtube accounts showing a build. We do have to build 3 of them in total, one for each of us.</p><p name="4914">Edit* Figured it out, duh, late</p><p name="313a"><img src="//cdn.hackaday.io/images/938171398659781318.JPG"><img src="//cdn.hackaday.io/images/4706921398659798809.JPG"><img src="//cdn.hackaday.io/images/6046531398659829036.JPG">We'll post more pics in the how to build<br></p></div>

                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-1928"><p name="425f">We got the base of the unit. We don't have a 3d printer, so it was sculpy to the rescue. Designing the throat for the speaker cavity was a bit of a challenge. Namely James wanted to add an amplifier to the project, but given that we started with literally a week until the deadline. We ultimately decided against.&nbsp;</p><p name="7eaa"><img src="//cdn.hackaday.io/images/5219681398658907547.JPG"></p><p name="e0b6"><img src="//cdn.hackaday.io/images/1126201398659493766.JPG"></p><p name="c954">Have'nt tested the board since we made the base unit. I un-wittinly baked it with the sculpy.(Dangers of watching a young child and trying to complete projects) But that's ok, cuz radio shack had just one more unit, and yes it was still on clearence. ;) Told ya the first one always gets fried for some reason. </p><p name="3f8e">Here it is with the board, speaker and switch adapter in.<br></p><p name="eae4"><img src="//cdn.hackaday.io/images/1821131398659582897.JPG"></p><p name="0bf9"><img src="//cdn.hackaday.io/images/9155431398659609848.JPG"></p></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/933/log/1932-switcheswe-dont-need-no-stinkin-switches">Switches...We don&#39;t need no stinkin&#39; switches</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a year ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/933/log/1932#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-1932"><p name="a7b6">Well actually we do...However, given the height already 5 mm in just the base and sound channel. Another 5mm micro switch, to make it look asthetic would be imposible without increasing the size. Defeats the coolness if you've got a big ol cd case riding on your chest.</p><p name="e54b">After a lil creative recon, we happened upon some micro switch domes that were salvaged from some old cell phones. </p><p name="381f"><img src="//cdn.hackaday.io/images/4883911398663712199.JPG"></p><p name="be1c">Simple, long life, Up-cycle = hackerific! :)&nbsp; They're the lil black dots on the tape.<br></p><p name="3354">Did'nt really have time or extra money to do a custom pcb, so we opted for the always elegant Radio Shack Proto-Board. That and a dremel, you could almost be McGyver. lol.</p><p name="6950">So anyways...The initial testing was a bust. The micro switches worked great if you pressed them with your finger, and terible with the flat buttons we had crafted to press them down.&nbsp;</p><p name="9b39"><img src="//cdn.hackaday.io/images/3352571398663857759.JPG"></p><p name="feb6">One of us finally had the idea of making the buttons directly on the protoboard. That way we could force the putty through the wholes, and we'd have a bunch of pegs to press on the buttons. As it turns out this worked, and worked great. We were able to cut the number of disk needed down to 2 for each button, putting an extra one on the delta shield to be on the safe side.</p><p name="d574"><img src="//cdn.hackaday.io/images/436751398663913825.JPG"></p><p name="e6ed"><img src="//cdn.hackaday.io/images/8529081398663957820.JPG"></p><p name="e03e">Height is still a lil bit of a concern, but I think it'll be fine once we get it wrapped with the aluminum brace.</p><p name="92d0"><img src="//cdn.hackaday.io/images/4220571398664027400.JPG"></p><p name="d1bf"><img src="//cdn.hackaday.io/images/3792141398664063803.JPG"></p></div>
                            </div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/933/log/2159-polymer-clay-is-a-good-idea-but">Polymer clay is a good idea but...</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a year ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/933/log/2159#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-2159"><p name="67a0">It's not so good in practice. I wish we had a 3d printer. BTW shout out to Tampa Bay on their communicator. Way to go guys!</p><p name="efe9">Our current problem is that we keep breaking the polymer clay components of the build. It'll work great, you start fiddling with it and it breaks. Currently the game plan is to re-inforce the clay with drywall weave, and the aluminum sheeting.</p><p name="7547">As a result our design has changed almost completely. We are borrowing more from my personal development that I was going to release, but that'll be released after this.&nbsp;</p><p name="1d6a">We have a total of 3 major parts now. The base that attaches to the article of clothing. In this base we have included the battery, speaker and amplifier circuit, which to be fair was ripped out of a walgreens hearing aide. Second we have the logic stage which sits on top and connects to the base. This stage has the BT headset board, microphone and activation buttons.&nbsp;</p><p name="05e7">...</p><p name="5994">Unfortunately, getting pulled away. I'll post the new design, and pics later. I think this disqualifies us from the contest though. </p></div>
