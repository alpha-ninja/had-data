                            <h2><a href="/project/5066/log/20004-novel-charging-concept">Novel Charging concept</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">2 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5066/log/20004#discussion-list">
                                    27 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-20004"><p>On board chargers are expensive.  They are also slow.  As EV fast charge infrastructure is still crap we are definitely maintaining our 'plug into any socket' capability.  Plugging into any socket should allow people drive to work &amp; back &amp; plug in at both places.  A least at home, getting a 3 phase socket installed will not be a big deal.</p><p>A 3.6kw charger seems to cost about USD1K.  An 8kw charger is more like USD2K.  This is big money for a car supposed to retail for USD22K.  The little 2kw one we have on board now cost about USD500..</p><p>So, we set about thinking.  In fact, we already have 2 x 8KW chargers on the car.  Each of our hub motors is, in effect, an electric generator.  Plug these into the mains, turn the motor controllers to 'regenerate" &amp; hey presto, we have 2 x 'free' 8KW chargers.  It means we could remove the crappy 2kw charger we have now, saving about 25kilos in weight &amp; we can save loads of  money!.  </p><p>If plugged into a normal socket, no matter what charger we have, we will be limited to 16A/230V so about 3.7kw.  With a 20kw bat pack, this means about 5 hours charging.  But, with 3 phase, these limitation do not apply.  I think it gives us 3 x 25a x 230v = so 17kw..  3 phase 'should' allow for us to use 2 x 8kw motors as on board generators.  A 16kw charger would fully charge us in just over an hour. </p><p>This idea is only in the formative stage.  We think this might be another word first.  We do not believe anything like this has been done before.  The solution is probably unique to cars driven by hub motors.  We hope to start testing next week.  We will need to play with the motor controllers a little.  It is unlikely they will support this idea without substantial tampering.  There is every possibility lots of things can get fried as we test the idea.  But, wow, it is such a cool idea what frying some electronics would be more than worthwhile if we could actually make it work.   The charger represents a serious % of the overall cost of the vehicle &amp; if we could remove that cost completely &amp; get close to 1 hour charging it would represent a major breakthrough for LUKA.   Obviously, sharing an early concept like this with no testing means people can rip us apart as we might be totally wrong !.  But, the LUKA project is all about putting it out there with no bells or whistles so lets see what happens..  </p><p>In other developments, we did a lot of work on the future interior of the car.  We  also re-calibrated the BMS.  It is now very conservative.  It is set do it should show 'empty' when we can still dive about 30km. This week, we set a new top speed of 118.5km/h.  On a 130km journey, the BMS showed that we used 60% of the battery (our SOC 40%).  This 130km drive was done with no attempt to be economical &amp; with regeneration not functioning correctly. We will now state with confidence that the 'real world' range of the LUKA EV (using its smaller battery pack) is 250km.  We are confident our target range of 300km will be reached with the bigger pack (40 cells x 3.2V, 200ah). this bigger pack is just one extra set of 4 cells in the back &amp; in the front. </p><p>we hope to do work on suspension next week &amp; possibly do a video tour of the car  next Friday.<br></p></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/5066/log/22495-august-2015">August 2015</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">8 days ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5066/log/22495#discussion-list">
                                    6 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-22495"><p>This project log is just to say we are still working on the project &amp; recap where we are &amp; what we have to do next...   Our main focus is now on LUKA MK II. </p><p>LUKA 1 has very much done what it was supposed to do.  It gave us proof of concept.  The original project aims were a top speed of 130km/h.  Luka has a top speed of 134km/h with just 2 motors.</p><p>The second aim was for the wight to be under 750kg.  Luka is 721kg.  Even with 4 motors, LUKA II will be well under 750kg because we have made various changes that save weight in Version II.</p><p>EU certification by Sept 2015.  We got certified months ago so that box is ticked too..</p><p>Range of 300km on a single charge.  This Luka definitely goes 300km on a single charge.  But, range is like a piece of string.  There are a multitude of variables.  Huge compromises need to be made to make the car go 300km.  Driving normally with a mix of highways &amp; usual traffic in towns or cities, I think a realistic range is 250km with the 19.2kwh bat pack.  With range, we also need to look at price.  In physical terms, we can fit a bat pack of up to 60kwh in the car &amp; that would obviously have a very long range.  But, as a ballpark number, each additional 100km range will add about EUR3K to the price of the car.  </p><p>Retail price.  Under EUR20K.</p><p>There was confusion about this target.  People seemed to think this would be the price for a &apos;kit&apos; that people have to build themselves &amp; get certified themselves.  What we meant was that the price would be for a complete car, certified &amp; ready to drive.  The only caveat was that this price would be ex works our workshop (Czech republic) so it would cost some money to get it to an end customer... Without doubt, we could sell a complete car configured with the 19.2kwh bat pack &amp; 4 hub motors for under EUR20K &amp; make a profit...(if anyone wants one, let me know !)</p><p>So, we can say we met all the project aims.  We did not expect to.  We expected something to be a show stopper.  But, just because we met the aims does not mean we built a good car.  Unfortunately, the car does not pass the &apos;wife&apos; test.  My wife thinks it looks lovely but she will not drive it because it is too &quot;different&apos;.  It has no noise insulation, this makes driving much more &apos;real&apos;.  You hear the suspension, the brakes, the road noise..  Our brakes are &apos;rally car&apos; brakes (apply a lot of force &amp; they work well).  Normal folk are used to something very different.  We do not need power steering because the car is so light but normal people are not used to this type of steering.  The car does how have a proper interior...  Even &apos;one pedal driving&apos; is weird to people.  Because regen braking starts once you take your foot off the accelerator you usually do not need a brake..This sort of freaks some people out..  &amp;, even for me, it is just too hot to drive any car what does not have proper air conditioning... Here in Czech (not an especially hot country), we hit 40 degrees last week.  This week is high 30s.  A car without proper air con is useless... </p><p>We also learned that open sourcing a project is hard.  The key components for Luka II are not the same as those for Luka I &amp; they are not the same as our original 3D drawings.  All the millimeter changes have been done &apos;as we build&apos; the new chassis &amp; as we made small changes to the body.  So, we have a situation where our tooling will not match our 3D plans. This is far from ideal if our hope is that others can just download things &amp; make their own version of the car..  It has also become clear that even if people could download all the plans, they would still need a huge amount of (various) skills to have any chance of building a car themselves.  Making the chassis would be easy enough, the body almost impossible.  Even if we had a wonderful wiring diagram, only very experienced folk would have any chance of following it.....&amp; then there are all the other systems like brakes, suspension, steering, programing controllers etc...</p><p>We need to make further technical innovations.  We need to follow through on the novel charging idea &amp; we need to look at cleverly air cooling &apos;everything&apos;. </p><p>But, overall, we need to add one more big project aim.  The new aim is that the car must be nice to drive.!!  People who are not &apos;car people&apos; need to like driving it.  The car has to feel much more &apos;normal&apos; when a person gets in..  It must have full noise insulation &amp; have many more creature comforts like air conditioning, electric windows etc. We avoided these things with LUKA 1 because we wanted to save electricity but give me 230km of air conditioned driving any day over 250km of non air-conditioned driving..</p><p>The plan now is to make Luka II &apos;as soon as possible&apos; .  We had a plan to officially launch the vehicle at e car tech in Munich (October 2015) but this now seems unrealistic.  The vehicle has to be 100% perfect for a show like e car tech &amp; paying now for a fair that happens in October just puts too much pressure on us.  If we were full time on the project we might do it but real life &amp; real jobs tend to get in the way...</p><p>I do not expect to post many more updates in August.  It is not only too hot to drive the car, it is too hot to do anything !.  But, for sure, if anything exciting happens, I will post it.....<br></p></div>
