                            <h2><a href="/project/945/log/2618-velocity-ramping-is-a-go">Velocity ramping is a go</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a year ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/945/log/2618#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-2618"><p>TL;WR: <a href="http://www.github.com/marginallyclever/arm3">http://www.github.com/marginallyclever/arm3</a></p><p>I know the total steps and the maximum velocity I want to reach.&nbsp; I can find how many steps I should accelerate and how many I should decelerate.&nbsp; So I think I spent 2h writing the code to do exactly that and 2h tweaking the acceleration and top speed to minimize overshoot.&nbsp; How good is it?&nbsp; The overshoot is almost completely gone.&nbsp; I'm picking up a bit of vibration from the motors and I wonder now if gear backlash is becoming the dominant issue.</p><p>The GcodeSender app has been improved to create "your message here" gcode for the arm.&nbsp; I'm finding that lines are very straight, but the pen holder is now the weakest link - the grip on the pen is loose and the pen wobbles in the holder.&nbsp; I tried a rubber band to grip more tightly and that created a new set of problems.</p><p>Philisophical question: how do you know the difference between a problem and a symptom?&nbsp; Can't a problem be a symptom of a deeper, more subtle problem?&nbsp; I need a loose grip on the pen because the arm's distance from the table isn't perfect.&nbsp; I also notice that long horizontal lines on paper are bowed.&nbsp; Perhaps I'm getting the machine home position wrong.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; If it were perfect the arm would move in straight lines with constant Z (if desired).&nbsp; that would mean I could put a tighter grip and reduce the play in the system.</p><p>Since I'm at maker faire I think I'll wander over to the TechShop booth.&nbsp; They have a laser cutter.&nbsp; It shouldn't be any trouble for them to cut a better fitting pen holder as an experiment.&nbsp; Someone around here has got to have some glue.</p><p>You'd be surprised how hard it is to get makers to make something at a maker faire.&nbsp; Wierd, huh?</p></div>
