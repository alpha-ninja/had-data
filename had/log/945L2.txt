                            <h2><a href="/project/945/log/3271-wrist-to-base">Wrist to Base!</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a year ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/945/log/3271#discussion-list">
                                    1 comment
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-3271"><p><a href="http://instagram.com/p/o6fQlBofL3/">http://instagram.com/p/o6fQlBofL3/</a></p><p><img src="http://scontent-b.cdninstagram.com/hphotos-xap1/t51.2885-15/10387995_660155264075252_302021597_n.jpg"></p><p>Just in time for the Vancouver Mini Maker Faire I've assembled all the linkages between the base and the wrist.&nbsp; Everything moves smoothly and the three motors on the top should be able to drive the wrist and elbow.&nbsp; I have the electronics already to drive the rest.&nbsp; I don't have time before the show to add the motors and gearing to make it all go.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>I've only had to change the forearm once, to add mounts for the limit switches at the wrist.&nbsp; They serve double duty - making sure the wrist can't turn to far in either direction AND making sure the wrist doesn't hit the shoulder.&nbsp; It's a little hard to see the switch on the back of the shoulder in this photo.&nbsp; when the arm is powered off it rests on the ribs across the shoulder and the elbow motor touches the switch.</p><p>Waving this thing around I can see already that the bicep is going to need some ribs to reduce torsion.&nbsp; Also the whole thing is very top heavy.&nbsp; The base will be ~20cm tall.&nbsp; Inside I'll hide the electronics and the power supply, so you'll only have to plug in and go.</p><p>I'd love to see someone design an automatic tool changer.&nbsp; Right now my only option is to bolt a tool onto the four screws at the wrist.</p><p>A rough measurement says base center to wrist face at full extension is ~53cm.&nbsp; might even go as far as 55.&nbsp; Getting enough power at the shoulder to lift the arm - let alone the 1kg target mass - is going to require some serious gearing.&nbsp; I feel confident that I've got bearing prototypes down now, so that shoulder bearing into a gearbox with something like 200:1 reduction is called for.&nbsp; Hypocycloids, to the rescue!&nbsp; If I can build it parametrically I might use them in the shoulder and base, too.</p><p>In the coming weeks I have a bunch of new products to list on <a href="http://marginallyclever.com">my online robot store</a> and manufacturing is taking up a lot of my time.&nbsp; Whenever I get a break I like to relax by playing my game.&nbsp; Maybe you've heard of it?&nbsp; It's called "robot arm".&nbsp; I like a game that doesn't have cheat codes or walkthroughs.</p><p>If you're enjoying this project, please pimp it to your friends.&nbsp; Let's see if we can get this thing featured, OK?</p></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/945/log/3493-a-little-story-about-gearboxes">A little story about gearboxes</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a year ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/945/log/3493#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-3493"><p>I love sub-projects.&nbsp; Some people start a lot of projects totally divorced from each other.&nbsp; I like projects that are building towards a greater goal.&nbsp; They have added purpose, makes them feel more important, I guess.</p><p>For example, I really enjoyed making the bearings that turned out to work really well.&nbsp; Seems other people did, too.&nbsp; Now I'm wondering if there's opportunity there for my first kickstarter - money would be used to make an injection mold for higher quality mass production and contributors would get bearings at a discount for their projects.</p><p>As the arm is coming together I can see that I'm going to need a gearbox to get enough power from my stepper motors to lift at the shoulder.&nbsp; I've been in love with the idea of hypocycloidal gears for a long-ass time now.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>
<iframe allowfullscreen src="//www.youtube.com/embed/D_uAb-iEb_Q" frameborder="0" height="281" width="500"></iframe></p><p>I'm filming Matthew (a Solidworks master) back in 2012.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; I was originally inspired by this other guy:</p><p>
<iframe allowfullscreen src="//www.youtube.com/embed/Ye8NtIZkixI" frameborder="0" height="281" width="500"></iframe></p><p><br>
<iframe allowfullscreen src="//www.youtube.com/embed/SH46bpe1cNA" frameborder="0" height="281" width="500"></iframe></p><p>Who's website I found here: <a href="http://www.zincland.com/hypocycloid/">http://www.zincland.com/hypocycloid/</a></p><p>This morning I decided to download zincland's python scripts and see if I could generate some of these profiles.&nbsp; Save File &gt; My Documents &gt; Design - oh, there's already a folder for gearboxes?&nbsp; Hey!&nbsp; There's a folder for hypocycloids, too...and the files I'm trying to save are already here!&nbsp; But then what's this HTML file?</p><p><img src="http://oinkpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/My-God-its-full-of-stars1-1024x1024.jpg"></p><p>Turns out in Two thousand and eleven I had started creating a Javascript version of the DXF generator and then <em>completely forgotten it</em>.</p><p>So here it is for you: <a href="https://www.marginallyclever.com/other/hypocycloid-generator.html">https://www.marginallyclever.com/other/hypocycloid-generator.html</a></p><p>No need to install or run anything.&nbsp; Just change your parameters and then save the DXF file contents (in the textarea) to a file of your choice.</p><p>If that's helpful to you, let me know and share with your friends.</p><p>Next update I should have a prototype gearbox.<br><a href="https://www.marginallyclever.com/other/hypocycloid-generator.html"></a></p></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/945/log/4476-ok-i-think-i-might-need-your-help">Ok, I think I might need your help</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a year ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/945/log/4476#discussion-list">
                                    3 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-4476"><p>Some people have trouble with public speaking, some with spiders.&nbsp; I have trouble with asking for help.</p><h2>Where I'm at</h2><p>It seems I've reached a plateau.&nbsp; I don't seem to be smart enough to get over the stuff I'm stuck on, yet.&nbsp; To avoid the issues I've been blogging a lot to teach programming and robotics.&nbsp; The high-school level stuff I'm good at.&nbsp; I just wrote some stuff about <a href="https://www.marginallyclever.com/blog/2014/07/program-a-line-following-robot/">making a line following robot</a> and <a href="http://www.thingiverse.com/thing:384904">released the plans on thingiverse</a>.</p><p>I'm also getting ready for NYMF.&nbsp; Heck, tonight I'm at MakerLabs in beautiful Vancouver, drawing a huge mural for their official opening party.&nbsp; Seems that now most of my day-to-day operations are smooth flowing, so I finally have time for outreach and generally giving back to the world.</p><p>See this? I'm trying to avoid the issue.&nbsp; Even thinking about it is hard.</p><h2>Gearbox</h2><p>If you've been reading the updates, you know that I've been trying to make a hypocycloid gearbox.&nbsp; So far I've got one that works but only sort of.&nbsp; It gets stuck a lot and the output isn't consistently even.&nbsp; I'd really like to make it out of another material.&nbsp; That very quickly reaches the corner of stuff-i-know-little-about and experiences-i-mostly-regret.&nbsp; So I'm looking for some help, maybe a confidence boost, I dunno.</p><h2>Software</h2><p>The <a href="https://github.com/MarginallyClever/arm3">software is working</a> fine in three axies.&nbsp; I've even updated the instructions to make it easier for you to download, setup, and compile the java yourself.&nbsp; There are some big challenges here.</p><p>1. how to turn the finger tip with inverse kinematics?&nbsp; I was going to rotate it around the world XYZ axies, but some people might want something else.</p><p>2. What happens when switching from forward to inverse kinematics?&nbsp; Rotating the tip with FK or IK only is fine.&nbsp; Switching from one to the other after rotating the tip.... I don't know how to do that math yet.</p><p>3. What do I do about singularities?&nbsp; A singularity happens when there's more than one solution.&nbsp; For example, if the finger tip were point straight up AND directly above the shoulder?&nbsp; Well, the elbow could be anywhere and the results would be the same.&nbsp; So where should the IK put the elbow?</p><h2>So</h2><p>I'm going to keep working on this.&nbsp; Eventually I'll figure it out.&nbsp; I'd rather learn how others solved similar problems, it's a more efficient solution.</p><p>If you have ideas, time, or resources that might help, I would really appreciate it.</p></div>

                            <small class="authorship">a year ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/945/log/4872#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-4872"><p>....aaaand now the internet it horrified of me.&nbsp; Ah well, I guess I'll just have to be a super villain.</p><p>The meaning of the expression is that there's more than one way to solve a problem.&nbsp; I've been very stuck on the softare because I've been trying to drive the robot using a mouse and keyboard.&nbsp; I've come up with a new idea that should work better: I can use 6 potentiometers, an UNO, my 3D printer, and some metal bearing balls to make a scale model of a robot arm that connects with USB to the computer.&nbsp; The computer would copy the real model's movements to a computer model that could be recorded and played back.&nbsp; Best of all, I've done all these kinds of things before. There's no mystery parts to figure out!</p><p>Now I feel like I know what to bring to the New York Maker Faire.&nbsp; Will I see you there?</p></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/945/log/5264-theres-more-than-one-way-to-skin-a-cat">There&#39;s more than one way to skin a cat ||</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a year ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/945/log/5264#discussion-list">
                                    1 comment
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-5264"><p>Teaching a robot arm what to do from a keyboard with inverse kinematics is friggin' hard.&nbsp; The IK has lots of singularities - special cases where things suddenly go stupid.&nbsp; To avoid this I'm trying to make a 6DOF joystick.&nbsp;</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/5314461405905860436.JPG"></p><p>It works just like the robot arm but it has no motors.&nbsp; When I move it around the arduino in the base will pick up the angle values, copy that to the simulation on the screen, and even send it to the robot arm.</p><p>I've already made the sensor blocks.&nbsp; See <a href="http://instagram.com/p/qsKWdpofLu/?modal=true">picture 1</a> and <a href="http://instagram.com/p/qsKWdpofLu/?modal=true">picture 2</a> on instagram.&nbsp; There's a sensor block under the base, next to the arduino.&nbsp; I need some 5mm female headers to plug in and someone to help me make the base, the L brackets, and the other mechanical parts.&nbsp; Anyone want collaborate with me?</p><p>In the mean time I'm printing another 5 blocks.</p><p>I hope this joystick will have other uses, too.&nbsp; Maybe Kerbal Space Program fans will be able to pull of orbital maneuvers more easily.</p></div>
