                            <h2><a href="/project/6491/log/21839-random-ramblings">Random Ramblings</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">20 days ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6491/log/21839#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-21839"><p>This entry is dedicated to explaining the logic behind why this mixer as a concept has the ability to severely financially damage the tobacco industry and why that will aid in the legalization of cannabis.</p>
<p>Picture TOR.  The system works because there are others using it.  You can&#x2019;t be anonymous alone. </p>
<p>E-Juice is a physical router.  Every bottle of nicotine ejuice is serving as a proxy to those using cannabis infused ejuice.  Through our postal system and in everyday situations.  </p>
<p>Even in some states that have decriminalized cannabis, oils and edibles are still considered a felony.  Ironically edibles and oil are what those using for medical purposes most need.</p>
<p>There needs to be a way to organize an underground railroad of access to cannabis hidden in ejuice (tinctures for edible effect and oil infused for a vaporized effect) to patients in need that don&#x2019;t happen to be fortunate enough to live in a non-ass-backwards state.</p>
<p>Location should not limit access.  One country united.  Not one country where a child with epilepsy will be allowed to die in one state where a state over they would have access to lifesaving medication.</p>
<p>This puts the gov. in an interesting position.  They will want to straight out ban ecigs and fear monger with poorly conducted studies just like how they said weed kills brain cells after gas masking a monkey for several minutes straight.</p><p>Banning ejuice &amp; ecigs would cause a generation addicted to them to make the switch to traditional cigarettes.</p>
<p>A moral dilemma.  Protect big tobacco and the tax money it generates and damn another generation to be plagued with smoking related diseases or deal with it and find a way to supplement the lost income.  I suggest cannabis.</p><br>Also with 3d printing ecigs and the common uses of the ingredients ejuice consists of will make it impossible to ban.  VG and PG are common ingredients that are used in a wide range of applications.  The flavor extracts existed before ecigs, they are for flavoring candy.  It&#x2019;ll be another case of the waterpipe (candy flavoring) VS the bong (using the flavoring for ejuice) if they choose to use the ban-hammer.</div>

I figured I would post it here since its a 3d print of my actual brain and I have discussed how I had a TBI.</p><p>Based of 2d fMRI slices from a recent check up, reconstructed into a 3d model then edited to be suitable for printing.
