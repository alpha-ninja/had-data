                                <div id="post-body-20654"><p>Below is a rough 3D design outlining most of the parts</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/2978001436374389687.png"></p><p>On top of this will be a magnetic assembly (to produce electricity).  Heat will be added to the the green bit at the bottom (Hot cap)...For test reasons, the heat source can be anything, we will use a candle or any flame for tests.  But, longer term, we will use solar power.  We have a really small dish we no longer use.  We can cover this in tin foil for test purposes...</p><p>In the next project log, I will attempt to explain how this thing is supposed to work !..  It is fairly simple with a hot cap, displacer, planer spring, O ring, magnet assy on top...&amp; a few other bits &amp; pieces!.  It will not be exciting to watch it because it will have no moving parts that are visible to the naked eye.  I expect the displacement to be about 1 mm..  So, we will connect it to something like a LED array so results are visible....<br></p></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/6088/log/20742-how-the-thing-is-supposed-to-work">How the thing is supposed to work</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a month ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6088/log/20742#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-20742"><p>Somewhat dreading this but here we go.</p><p>The Stirling cycle is well described all over the net so lets not go there.  I believe our device is best described as working on a free piston Stirling cycle.   In our engine, the piston is replaced by a diaphragm.  In addition to a free piston, the engine also has a free displacer.  The diaphragm<span class="redactor-invisible-space"> &amp; the displacer will work out of phase (by 90 degrees).</span></p><p>OUTER CAN / HOT CAP<br><span class="redactor-invisible-space"></span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space">Heat from any source is applied to the bottom end of the  "hot cap" or outer can (green in diagram). The bottom of this is the hot end of the device.  The top is the cold end.  The outer can serves to heat &amp; cool the gas &amp; keep the gas in place. </span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space">We have choice with gas.  The choices are as follows.</span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space">A.  Normal air as normal or high pressure.   </span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space">B.  Helium at normal or high pressure.</span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space">C.  Hydrogen at normal or high pressure.</span></p><p>We are likely to start with air at normal pressure then migrate to helium at normal pressure &amp; then helium at high pressure.  The choice of gas &amp; the pressure of gas will only effect efficiency, it will not interfere with the basic workings of the device.  </p><p>INNER CAN / DISPLACER (shown in Blue)<br></p><p>Inside the outer can is an inner can.  The inner can is the DISPLACER  This hollow can is the same as the outer can, just 1 mm smaller.  Enough to allow for free movement of the inner can. It should be a snug fit but have the ability to move freely. The bottoms of both cans should be slightly rounded as flat surfaces are prone to deformation.   The displacer moves back &amp; forth moving the gas from the hot end to the cold end.</p><p>The inner surface of the outer can and the outer surface of the displacer also act as a regenerator. This is important for efficiency but less important for understanding.</p><p>All the gas would stay at the same temperature if the displacer was static. (Well, if it were an ideal gas with zero viscosity &amp; ours is not).   The temperature variance happens because the displacer is moving.</p><p>PLANAR SPRING (Shown is Yellow)</p><p>The displacer is mounted underneath the planar spring.  The spring keeps the displacer in the correct position &amp; allows the displacer to move to its own rhythm</p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space"></span> DIAPHRAGM. (Shown in red)</p><p>Made from Beryllium copper, the <span class="redactor-invisible-space">diaphragm moves back &amp; forth (out of phase with the displacer) depending on the pressure in the outer can.</span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space">Flanges. (2 fairly fat yellow bits, one at the top of the drawing, other around the hot cap)</span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space">These are bolted tightly together holding everything in place.  The entire assembly is put on springs to allow the device &amp; displacer to both move increasing overall efficiency &amp; allowing for a natural rhythm.</span></p><p><br><span class="redactor-invisible-space"></span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space">NOT SHOWN.</span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space">There are many more parts but they are not in the 3D representation. Key part are the upper &amp; lower caps enclosing the <span class="redactor-invisible-space">diaphragm &amp; planar spring, the bulkhead displacer, casing springs, various Gaskets, clamping rings &amp; then entire magnet array that goes on top of the device.  These parts will be added later.</span></span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space"><br><span class="redactor-invisible-space">SOME SCIENCE..</span></span></p><p>The total pressure/volume gas is changing as its average temperature is changing (pV=nRT for ideal gas, where p is pressure, V is volume, n is the number of moles of the gas, R is a constant, and T is absolute temperature in kelvins). V is almost constant, it changes a bit because of the membrane deflection; the p is what pushes on it. n is the amount of gas in the can, that's constant. R is also a constant, so we can count nR as a constant specific for the given assembly. T is the average temperature of the gas in the can (the active volume, displacer does not count).  As the displacer is itself serving as a re-generator, it stores the heat of the gas along its length. So a material with low thermal conduction is used . In other words, gas flowing from hot to cold end is heating the displacer's left end (assuming hot end is left) and on its way back cools the right end.)</p><p>PROJECT LOG CONCLUSION.</p><p>If the above does not make sense, do not worry, it is me, not you.  The diagram is incomplete &amp; the explanation is poor.  The next log will show representations of the missing bits &amp; hopefully an overall explanation.  I suspect the only way to demonstrate this effectively is with pics of actual parts &amp; a video of the device being assembled.  Both will follow someday....&amp; this log will probably be heavily edited once the thought process is more clear....<br></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space"><span class="redactor-invisible-space"></span></span></p>

                            <h2><a href="/project/6088/log/22596-august-12th-2015">August 12th 2015</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">7 days ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6088/log/22596#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-22596"><p>The project to date is very poorly described.  He have been working to fabricate the parts.  For such an apparently simple device, fabrication has been very difficult.  Parts can be seen in the video below.</p><p><iframe src="//www.youtube.com/embed/a5Lala5QbFA" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" width="500"></iframe>There is a strong metal ring. You can see that this has 3 springs attached.  These 3 springs sit on a frame.  The frame is visible at about the 30 second point in the video.  At about the 4 second point, you can see we also have a regulator attached to the big structural ring.</p><p>You can see the hot cap &amp; this is screwed to the ring with 12 screws.  </p><p>On the reverse side, there are 24 screws holding the beryllium copper membrane in place (sourcing Beryllium copper is hard, we found some at a very specialist UK online shop).  Forming the Beryllium copper to the correct shape  is even harder but that is for a different project log.</p><p>At about 35 secs, we have removed the 12 screws so we can take away the hot cap (outer Can).  We can now see the displacer (inner can).  The outer can has a metal ring &amp; a seal to keep it in place &amp; to keep the device air tight. </p><p>At 52 secs, when we remove the displacer, you can see the planar spring.  This is screwed to the top of the displacer by 3 screws. For the device to function, the planar spring must be completely straight.  Our spring is not straight, hence the device does not work.  Forming a planar spring is far from easy.  Using any normal methods, the spring will warp.  We now know that the only realistic way to make a straight spring is to use a water cutter.  Thankfully, we know someone who has one of these so a new spring is on the way.</p><p>When heat is added to the hot cap, the displacer acts on the planar spring.  The spring acts on the &apos;chamber&quot;.  You can see slight movement on the chamber at about 1 min 30 secs (before we remove it as at this time we have already removed the 24 screws from the other side.).  Movement of the sealed chamber is critical !. </p><p>At about 2 mins, you see inside the displacer.  The component inside ensures that the displacer does not lose its shape when heat is applied to the outer can..  </p><p>At 3 minutes, we are trying to show the &apos;free movement&apos; of the displacer inside the outer can.  Obviously, we do not have free movement.  Without free movement, the device will not work...  The gap between the displacer &amp; outer Can is supposed to be as small as possible (like 1mm) but still allow free movement.  We are going to rework the inner &amp; outer cans to get the required free movement..  Honestly, even making the inner &amp; outer Cans to this degree of accuracy is challenging.  The gap has to be uniform.  We do not know any easy way to make these things.  We just got some metal &amp; bent is as best we could.  To add to the complexity, the bottom of each Can needs to be slightly curved as the device will not work with a flat bottom..  The curves on both have to be the same..  It is just a matter of fiddling around with the metal, cutting it accurately &amp; welding it.  But expect to have a good few goes before you get it even half right.  </p><p>At about 5 minutes, we remove the sealed chamber containing the beryllium copper membrane.  You can also see a rubber gasket. At 5.40 the basic idea is that the planar spring should touch the sealed chamber.  This does not happen for us because the spring is warped.  The sealed chamber has divots for the screw tops as the spring, not the screw tops should act on the chamber..</p><p>The sealed chamber holding the inner part of the membrane is sealed using a single central screw.  Both sides of the sealed chamber have rubber gaskets (it is hard to see these in this video)..</p><p>What is visible in this video is only half the device.  On top of this, we need to place a magnet &amp; coil contraption.  The magnet &amp; coil will turn the motion into electricity.  The &apos;motion&apos; in this device is movement of possibly 1mm.  We will be able to detect the motion when we get it working &amp; the magnet assy being attached or not should not effect functionality of this half of the device..  </p><p>So, there we have it.  A badly described device that does not work !.  But, if we can make it work, it will be an amazingly elegant electricity generating device.  It should work from any heat source.  This leads to the tantalizing possibility of running it on solar power during the day &amp; gas at night. That would be a biggie, meaning that a solar device does not have to do noting when the sun does not shine... &amp; even NASA could use solar power when their plutonium runs out in deep space..  </p><p>&#x201C;Course heading, Captain?&#x201D;.  &#x201C;&#x2026;Second star to the right &#x2014; and straight on &apos;til morning<a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nrizm2gnQBo" target="_blank"></a>.&#x201D;</p><p>More updates to follow....<span class="redactor-invisible-space"></span>   <br></p></div>
