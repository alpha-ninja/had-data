                            <h2><a href="/project/4201/log/19413-new-board-ready-with-lots-of-new-function">New board ready! with lots of new function</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">2 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4201/log/19413#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-19413"><p>I have put several additional functions such as 2-byte kanji ROM, SPI-SRAM, and recently piezo beep modules. These are implemented by jumper-by-jumper and it is getting fragile for handling. So I made a new board containing all additional components as following pictures.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/711761434189642039.jpg"></p><p>It looks similar to previous one,,,, yes certainly. but behind OLED....</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/240871434189704910.jpg"></p><p>RTC (PCF2129AT, crystal embedded single chip RTC),  23K256 SPI SRAM, SPI Kanji-ROM are arranged on the frontside. As same as previous one, Hardware-serial (fortunately ATMega1284P has two hardware serials, it is quite useful!) is put on top-left for camera connection and D0/D1 terminal is put for thermal printer interface (working by software serial,,, This tiny computer has total three serial terminals!!)</p><p>You may think,, "where is CPU?"</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/2134631434189935921.jpg"></p><p>Yes, it is backside with RTC back-up battery. The five un-touched terminals are A/D converter inputs of ATMega1284 and not yet populated.</p><p>I am not sure the board and schematic information is useful, but I have already uploaded at</p><p><a href="https://github.com/kodera2t/TinyBasicPlus_OLED_support/tree/master/atmega1284p_v4_gerber">github....</a> have fun!</p></div>
                            </div>
                        </li>
                    
                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/4201/log/19466-by-product-kanji-clock-with-every-hour-beep-music">By product: Kanji clock with every hour beep music</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">2 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4201/log/19466#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-19466"><p>As wrote in the previous log, I put RTC and Kanji ROM on the board. As a first application using these component, I quickly made "Clock by Kanji display with hour beep"</p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/1T2eyee8XfM" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p>Actually this time has no relation with TinyBasic and it is just a clock program, but as far as I know, most of clock by Arduino is made of just single byte ASCII code clock but this clock showing "Kanji",, I guess most of western people are hard to read and understand. In addition to Kanji clock, every hour it will play beep by different music. Full source is uploaded to <a href="https://github.com/kodera2t/TinyBasicPlus_OLED_support/blob/master/Kanji_clock1.ino">github...</a> </p><p>Please enjoy Asian exotic taste! Have fun!</p></div>
