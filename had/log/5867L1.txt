                            <h2><a href="/project/5867/log/19259-delicate-leds">Delicate LEDs</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">2 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5867/log/19259#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-19259"><p>Just wanted to note, we did a rigid PCB demo board with 64 APA102Cs and it is clear the APA102Cs are very delicate compared to even other LEDs.  I believe we will need to utilize a lower temperature solder or improve our reflow oven's timing.  We ended up needing to rework many APA102Cs and re-reworking some.  The demo proved our concept though, so we will next be producing hand etched flexible PCB prototypes.</p></div>
                            </div>
                        </li>
                    
                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/5867/log/20493-update-on-rigid-design">Update on rigid design</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a month ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5867/log/20493#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-20493"><p>Rigid version works quite well with a much lower 220'C reflow.  I am using leaded solder, but I'll be trying some experiments to bring the SAC solder reflow to lower temp with a longer soak too.</p><p>We originally planned to make a low cost microcontroller version to just make pretty jewelry that integrates LEDs.  Since the rigid is somewhere between this and a garment insertable panel, I figure it will most likely be used in DIY projects.  I grabbed Adafruit's 5V Trinket board file and made a rough pattern for it as an optional controller.  The result is available on Dirty PCB <a href="http://dirtypcbs.com/view.php?share=8802&amp;accesskey=886f9b5b20749e82de0fed6f0aea530a">http://dirtypcbs.com/view.php?share=8802&amp;accesskey=886f9b5b20749e82de0fed6f0aea530a</a> .  We made an order of them (obviously) and we hope to start using and selling them in a couple weeks when we get them.</p><p>I'll still be working on a smaller footprint controller with battery power.  Probably based on Cortex-M0 with Tag Connect SWD pads.  It will be used for jewelry and small accents.  I'll post the design in a new project.<br></p></div>
                            </div>
                        </li>

We plan to get around 80 orders and order the rest to use ourselves and to sell on Tindie.  This is for the 5&quot; 64x APA102C 16mm spaced flexible PCB design.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/8994251437556921038.jpg"></p><p>Here is the hand etched version running a rainbow pattern:<br></p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/f-bebIYQ4ag" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" width="500"></iframe><p>Chasing color pattern at full brightness:</p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/ZFTAP3S0BtA" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" width="500"></iframe></div>
