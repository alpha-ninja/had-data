                            <h2><a href="/project/3725/log/22970-lit-review-lite-frame-design">Lit. Review Lite: Frame Design</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">3 days ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/3725/log/22970#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-22970"><p> This is a lighter literature review than some applications call for. I am excusing myself from anything more deptailed until I understand the math behind most Mechanical Engineering. Currently I lack a background in Physics, Statics, Dynamics, Calculus (differential and partial differential equations), Linear Algebra, and applications there-of.</p><p>I am taking steps to rectify these gaps in my knowledge. I have enrolled in a Calculus course at my local community college. I am enrolled in an online course for Linear Algebra and its applications (specifically in programming). I am completing a course in programming (CS50 through edX.org) after which I will be approaching Physics.</p><p>And there is probably more that I haven&apos;t mentioned. Keeping all this straight is a task unto itself and it can be rather time-consuming.</p><p>Complaining aside; here is what you&apos;ve skipped everything above for:</p><hr><p>Hung, Jui Pin, Yuan Lung Lai, Tzuo Liang Luo, and Hsin Chuan Su. 
2013. &#x201C;Analysis of the Machining Stability of a Milling Machine 
Considering the Effect of Machine Frame Structure and Spindle Bearings: 
Experimental and Finite Element Approaches.&#x201D; International Journal of 
Advanced Manufacturing Technology 68 (9-12): 2393&#x2013;2405. 
doi:10.1007/s00170-013-4848-6.</p><p><strong>Overview</strong></p><p>Analysis of the Machining Stability of a Milling Machine 
Considering the Effect of Machine Frame Structure and Spindle Bearings: 
Experimental and Finite Element Approaches (hereafter &quot;the study&quot; or &quot;the paper&quot;) specifically considers the combined dynamics of a vertical milling machine frame and spindle. An application of finite element analysis was used to predict the dynamics of the milling machine and spindle and an experiment was used to confirm the analysis.</p><p><strong>How is the study useful to this project?</strong></p><p>In the introduction the author&apos;s clearly state how this application of experimental and mathematical analysis could be directly useful to an end-user, saying that, &quot;The prediction [...] of the abnormal vibration is of great importance for the selection of the free chatter machining conditions.&quot; This study aims to specifically address the dynamic conditions that result from using a high-speed spindle in combination with the mill&apos;s frame. A complete solid model is used for the analysis, rather than a simplified model (as other studies have done).</p><p>Items that are specifically useful are the mathematics and derived equations, the mathematic modeling methodology used for the spindle unit and mill unit.</p><p>The math presented will allow me to <em>not</em> have to experiment to find the modal frequency by experimentation and will thus allow a program to handle the heavy lifting (iterative design via algorithm) once the design phase has begun.</p><hr><ol><li>Huo, Dehong, Kai Cheng, and Frank Wardle. 2010a. &#x201C;Design of a Five-Axis 
Ultra-Precision Micro-Milling Machine-UltraMill. Part 1: Holistic Design
 Approach, Design Considerations and Specifications.&#x201D; International 
Journal of Advanced Manufacturing Technology 47 (9-12): 867&#x2013;77. 
doi:10.1007/s00170-009-2128-2.</li><li>&#x2014;&#x2014;&#x2014;. 2010b. &#x201C;Design of a Five-Axis Ultra-Precision Micro-Milling 
Machine-UltraMill. Part 2: Integrated Dynamic Modelling, Design 
Optimisation and Analysis.&#x201D; International Journal of Advanced 
Manufacturing Technology 47 (9-12): 879&#x2013;90. 
doi:10.1007/s00170-009-2129-1.</li></ol><p><strong>Overview</strong></p><p>These studies walk through the design and implementation process of an ultra-precise (&quot;nano-meter finish&quot;) 5-axis CNC milling machine. The first article focuses on the existing ultra-precision machines, component options, and the specifications for the proposed &quot;UltraMill&quot;.</p><p>The second article focuses on the modeling and simulation of the &quot;UltraMill&quot; with an emphasis on machine dynamics. The simulation allows the authors to predict the performance of the machine over its entire performance envelope.</p><p><strong><strong>How is the study useful to this project?</strong></strong></p><p>The second portion of the paper is far more useful than the first. While the first sheds some light on the goals of the entire project, the second shows the process by which specific design requirements were met analytically.</p><p>I will be posting a more in depth review of these last two papers (as well as an outline with my notes) for the purpose of adapting the design process to this project&apos;s needs.<br></p></div>

                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-23103"><p>My light literature review continues. In this rather short episode I consider more advanced math that I am rather unprepared to tackle. Huzzah for brain-pain!</p><hr><p>Park, Sung-Ryung, and Seung-Han Yang. 2010. &#x201C;Design of a 5-Axis Machine 
Tool Considering Geometric Errors.&#x201D; International Journal of Modern 
Physics B 24 (15n16): 2484&#x2013;89. doi:10.1142/S0217979210065131.</p><p><strong>Overview</strong></p><p>This paper presents a kinematics drive approach for modeling and predicting volumetric error within a 5-axis mill&apos;s movement. Three mill configurations are considered, analyzed, and the critical design factors contributing to volumetric error are identified.</p><p><strong>How is this study useful to this project?</strong></p><p>This study may represent a way to identify components and assemblies that are critical to precision very early in the design phase. Further, because the math is available it may prove useful during computer driven design revisions for the identification and selection of components that minimize volumetric errors. </p><p><strong>What problems will utilizing this research present?<strong></strong></strong><strong><span class="redactor-invisible-space"><strong></strong></span></strong></p><p><strong><span class="redactor-invisible-space"><span class="redactor-invisible-space"></span></span></strong><span class="redactor-invisible-space"><span class="redactor-invisible-space">Aside from my distinct lack of training in mathematics the largest challenge will be to appropriately integrate this into some form of computer driven task. A deeper understanding of the method and its outcomes will aid in figuring out where this will fit in the design process and what decisions it will contribute to making.</span></span><strong><span class="redactor-invisible-space"><span class="redactor-invisible-space"></span><strong></strong></span></strong><br><strong><strong></strong></strong></p></div>
