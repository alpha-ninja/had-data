A couple of issues meant the maxi was under powered and ultimately left out of contention.                                 <div id="post-body-10472"><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7981421413456025318.jpg"></p><p>This massive #meArm was designed and 3D printed by instructables member <a href="http://www.instructables.com/member/Jazzmyn/">Jazzmyn</a>, who it would seem is a serial hacker and maker! She purchased a <a href="http://www.adafruit.com/products/2012">Heisenberg Blue #meArm from Adafruit</a> and learned so much from the build (her words not mine!;)) that she decided to have a go at making a bigger, stronger, longer version. I think she's been amazingly successful! She plans to add an electromagnet to the end and I would assume assemble some kind of Matchstick car crusher!</p><p>Jazzmyn has entered the <a href="http://www.instructables.com/id/My-meArm-Bigger-Stronger-Longer/">#meArm - Bigger, Stronger, Longer</a> into a few contests so if you're over at instructables please vote for her! Or if you're on the featured team then what are you thinking?! It's a 3D printed robot arm!! Feature it stat!</p><p>I personally want to say great job and thanks for sharing. More power to your #meArm (literally!). </p></div>

                            <small class="authorship">8 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/181/log/12024#discussion-list">
                                    1 comment
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-12024"><p>The MeArm was lucky enough to be invited along to the Hackaday Prize Party in Munich.  Since the MeArm was going to be used in the Roboto workshop we thought we'd make a little something special to honour the occasion. That was the Hackaday Prize Party Special Limited Edition MeArm!</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1917521419008788821.jpg">25 were made for the event and boy were they made for the event! All assembled in the bar the night before the event by hard pressed Hackaday staff or by myself and willing volunteers at the prize party. Being tired and emotional at the end of the MeArm build marathon it seems I forgot one thing! The spares! We'd actually produced 28 as we expected there to be a few breakages. Since they were minimum it means we actually have three Limited Edition Hackaday Prize MeArms (numbered 26/25, 27/25 and 28/25) left!</p></div>
