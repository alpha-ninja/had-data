                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-14728"><p>Now that the footprints for most of the Flex Modules are set, we will begin producing libraries for various EDA tools.  We will support DipTrace, Eagle, KiCAD, and maybe others.  We plan to support Fritzing and Cynthia(Cindy) and I will be releasing breadboard demo designs for it assuming we can make decent SVG images for the Flex Modules.</p><p>I'll put some effort into cleaning up schematics and producing PDFs to make it easier to view from the web.  We will also produce a datasheet for each board. The BOMs are complete for the new Flex Modules and we will be testing them in the next few weeks as they come in.</p><p>In between Flex Module board testing/redesign work, we will be working on testing jigs and demos as we can.  I am getting quotes from the distributors and I'll also be talking to manufacturers to try to negotiate prices further.  By the end of March we should be selling Flex Modules again.<br></p></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/2236/log/21033-design-updates">Design updates</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a month ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/2236/log/21033#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-21033"><p>I believe I have an improved design with the i2c solder bridges.  Most i2c devices in the latest designs have a solder bridge to optionally enable the i2c pull up resistors.  In addition, the address solder bridges are closer together to simplify soldering.  Almost all sensor and output boards are now 2 layer, so that should lower costs in the long run.  I have all the boards up on Tindie (other than some new ones we will be making projects for shortly).  I am still testing the microcontrollers and finishing drivers.  So those boards, though up on Tindie will be out of stock till I feel they are really ready.</p><p>I will be launching a tiny Indiegogo campaign to test interest in customizable flexible LED panels.  The first up will be the 5&quot; Light Square.  We have some smaller rigid LED jewelry concepts that I will be experimenting with once the initial board run is delivered.</p><p>The mini EL works better than expected and I am very happy with its design.<br></p></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/2236/log/21991-pattern-improvements">Pattern Improvements</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">18 days ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/2236/log/21991#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-21991"><p>Our last order from OSHPark I made the solder bridge spacing 10mil.  These look much better and should be less of a challenge to solder.  I&apos;ll be porting our castellation shape, solder bridges, and the Flex Module patterns to Eagle (and KiCAD) soon.  We had a volunteer for some layout work and so I need to make our patterns more portable.</p><p>I am still working on some firmware updates, especially to our haptic boards as they are so popular and the current libraries from Adafruit and Precision Microdrives are limited in features and testing.</p><p>We got our initial TinyLED and rigid Light Square designs back from DirtyPCB and they look good.  I&apos;ll test them and hopefully get some videos up of them soon.  The accelerometer on the TinyLED is not going to work (I missed a couple of pins) on the initial design.  I&apos;ll have a run ordered after testing that should work :). </p><p>I have a bunch of samples in from Arrow (thanks ST, TI) and Coilcraft, so the new Flex Module projects I put up should be coming along.  Many of the light sensing boards will take a few revisions to get right as they will have source LEDs that need experimental placement to be reflected accurately.  Some boards take only a day.  Some take months of testing.<br></p></div>

                            <small class="authorship">a day ago  &bull;

                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-23679"><p>Licensing is a pretty complicated discussion, and I wanted to try to clarify our position since the HackadayPrize requires license declaration.<br></p><p>We are releasing our SociACL and any related SAAS as AGPL to keep any usage of it open.  I tend to prefer to use BSD license for drivers, but the MIT license is much simpler so for anything else that is code we make it, will default to that.</p><p>I have updated the general repositories to show the MIT license, though the driver repository will be a mix and the individual files will likely contain their own licenses that may be a version of the BSD.  We will only publish OSI approved licenses and retain the license of any drivers or other software we have made any derivative from.</p><p>As for content on our blog content, images (excluding any servicemarks or trademarks), and anything else we will be using the following CC license:</p><p><a rel="license" href="http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/"><img alt="Creative Commons License" src="https://i.creativecommons.org/l/by-sa/4.0/88x31.png"></a></p></div>
