                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-19264"><p>Even though it is not quite finished, I used my power supply for the first time today (the first time for a real project, not just testing the supply itself).  I was powering on an H-Bridge circuit for the first time, and wanted to a) ensure that it didn't suck too much current if there was a bug, b) see how much current was being drawn.  It performed both tasks flawlessly (and for the record, there was in fact a problem with the H-Bridge board, so current limiting came in handy!</p></div>
                            </div>
                        </li>
                    
                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/4154/log/19609-almost-there">Almost There...</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">2 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4154/log/19609#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-19609"><p>The past week or two has been very busy and productive, although I have not posted many updates.  Most of the work has been related to the case.</p><p>I have always planned for the case to be made of wood.  I had a rough idea of the design (two sides + top, joined with finger joints, sliding down over the aluminum faceplates and secured with screws on the sides).  In order to do this, though, I needed to make some tools... read more for details and to see all sorts of pictures!</p><p><br></p><p>In the past I have made finger joints on my scroll saw.  It works, but it is slow and error prone.  I have had a router for years, but did not have a decent router table, so I was limited in the jigs that I could make.</p><p>So, first step was to build a router table.</p><p>I based my design loosely on <a href="http://www.finewoodworking.com/woodworking-plans/video/stow-and-go-router-table.aspx" target="_blank">this design</a>.  I skimped on some parts (e.g. I used 1x4's for the legs, etc), but it turned out to be quite nice.  I put a <a href="http://www.leevalley.com/en/hardware/page.aspx?p=45161&amp;cat=3,43576,61994,45161" target="_blank">mitre slot from Lee Valley Tools</a> into the table to allow for jigs.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/497061434467517882.JPG"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3872721434467560380.JPG"></p><p>Next I made a finger joint jig.  I based my design off of <a href="http://www.finewoodworking.com/interactive/finger-joint-jig-for-the-tablesaw/" target="_blank">these plans</a> (even though they were designed for the table saw, the concept is the same)</p><p>I don't have pictures of the actual cutting (I don't have three hands, although sometimes I wish I did), but here is the setup right before I turned the router on:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4212341434467696808.JPG"></p>The cuts worked out well; both sides fit together perfectly.<p>I then (over the next few days) cut some air outflow holes in the sides + top, epoxied nuts to angle brackets so that the sides can be secured to the bottom, cut slits in the grooves + top on the table saw for the aluminum to slip into, and finally, last night, glued everything together:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3269991434467854553.JPG"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/9269231434467890500.JPG"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1670861434467875432.JPG"></p>Over the next few days I will be sanding, staining, and attaching the fan grills to the outflow holes.  I am considering adding some handles as well, although I may not do that (it is not meant to be portable, and will likely spend most of its existence on the same shelf in my electronics area).  I also may make a wooden border for the screen (I didn't get the aluminum cuts quite as nice as I had hoped to...)<p>Cheers</p></div>
                            </div>
