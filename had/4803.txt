                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Depression is an absolute bastard. <br /><br />For me, it is like a wave. All fine and then I crash. <br /><br />Would an early warning system be possible? Buddhist monks, through years of training, use mindfulness to recognise unhelpful thoughts. If we knew our thought patterns were changing, we could take active steps to say NO! Unfortunately, we rarely ever notice this change. But could technology help?<br /><br />I propose using a massive mash-up of:<br />---bio-sensor data from on our gadgets such as skin conductance, heart-rate and sleep.<br />---fine movement detection from our smartphones (movements SLOW in depression).<br />---voice analysis via smartphones<br />---implantable EEG?<br /><br />                                             With thousands of people worldwide freely providing their data.<br /><br />                                             To figure what combination of factors best predict a depressive.  <br /><br />                                             To make the world's first:<br /><br />DEPRESSION ALARM.<br /><br /></div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>negative thoughts prompt a whole host of physiological changes, some of which can be detected fairly easily:<br></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">skin conductance -- e.g. <a href="http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1469-8986.1991.tb03392.x/abstract" target="_blank">http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1469-8986.1991.tb03392.x/abstract</a></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">heart rate chance -- e.g. <a href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0167876006002194" target="_blank">http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0167876006002194</a></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">(not so easy to detect) eeg -- e.g. <a href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0191886905000413" target="_blank">http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0191886905000413</a> </p><p>On their own, this data is not so useful.I suspect though, that if they were used together, they would a lot better at detecting short term and long term patterns leading to a 'downward swing'. </p><p>Mashing in data other sources could improve this too:</p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">voice pattern analysis. -- e.g. <a href="http://knoceq.blogspot.co.uk/2013/12/detection-of-disease-through-sound.html" target="_blank">http://knoceq.blogspot.co.uk/2013/12/detection-of-disease-through-sound.html</a></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;"><a href="http://knoceq.blogspot.co.uk/2013/12/detection-of-disease-through-sound.html"></a>fine movement changes. -- e.g. <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17515173" target="_blank">http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17515173</a></p><p>How to combine this information? One option is to use artificial neural networks like used by Smartphones for understanding speech <a href="http://www.wired.com/2014/06/siri_ai/" target="_blank">http://www.wired.com/2014/06/siri_ai/</a> . </p><p>THE CHALLENGES</p><p>--enthusiasm for this project! Join us and spread the word! Citizen Science!</p><p>--are there other bio-signals that can be used? E.g. could we develop an unobtrusive headset to be worn to detect errant brain patterns via EEG? e.g. <a href="http://www.newscientist.com/article/mg21829146.000-etattoo-monitors-brainwaves-and-baby-bump.html#.VQfp_I6sWw4" target="_blank">http://www.newscientist.com/article/mg21829146.000-etattoo-monitors-brainwaves-and-baby-bump.html#.VQfp_I6sWw4</a>. </p><p>--we will need thousands of people to take part in studies. I expect an app on your phone would monitor your vitals and periodically ask you about your mood. e.g. <a href="http://www.ted.com/talks/matt_killingsworth_want_to_be_happier_stay_in_the_moment?language=en" target="_blank">http://www.ted.com/talks/matt_killingsworth_want_to_be_happier_stay_in_the_moment?language=en</a></p><p>WHO CAN HELP?<br></p><p>--everyone, by taking part in our studies.</p><p>--software engineers, hackers, statisticians, psychologists, citizen scientists.</p><p>CAN I HELP NOW?</p><p>Yes! Start chatting about this. Sign up to the project.</p>
                                
                            </p>
