                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">A Connect a coffee jug to the internet via a network of NRF24l01 and the ESP8266. Data stored and displayed on ThingSpeak.com and the VFD.<br /><br />Jug:<br />- 3 LDRs in order to detect the level of coffee<br />- 1 TMP36 for temperature<br />- 1 NRF24l01 module<br />- 1 MSP430G2553 running energia<br /><br />Base relay:<br />- 1 16Character Vacuum Fluorescent Display<br />- 1 MSP430G2553 running energia<br />- 1 NRF24l01 module<br />- 1 ESP8266 running the AT firmware<br /></div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p><strong>Hardware:</strong></p><p>- The necessary components for the MSP430 to be operational are quite simple: only a CR2032 battery for power, two decoupling capacitors, a pull-up resistance on the RST pin. Note some pins connected to TX, TEST, RST and GND have been soldered in order to facilitate the programming and debugging of the MSP430.<br></p><p>- The cluster in the top left corner are the LDR in a potential divider with the 10k resistors and the TMP36 temperature sensor: light levels are then directly read with the pins analogue A0, A1, A3 from the MSP430, and the temperature from A4.<br></p><p>- The rest is just a lot of wires to connect the MSP430 to the transmitter NRF24L01, the colour code is used to facilitate the wiring (sorry for brown --&gt; GND but black looked messy)</p><p><strong>Software:</strong></p><p>On the software side, the micro first convert the analog read from the temperature sensor to celsius degrees. Then combined with the light sensors, they determine the level of coffee: full, half, low or empty. Finally the temperature and the coffee level is sent to the receiver to be uploaded on the web.</p><p>The coffee jug transmitter circuit is implemented on a stripped board:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/5242361425750036958.jpg"></p><p>Messy Middle man NRF24l01 to ESP8226:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7097341425749969657.jpg"></p><p>Level of coffee followed by its temperature:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/2070601425873495527.jpg"></p>
