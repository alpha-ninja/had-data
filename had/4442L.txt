                                <div id="post-body-14532"><p>Ultra thin and tiny keyboard for tiny computers is finished! I made a small interface board which transferring from touching position information to ASCII code, which is sending out 4800bps (as same as PS/2 and chatpad of Xbox360) to computer.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3504761425045227150.JPG"></p><p>Now this keyboard supports full CAPS and shift operation. In addition to complete your tiny computer project, this keyboard will work for Raspberry Pi and even old Sun Sparc workstation, since those keyboards (Type 3, 4, 5) are fundamentally serial keyboard. </p></div>

                                <div id="post-body-14564"><p>A electronics shop (Aitendo.com ) in Akihabara, Tokyo is always selling rare and mysterious electronic part I sometimes wonder where they get and why they are selling such a wonderful things.</p><p>Also this time, </p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7757411425126505076.JPG"></p><p>I got a 7-inch touch-screen panel (only, without LCD) by 950JPY. This touch-screen works as a x-y position to resistive value as same as previous small one. I simply enlarged the previous keyboard layout and quickly made...</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7857011425126646892.JPG"></p><h2>Full size? (still small,, may be) <br>                  Touch typing capable ultra-thin keyboard!</h2><p>Let's see how it works in the following movie!</p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/BpnAnSP_JUA" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p>Currently the keyboard controller is ATMega328P but if this chip is replaced with ATMega32u4 (not u2, because A/D is indispensable), this thin keyboard will become USB keyboard with any layout as you wish!!!!</p></div>

Any computer and tablet supprorting HID USB ( like Raspberry Pi, BeagleBone, of course just PC) is working with this ultra thin keyboard.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/2882911426858151801.JPG"></p><p>The good point is, it can be "water proof" if whole unit is shrinked in vinyl bag and no need for maintenance ( I am not sure the lifetime of touch panel, though..)</p><p>The actual operation can be seen in the following movie, (please wait for youtube processing, if it does not appear..).</p><p>Have fun!!</p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/66DBhy7jiUc" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe></div>
