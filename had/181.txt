                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">The MeArm project aims to bring a simple Robot Arm well within the reach and budget of the average educator, student, parent or child. The design brief we set out with was to build a full robot arm kit with standard low cost screws, low cost servo motors and using less than 300 x 200mm (~A4) of acrylic. <br /><br />The current version is the v0.4, which is a collaboration between Jack Howard and Benjamin Gray. We've both learned a great deal from the Open Hardware movement and this project aims to contribute and bring people into the Open Hardware scene. <br /><br />There are well over 1000 MeArms in the wild at the time of writing, we're collecting feedback, working on improving the build instructions, improving the control of the arm and improving the electronics set up.</div>
                </div>

                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p name="578b">The MeArm is an Open Sourced Robot Arm. It's small, like pocket sized and that's for a reason. It can be cut entirely from an A4 (or more accurately 300x200mm) sheet of acrylic and built with 4 cheap hobby servos. It's live on our site for about $35 or £25 for those in the UK. All the files are linked on this project page and hosted on thingiverse. It supposed to be an educational aid, or more accurately a toy. It still needs some tinkering but is at a good first draft state. There will be more instructions coming and no doubt more tweaks too.</p><p name="c386">Please get involved, build your own, break it, improve it and break it again.</p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>
