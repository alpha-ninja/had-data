                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">I am developing a single cylinder open source scalable diesel engine (based on the Junker Jumo 205 motor) to be used in a multitude of projects. This engine can be used to power moving sculptures, micro cars, mopeds, generators, pumps or anything that needs a power source. The open source approach allows me to develop a quality engine for everyone to enjoy at a minimal cost. I have received interest from several non-profit organizations (that would like to use it in 3rd world countries), artists/designers, manufacturers and several private companies.</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>The purpose of this project is to design an easy to manufacture, light weight, low cost diesel engine. The initial design will be for a 49.5cc engine with the intent of being able to eventually scale to 125cc and 250cc. Such a motor could be used to power mopeds, small farm equipment, well drilling machines, pumps, etc.</p><p>I have chosen the Junker Jumo engine design due to its piston configuration. On a standard diesel engine heavy cast iron cylinders and heads have to be manufactured in order to withstand the engine's compression. With the compression and fuel detonation taking place between two opposed pistons and in the center of the cylinder the walls can be thinner the further they are from the center of the cylinder. Both pistons will connect to the same crankshaft reducing the number of rotating parts.</p><p>Variable compression can be handled by an ECU (engine control unit) electric turbocharger.</p><ul><li>Geometric Compression - Is the compression that is created by the physical dimensions of the parts when the are at TDC (top dead center) or in this case DC (dead center).</li><li>Effective Compression - The adjusted compression when additional pressure is added to the cylinder.</li></ul><p>Along with designing the engine itself I am also planning on designing the tools and jigs required to build/manufacture the components. These would be open sourced as well.</p><p><strong>Cylinder:</strong></p><p>I would like to sand cast the cylinder out of aluminum using either a routered hardwood or ABS 3D printed pattern. The casting would be bored and a machined steel sleeve would be pressed into the cylinder housing. This would be an air cooled motor.</p><p><strong>Pistons:</strong></p><p>The pistons would be a two piece design with an aluminum skirt and steel crown. The skirt would be sand cast out of aluminum using either a routered hardwood or ABS 3D printed pattern. The crown would be machined out of steel. Traditional style piston rings would be used.</p><p><strong>Case:</strong></p><p>I would like to sand cast the case out of aluminum using either a routered hardwood or ABS 3D printed pattern.<br></p><p><strong>Connecting Rods:</strong></p><p>Sand cast out of aluminum using either a routered hardwood or ABS 3D printed pattern. The rod journals would be machined and fitted with bearings.<br></p><p><strong>Valves:</strong></p><p>Since this is a two stroke engine it would be a valveless design.</p><p><strong>Power:</strong></p><p>A modified magneto would provide power for glow pugs, ECU, etc.</p><p><strong>Adjustable Timing:</strong></p><p>Still trying to figure out how timing could be adjusted on the fly, Any suggestions?</p><p>I would like to clarify that I don't see these motors being manufactured on the open planes of Africa as some have assumed. I see them being manufactured in shops (home, industrial, whatever) and being used in rural areas. I want to keep the design and simple as possible so when the day comes that repairs need to be made they can be done with minimal shop availability. </p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>

                    <p>The main issue is weight, diesels generally need thick cast iron engine blocks to withstand the compression. Using two opposing pistons meant (in theory) that only the center of the cylinder needs to withstand the pressure. It wasn't super efficient and was pretty bulky for only being 18cc.</p><p>I feel we are at a point where technology and manufacturing techniques are good enough to pull off this build.<br> </p>
