                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">I'm looking to get collaboration and input from the HaD community for the design of a half sleeve tattoo I want of a circuit board design, that also will work as a functional PCB.<br /><br />Originally I was thinking an Arduino clone or similar with awesome tracework is what's needed, but now- I'm willing to go a step further and get one of the HaD Prize 2015 finalists permanently inked onto my body.</div>

                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>Like the description says, I'm looking to help in designing a functional tattoo (design.) I think it makes sense to design and produce the PCB first, and verify that it will function. Then upon validation of that, get a tattoo of the trace-art done on my shoulder/ upper arm.</p><p><br>This is a shot in the dark at using HaD Projects for something a bit more than traditional hardware. I want everything to be open source, too.<br></p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>

                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/4101/log/13434-you-have-to-start-somewhere">You have to start somewhere</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">7 months ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4101/log/13434#discussion-list">
                                0 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>What's a good starting point for this project? Learning about PCB design. I've always been a big fan of pulling things apart/ turning them on and playing with it, <em>then</em> reading the manual. I'm doing that with PCBs, and what a better place to start than by downloading the EAGLE files for the <a href="http://arduino.cc/en/uploads/Main/arduino-Due-Reference-design.zip">Arduino Due</a>? This nets us a fun two layer board design as follows:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/5247951422424351561.PNG">So at first look, this looks pretty sweet, but it's a little busy and I'm not digging the ~4 colors for a tattoo. After a quick turning off of the vias and some other things in EAGLE (plus make it monotone), then BAM:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3163671422425241159.png"></p><p>Now that looks much better for a tattoo. At first I wasn't sure how things would work being dual layer, but this isn't too bad. I would like something that the shape would fit the contour of my shoulder/arm better.</p><p>If you have any interest in helping out with design or technical input, send me a message. I'm moving forward blind at this point, and I want to document the journey for fun. I'll try to update this as frequently as possible with thoughts and findings.</p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </li>
