                            <p><p>So today I recorded my Strawberry growing.  Some growth can be seen in the middle.<iframe width="420" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/DgTIqth-xAw" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe>I also got the <a href="http://www.ebay.com/itm/331531380465?_trksid=p2057872.m2749.l2649&amp;ssPageName=STRK%3AMEBIDX%3AIT">pH strips</a>! As you can see, the solution has pH of about 6, which is in the pH range hydroponic strawberries should be grown (5.5 - 6) (<a href="http://cals.arizona.edu/strawberry/Hydroponic_Strawberry_Information_Website/Irrigation.html">source</a>).</p><p>There is one problem though. I&apos;m not sure how accurate this is, since tap water appears to be 8 or 9 on the pH scale which looks absurd. I may test this pH strips tomorrow in some home made buffer solutions. </p><p><img src="http://i.imgur.com/gWwvT7K.jpg">&#x201C;Don&apos;t just wait for inspiration.                             <h2><a href="/project/6300/log/21566-cleaning-the-hydro-system-timelapse">Cleaning the hydro system, timelapse</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">23 days ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6300/log/21566#discussion-list">
                                0 comments

                            <p><p><strong>Abstract</strong>: I decided to clean the bucket and change the solution anyway. I also switched back to the full spectrum LEDs. </p><p>I&apos;ve changed the lights back to the full spectrum LEDs, since the 90W&apos;s of white LEDs gave off to much heat. </p><p>I taped some aluminium foil to the document file folder after I removed the mechanism with my dremle. <br></p><p><img src="http://shrani.si/f/40/Iy/348H6iig/ezgif-3288406072.gif"></p><p>I have also cleaned the bucket end everything, even my medium to hold all the roots in the net pot. I cleaned it with hot water and 12% hydrogen peroxide cream (for dying hair, but I guess it should also work as a cleaning agent).</p><p><img src="http://shrani.si/f/f/E8/JkHAIjS/ezgifcom-maker-1.gif"></p><p>Then I taped the bucket with aluminium foil so very little light would get into the bucket. I also glued down the air stone, so it would be under the roots all the time and wouldn&apos;t move.</p><p><img src="http://shrani.si/f/2G/nh/2mnjJEre/ezgifcom-maker-2.gif"></p><p>I added 2 litres (about 0.53 gallons) of water, then I mixed in (5ml of) HESI TNT Complex fertilizer (NPK is 3 2 3). It&apos;s supposed to be for soil only (they also make a separate one for hydroponics) but this is what I have by hand. I also added HESI Super Vit (one drop) which has some vitamins to help with the growth, together with (2.5ml of) BIOBIZZ ROOTJUICE root stimulator.</p><p><img src="http://i.imgur.com/LGtDNSy.jpg"></p><p>I also started a time lapse of the growth. The program is called webcam time lapse and I like using it. You can make a video form the photos inside the same program so that&apos;s nice too, since it&apos;s freeware. <a href="http://download.cnet.com/Webcam-Timelapse/3000-13633_4-75532347.html">Download link</a></p><p><img src="http://i.imgur.com/lAfSE9T.jpg"></p><p>Once again, I&apos;m ending this post with an inspiring quote, I think that I&apos;ll just write it at the end of every log, without announcing it. :D</p><p><em>Start by doing what&apos;s necessary; then do what&apos;s possible; and suddenly you are doing the impossible. <br>Francis of Assisi</em><em></em></p>

                            <h2><a href="/project/6300/log/21400-big-update-growing-hydroponic-strawberries">Big update, growing hydroponic strawberries</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">25 days ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6300/log/21400#discussion-list">
                                0 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>Finally, this is it. I&apos;m writing the new project log.<br>I had a really lazy week after my vacation so I procrastinated all the time and didn&apos;t write any logs. I did however start growing a strawberry hydroponically.</p><p>I&apos;ve written a lot about it here before, but my computer crashed and since the hackaday editor doesn&apos;t have auto save, I lost all that. I really don&apos;t have the nerve to rewrite it all so I will just write a short summary. </p><p><img src="http://i.imgur.com/9TlBsmd.jpg"><br>I&apos;ve transplanted a strawberry I have bought in a store to hydroponics (deep water culture). I am having some problems though.<br></p><p><img src="http://i.imgur.com/oCv9PM2.jpg"><br></p><p>Because I was havng problems I added more water and change the lights to 9 10W warm and cold white LEDs instead of about 22W full spectrum. I don&apos;t know what&apos;s causing the problems so I thought it&apos;s better so stick to white LEDs. I&apos;ve done a lot of things wrong growing this, like light stressing the plant since I don&apos;t control how many hours of light the strawberry gets, I don&apos;t check the pH ...
