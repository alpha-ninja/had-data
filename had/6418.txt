                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">For the Hackaday Prize, my chosen problems to attempt to solve are a need for companionship by just about everybody and the expense of companion robots.  The ultimate goal is, as the description says, to bring in a new age- an age where robots are intelligent and both humans and robots can live in harmony:<br /><br />“There was a time when humanity faced the universe alone and without a friend. Now he has creatures to help him; stronger creatures than himself, more faithful, more useful, and absolutely devoted to him. Mankind is no longer alone. Have you ever thought of it that way?”<br /><br /> - Susan Calvin, "I, Robot" by Isaac Asimov (exact quote courtesy of <a href='http://www.sciencefiction-quality.com/robbie.html' target="_blank">http://www.sciencefiction-quality.com/robbie.html</a> ).<br /><br />My community?  Humanity and robot kind is my community, and I am determined to be a leader in this glorious revolution that will make the world a better place for everyone.<br /><br /><br /></div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>How this will alleviate stated problems:</p><p>1. Refer to the quote in the description from the book &quot;I, Robot&quot; for the companionship/loneliness portion.  I think it sums the solution up better than I can.</p><p>2. Most commercial robots are several hundreds to thousands of dollars.  Using inexpensive hardware that&apos;s open for everybody to use, household supplies and other inexpensive materials, a companion robot can be made within the $100 to $200, potentially even less if you know where to look.<br></p><p>Potential Uses: </p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/9617381439657310688.png"></p><p>Goals:</p><p>- Have a mobile robot that is significantly less expensive than most commercial robots</p><p>- Have a personality coded that can be interacted with</p><p>- Have a <strong>foundation</strong> for creating an affordable roboticcompanion; as for the ultimate goal of the Initiative (extending far beyond the scope of the contest) will be to start a robotic revolution.</p><p>Not Goals:</p><p>- To create a sentient robot within the allotted time (I USED to be thisnaive; not anymore.  This is a very long term goal, but it is a goal.)</p><p>- To make any promises about what will and will not work; in all honesty, AIis still in its infancy, so development will take time, and no matter whatanybody says, there basically aren&apos;t any right or wrong solutions justyet.  Just because one method seems to be working well for AI doesn&apos;t meanit&apos;ll be the one that brings a robot revolution. <em> </em><strong><em>However, I will do whatever is in my power to make the Kasei Initiative be a leader in such a revolution, as it is coming, and it&apos;s going to be great.</em></strong></p><p>- To deceive anybody; I see many huge companies who thrive off newtechnologies like 3D printing and exploit it with restrictive patents and licenses, and I hate that so much.  Trust me- and Ihope you will grow to trust me through what I do and say- I am not that sort ofperson.  I will be honest in what I do; if it doesn&apos;t work, it doesn&apos;twork (see my &quot;Ehhh&quot; project log about the microcontroller)!  Also, I love open-source technology, and I will make this an open sourceproject, no matter how successful the Kasei Initiative becomes.</p><p>I hope I can count on your support, and any questions you have, ask, and Iwill (hopefully) be able to answer during a weekly blog post on the KaseiInitiative Site, which is linked on my Hackerspace.</p><p>System Design Document:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/9687221439822273354.png"></p><p>A separate one will come for the software once it is developed; the software part for now is simply my reasoning for choosing Python over the others since I do not plan to have the software done until the next checkpoint.</p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>
