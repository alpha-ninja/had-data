                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Oktopod Studio is an open source development platform for mechatronics, robotics and automation, which enables creating and controlling low voltage electronic devices, models and home applications. /in an extremely simple way/<br /><br />We designed Oktopod Studio to be as user friendly as possible, which features plug-and-play analog outputs, digital inputs, DC and Servo motor drivers and a graphical user interface for PC and Andriod devices.<br /><br />Using Oktopod Studio you don't need to be a programmer or an electronic expert to make your own robotic projects! </div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><img src="http://www.oktopodstudio.com/wp-content/themes/OKTOPOD/images/how1.jpg"><p>There are two parts of the system: <br> the <strong>hardware</strong> unit Oktopod_Board<br> and the <strong>software</strong> Oktopod_Control. </p><p><br> The connection between these two parts can be established through <strong>Bluetooth</strong> or <strong>USB</strong>.</p><p> <strong>HARDWARE:</strong> Oktopod_Board presents a Programmable Logic Controller (<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Programmable_logic_controller">PLC</a>), which features plug and play inputs and outputs for connecting low voltage electronic devices, like: LED lights, DC &amp; servo motors, electromagnets, switches, photo-, thermo- and magnetic sensors, and so on.<br><br> Technical characteristics:<br> - 8x Analog output (up to 3A)<br> - 2x DC motor driver<br> - 3x Servo motor driver<br> - 4x Digital inputs<br><br> Other: ATmega16 microcontroller, USB/Bluetooth communication modul, Power supply input 6-12 V (reverse polarity protected), On board 3 A fuse...<br> </p> <img src="http://www.oktopodstudio.com/wp-content/themes/OKTOPOD/images/okto-board.jpg" alt=""> <p> <strong>SOFTWARE:</strong> Oktopod_Control allows manual control or programming the board. There are PC and Android versions of the application, which both are open source! <br><br> In <strong>Manual Control</strong> mode, the software is used as a remote control for the board, like a virtual dashboard! The desired actions are executed in real time, by moving the sliders and pressing the buttons. <br><br> <strong>Programming</strong> the board is enabled by creating so called <strong>"WishList"</strong>. This presents a unique way of programming which you can get the hang of in just five minutes. When the board is connected, you just need to set the desired output actions, using the virtual dashboard, and add them to your WishList. When all of the actions are added to your list, just DOWNLOAD or click PLAY, and watch what happens! </p><p> For the inputs you can use different sensors: </p> <ul><li>Switches and tasters</li><li>Temperature sensors</li><li>Light sensors</li><li>Magnetic detectors</li><li>Sound sensors</li><li>Humidity sensors</li><li>...and any other sensos with digital output!</li></ul>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>
