                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>**** Please also check &quot;daily log&quot; below ****</p><p>And finally SDuino has an embedded graphic display.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/5807131437569484784.jpg"></p>Details can be found at <a href="https://hackaday.io/project/6435-sd-card-sized-board-lets-say-sduino/log/21319-sduino-advance-display-embedded-sduino">daily log!</a><p>--------</p><p>Now SDuino can measure pressure, temperature, and humidity. By measuring these values, SDuino will show the current temperature-humidity index, which is quite important to avoid heatstroke, the fatal disease in summer season!! (In Japan, hundreds suffers and a lot of people are dead, even well-organised 21st century!)</p><p>----------</p><p>history....</p><p>Once I was excited to see Intel&apos;s announce that they will release &quot;SD card sized (SD card shaped) computer&quot;, but it was not true. This time I made a real SD card sized full programmable (indeed, Arduino pro compatible) small computer board!</p><p>It consists of ATMega328P and four LEDs connected to D6 to D9. (red, blue, green, white).</p><p><span></span></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4541391435230731490.jpg"></p>As shown in above image, I assigned several non-standard function to SD card interface, but I needed to be careful for VCC and GND, not to make short circuit even if my wife puts it to computer by mistake. A0 is assigned to one of GND, which will not make fatal side-effect for computer connection.<p>Detailed operation can be found in the following movie... </p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/kxFpvQq4ABM" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p>And now I made very smart SDuino writer! Details can be found in the <a href="https://hackaday.io/project/6435-sd-card-sized-board-lets-say-sduino/log/20422-very-smart-sduino-writer-is-done">daily log!</a></p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1804201436006085140.jpg"><p>actual operation can be found in the following movie. Have fun!</p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/zD7MYRPAKNg" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p><span></span>.... actually I had an ambition to make self-powered (battery inside) SDuino, but smallest with enough current-out battery was 50mAh Lipo , which has more than 2mm thickness... Dammit!<br></p><p>(manganese dioxide has smaller one, but it can provide very small current around 0.5 mA...)<span class="redactor-invisible-space"><br></span></p><p>And now SDuino has pressure sensor init!</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1263181436443189648.jpg"></p><p>and now it can play music!!!! </p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/fHCXk_8IchY" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p>,, and now it can measure pressure, temperature, and humidity. By measuring these values, SDuino will show the current temperature-humidity index, which is quite important to avoid heatstroke, the fatal disease in summer season!! (In Japan, hundreds suffers and a lot of people are dead, even well-organised 21st century!)<br></p>

                            <p><p>Now my SDuino gained a bit more advanced sensing using very sophisticated sensor module (BME280) by Bosch. In my case, I just do it soldering iron as usual.</p><p>By measuring humidity, SDuino can evaluate &quot;discomfort index&quot; ( or temperature-humidity index) by following <a href="http://keisan.casio.com/exec/system/1351058230">equation</a></p><p><img src="/api/latex?tex=%5Ccolor%7BWhite%7D%20%5Clarge%20THI%3D0.81%20%5Ctimes%20T_d%20%2B0.01H(0.99%20%5Ctimes%20T_d%20-14.3)%20%2B46.3">, where T_d is temperature in deg.                     <p>SD card&nbsp;are&nbsp;accessed in 4-bit mode when it is hooked up to real products&nbsp;and at 25MHz/50MHz or higher. &nbsp;SPI mode is optional and is only&nbsp;for backward compatible reasons. &nbsp;As a media, you don't have much say on the access method. &nbsp;So unlikely this can be used without specialize OS drivers and hardware.</p><p>More interesting concept is a man in the middle approach as you would want some storage,&nbsp;not having to worry about talking to the host,&nbsp;handling small block sizes and implementing a wear levelling algorithm.&nbsp; &nbsp;i.e.&nbsp;Use&nbsp;muxes to multiplex a Micro&nbsp;SD between the host and your MCU. &nbsp;Also need to have program control of the power cycling as the SD vs SPI access mode&nbsp;is locked until power cycled.</p><p>Only simplified version of the&nbsp;specs are available to the public, but there are some additional info&nbsp;if you google hard enough.&nbsp;&nbsp;<a href="https://www.sdcard.org/downloads/pls/simplified_specs/">https://www.sdcard.org/downloads/pls/simplified_specs/</a></p>
