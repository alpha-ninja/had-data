                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Elderly asset tracker is intended to be worn by the elderly, those with dementia, or anyone you may care about.  Elderly asset tracker is MSP430 based, employs a Maestro Wireless A2235-H GPS module, and communicates via a SIM900 (GSM).  An accelerometer provides fall detection via inertial interrupts.<br /><br />What sets the elderly asset tracker apart from systems like Life Alert is its use the cellular network for communication.  This asset tracker works anywhere that a GSM (and GPS) signal is present.</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>The elderly asset tracker addresses several problems that have become<br>more pronounced over the last few decades, quality of life and safety of the elderly.  Advances in medicine have lead to increased lifespans which places a strain on existing methods of senior care, elderly asset tracker is an attempt to address this issue.  The tracker primarily consists of an msp430, GSM modem, GPS receiver, and an accelerometer.  The device is powered by a lithium ion battery and has a USB micro connector for charging. The device is meant to be worn and can alert any number of pre-programmed contacts in case of emergency.  The accelerometer provides inertial interrupts, detecting when the user is in free fall or experiencing a high g force event. The module can also accept commands via text message, the primary command being to reply back with the board&apos;s GPS coordinates, something that could be potentially life saving if someone with dementia gets lost. Battery life varies depending on the mode of operation and can last anywhere  from a few weeks to several<br>years.</p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>
