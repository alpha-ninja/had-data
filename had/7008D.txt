            <p><strong>Best Product Video: </strong></p><p><strong>Here is the condensed two minute version, Do yourself a favor and watch the one below it, it is a little longer but wont leave you feeling confused. </strong></p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/IlLdFgr1GbQ" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p><strong><br></strong></p><p>Here is the one I recomend watching:</p><p><strong><br></strong></p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/7i4o8qCxv3w" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p><strong>Note: [ when I say feed-forward I mean Open-Loop, it had been a long day] </strong><br></p><p>Here is a How To use the Products:</p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/fLURREGxTVw" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p>References:</p><p>[1] FAO Fisheries and Aquaculture Department &#x201C;THE STATE OF WORLD FISHERIES AND AQUACULTURE&#x201D; 2012</p><p>[2]  Steven Craig &#x201C;Understanding Fish Nutrition, Feeds, and Feeding&#x201D;    2002</p><p>[3] S. J. Yeoh, F. S. Taip  &#x201C;Development of Automatic Feeding Machine for Aquaculture Industry&#x201D; 2010</p><p><strong>Project Pre-Face</strong><br></p>  <p><strong>    </strong>  about me: Graduated with a Degree in Mechanical Engineering and a Masters in Electrical Engineering currently finishing my gap year and taking some time to design an automated greenhouse and small allotment type garden to provide a yearlong hassle free supply of food and a test bed for novel nutrient sourcing  Aquaponics grow system.</p>  <p> Until the age of 21 I would consider myself a computer-phoebe, using my laptop mainly as a coffee matt, Then I saw a tutor flying a Quadcopter and got  hooked!  with a lot of help from the tutor [Bob Mackin of Lancaster university] I overcame the phobia and  five years later I have automated and optimized many of the time consuming jobs on the farm and house, moving my area of expertise from mechanical engineering to control system engineering [Its much more Fun].</p>  <p><strong>Life Goals: </strong></p>  <p>&gt;Solve World hunger </p>  <p>&gt;Put a Sphere Based Automated Robot on the Moon</p>  <p>&gt;Give a Ted Talk to Promote Open Sourced Problem Solving </p>  <p>&gt;Enter a Hybrid 4x4 into the Dakar and Baja Rallies, to show them how it should be done</p>  <p><strong> </strong></p>  <p><strong>Shameless Plug</strong></p>  <p> I am looking for a PhD/Job in automation and control systems, preferably in regard to farming but open to offers. Willing to commute/relocate worldwide for the right position.</p>  <p><strong>Take a look at the website below for CV, Reference and examples of work: </strong></p>  <p><strong><a href="http://www.MichaelRatcliffe.com">www.MichaelRatcliffe.com</a>  </strong></p>  <p><strong>Want to Fund me ? I am in need of the many electronics components [drop me an message]. </strong></p>  <p><strong>Take a look at some of my other projects:</strong><br></p><p><strong>&gt;</strong><strong><a href="http://www.element14.com/community/community/design-challenges/vertical-farming/blog/tags#/?tags=adapted_greenhouse">Automated and Optimised Grow Cell [Over at Element14] </a></strong><br></p><h2><strong><br></strong></h2><h1>Problem: World hunger<br></h1>  <p>       Learning how to farm from your father as he did with your grandfather is no longer going to cut it, the world is changing, the demand is increasing and the methods are staying firmly the same, the number of people on the verge of starvation and suffering malnutrition is climbing and climbing fast.</p>  <p> So who will solve the problem, the government [probably not] me, you? We need modern solutions to modern problems, step in a hacker. A hacker see&#x2019;s a problem differently than the average person,where many see waste they will see opportunity. The Hacker Way is an approach to building that involves continuous improvement and iteration. Hackers believe that something can always be better, and that nothing is ever complete.</p>  <p>So what is a hacker&#x2019;s solution to world hunger, take something that is free and use it to solve a problem. See it as a computer code/ flow chart, what are the inputs, outputs and leakages in the food supply?  Plug the leaks, find alternative inputs and find a use for the waste.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4592211439810556144.png"></p><h1>Making Better Use</h1><p>       This is the Product submitted to the Best Product Challenge, it is a set of controllers providing modern solutions to modern problems. All aimed at taking the next great step towards automation of food delivery for maximum food efficiency and fish growth to feed the next billion. </p><p>The daddy of the controllers is the vision based feedback system aimed at fisheries using floating food [mainly the up and coming aquaponics industry]. However many fisheries don&apos;t use floating food, the product for this implements a well defined open-loop solution fine tuned for your environment. <br></p><p><strong>Some Basic Information:</strong><br></p><p>&gt;156 Billion KG of fish reared annual [that is more than beef!] [1]</p><p>&gt;Optimum feeding regimes can increase productivity by 40%, food efficiency by 20% compared to conventional once per day feeding [2]</p><p>&gt;There are many variables to consider when feeding fish [3]</p><p>       Feeding Fish Is a very time consuming task, taking skilled operators to visit sites multiple times a day and is prone to human error or implementation of inefficient feeding routines to save time. A computer could perform this task day in day out repeatable, efficiently and without human interaction, saving time, food and increasing yields from a system drastically.<br></p><p>If the potential benefits are so good, then why has no one already done this? I can only give you my opinion on this. The task of feeding fish is a complicated one, many variables and environmental factors effect the feeding of fish [see below] and fish farming is a pretty boring subject.<br></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3398441439811640827.png"></p><p>As you can see from the chart above the variables are numerous and sometimes hard to measure. Luckily a lot of these can be defined/measured and represented mathematical. </p><p><strong>Feed Spacing/Timings: </strong>The optimum time between feeding of fish depends on the size of the stomach of the fish, when they are young they require feeding regularly, decreasing as they grow larger. </p><p><strong>Feed Amount:</strong>  The amount in percent body weight to feed the fish daily is largley influenced by the water temperature and the age of the fish. These are the inputs needed for the open loop controller. The vision based feedback does not need to know this, it will use the rate of food consumption to mimic a skilled human feeder.</p><p><strong>Feed Chemistry:</strong>  the percent of protein you fed fish is related to the age of the fish and the water temperatures.<br></p><p><strong>A Note On DO</strong>: Dissolved Oxygen measurements are expensive, measuring probes need constant calibration and as such we will be assuming the pond has adequate oxygenation for growing fish. Spreading the feed serving over the day will spread the demand for DO over the day also. So in short, feeding multiple times a day decrease the peak demands for dissolved oxygen of the system. [this is a good thing] </p><p><strong>Useful Links for the Automated Feeders:</strong></p><p>&gt;DIY Feeder Mechanical Proportion</p><p>&gt;Control Box How-to </p><p>&gt;</p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/7i4o8qCxv3w" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p><strong>Open-Loop </strong></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;"><strong>Work Done:</strong></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">&gt;Code for Feed Amount vs Temperature</p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">&gt;Wiring Diagram</p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">&gt;How-to measure the feed-rate of your feeder</p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">&gt;Sourced housing for final product prototype</p><p style="margin-left: 20px;"><strong>Work To Do:</strong></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">&gt;Implement gps to allow feed timings implementation</p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">&gt;Build into the housing </p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">&gt;Increase type of fish from just Tilapia</p><p><strong>Camera Feed-Back</strong></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;"><strong>Work Done:</strong></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">&gt;Proved that food can be identified by the camera and OpenCv on Raspberry Pi</p><p style="margin-left: 20px;"><strong>&gt;Proved that Feed Delivery Rate can be changed by PWM</strong></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;"><strong>Work to Do:</strong></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">&gt;Control loop to keep constant amount of food in pond, making note of duty cycle </p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">&gt;Test at what time during decrease in PWM the fish are full for optimum growth</p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">&gt;Implement a simpler arduino version for the IBC Aquaponic growers out there </p><h1>Find Alternative Inputs<br></h1><p>       I have seen world hunger first hand in Africa and China, The poor people in these countries are seeing a problem that we in the west have not seen yet [But it is coming and approaching fast] starvation. So I took a little time to see what the problem was, surprisingly they had access to large volumes of water, they had sunlight the limiting factor was poor nutrient sources and soil. Hmm I have been growing with Aquaponics for years now, problem solved?<br></p><p>Take a Look at the Aquaponics testing set-up if you are interested:</p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/JMjDq4d86Qo" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p>Maybe not, in Aquaponics the food to feed the fish is relatively expensive for a person surviving on two dollars a day. What else can we use as a nutrient source instead of fish food, human pee will be a good source [its free, easily available]. And I ran some tests, it is possible, maybe considered disgusting in the west, but if you are hungry your definition of disgusting changes pretty quickly.<br></p>  <p>The problem with using urine is that a person will never output more nutrients than he eats, so we are really only reducing the nutrient cycle waste and not having a real input to it. It&#x2019;s a great start but we need to add to this solution. What is a plentiful and free nutrient source? On the day it was hard to think, I was sat in the hot sun enjoying a smoke and drink of tea in the south of china with a farmer who was just as interested in me as I was in his farming techniques, the flies were awful, ever drink of tea was accompanied with a complementary fly in your mouth. That farmer will think all westerns are crazy, for after eating  what must have been the 12th fly, I jumped up smiling at eating a fly. The solution had been more than staring me in the face it had been trying its hardest to get into my digestive system. The solution is flies, Fish eat Flies, and there is a definite correlation between poverty, hunger and the number of flies that piss you off while you are there.</p>  <p>I am just recently back from China, So now my concentration is on how to get the flies in the water, sure I could just hope they land in there for a drink but it is probable we need more than the stray flies popping in for a drink. </p>    <p><strong>The hackers Solution to free nutrient sourcing:</strong></p>  <p>What it need to do:</p>  <p>&gt;Energy efficient [Solar powered would be best]</p>  <p>&gt;Not source beneficial insects [bee&#x2019;s mainly]</p>  <p>&gt;Open source and cheap, despite their hospitality these farmers really haven&#x2019;t got the funds to pay me</p>    <p>Option one:</p>  <p><strong>Passive collection.</strong></p>  <p>This will be a very basic system that makes no attempt to identify how many flies it has sourced or what kind of insect it sources.</p>    <p><strong>Active Collection</strong></p>  <p>I recall reading about bill gates foundation making a mosquito laser killer and dubbed it &#x201C;Photonic Fence&#x201D;. Yes Really!  take a look:</p>  <p><a href="http://www.intellectualventureslab.com/work/photonic-fence">http://www.intellectualventureslab.com/work/photonic-fence</a> ]</p>  <p>We won&#x2019;t be going into as much effort as Bill due to budgeting concerns, we will be using a camera and Raspberry Pi to sense the flies and to keep costs below &#xA3;100.</p>      <p>Laser model: </p><p>Have a R-pi Based camera looking at a plain white back board above a fish pond, we can process the image to pinpoint dark spots [these will be the flies] exclude any with a high content of Yellow pixels [these will be the bee&apos;s] and use a servo controlled platform with a laser mounted [Does anyone want to be a gentleman and send me one to test with?] that uses short efficient bursts on light to cause the fly to fall into the fish tank. Have a set number of flies per day to keep the right feed requirments for the fish, and get it to turn off after it has reached its quota, reducing power consumption and increasing the number of flies ready for the next day. </p>    <p>Flash Model:</p>  <p>We only need the flies in the water long enough for the fish to eat them, is it possible that we can stun them with a bright flash [think camera flash] so they drop in the water. And let the fish choose to eat them or not. </p>        <p><strong> Waste not Want not</strong></p>  <p>It wouldn&#x2019;t be a true hacker solution unless It solved more than one problem at once, we have so far sourced a nutrient input by using flies, plugged a major nutrient leak by re using our pee but what about utilizing the waste. Aquaponics waste comes in two forms:</p>  <p>&gt;Green waste</p>  <p>All of the plant that isn&#x2019;t suited to human consumption [vine from fruiting plants, stalks etc] and the clean roots available from Aquaponics can be used as supplementary feed for animal stock like pigs/cows or directly composted. Animal waste can then be composted. </p>  <p>&gt;Carcass Waste </p>  <p>Aquaponics provide a very large source of protein to accompany the vegetables/fruit grown up top. The carcass from the fish and other farm animals can either be placed in a fly breeding chamber or composted with the green waste.</p>  <p>All of this compost will then be used to increase the nutrient, carbon and water storage properties of the soil nearby the Aquaponics system. Turning their sandy [think farming on a beach] farms slowly into the lush dark colored soil I am so used to seeing in England, taking Co2 from the atmosphere and storing it long term in the soil [Have we just helped to solve a third  worldwide problem?].</p>
