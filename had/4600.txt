                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">This is a system that uses a compressed hydrocarbon to store the energy produced by wind/solar or other renewable energy. Arc welding underwater produces a hydrocarbon equivalent to Natural Gas or Methane that can be cheaply compressed, stored and transported for use either away from the source, kept for use at the source, and "even out" the seasonal fluctuations of wind & solar. <br /><br />What I have done so far is produced the gas, compressed it, and contemplated methods to automate the process. This process produces a LOT of gas in comparison to electrolysis, and the hydrocarbon produced requires additional oxygen for the combustion mixture, which makes it far more efficient than electrolysis. The fuel can power internal combustion engines, used for home heating, electricity generation, or to power a fuel cell.  1 way around the Fracking ban in NY. <br /><br /><br /> </div>
