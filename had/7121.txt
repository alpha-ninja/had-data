                    <div class="description">Let's give life to objects !<br /><br />We looked for tiny, autonomous, easy to use, and 9 Degrees of Freedom IMU, but none of the available wireless motion sensors were affordable enough to really unlock creativity, so we built one.<br /><br />In a few simple points, here are the specs:<br />- 9 DoF with on-board sensor fusion (more explanation below)<br />- Easy to connect with BLE: good for computer + smartphone + tablet connection possible (lower power than WiFi)<br />- Compact & autonomous: Embeds a CR2032 battery holder (available in any corner shops, gas stations…)<br />- Size of a coin cell battery!<br /><br />Here is an arty example by one of our early adopters:<br /><a href='https://twitter.com/sojamo/status/611907458865471488' target="_blank">https://twitter.com/sojamo/status/611907458865471488</a><br /><br />Subscribe / Follow us for the latest newz:<br /><a href='http://Twiz.io' target="_blank">http://Twiz.io</a><br /><a href='http://twitter.com/Twiz_io' target="_blank">http://twitter.com/Twiz_io</a></div>

motion</a></li><li>Scientific studies: analyse a building/bridge vibrations, animal activities&#x2026;</li><li>2/3D Software: motion capture to animate <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qM7b1JayNMg">3D model of object</a>, human or virtual creature</li><li>Games: control your favorite doom-like from your hand, or <a href="https://vimeo.com/127490120">breakout</a></li><li>Anything that a beacon or activity tracker does</li><li>Home automation: control your room&apos;s light volume by spinning your candle:</li></ul><p><iframe width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/4jTlBb5npw0" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><br></p><h2><strong>HARDWARE</strong></h2><p><strong>MPU9150, 9 axis IMU motion sensor:</strong></p><p>3D accelerometer for linear accelerations, 3D gyroscope for rotational accelerations, 3D magnetometer for orientation reference</p><p><strong>nRF51822, processor + connectivity:</strong></p><p>32 bit ARM processor with low consumption optimization (Cortex-M0), BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy AKA Bluetooth smart/4.0)</p><p><strong>Physical characteristics :</strong></p><p>2.4 cm x 2.4 cm (&lt; 1 inch), 1.1cm thick (&lt; &#xBD; inch), 4.6g (~7.6g w/ battery)</p><p>It started with usual dev boards such as teensy, IMU and radio modules:</p><p><img src="http://www.tangibledisplay.org/wp-content/uploads/Twiz_prez1.jpg" width="278" height="208" alt="" style="width: 278px; height: 208px;"></p><p>...but we shrunk it:<br></p><p><img src="http://www.tangibledisplay.org/wp-content/uploads/Twiz_prez2.jpg" width="279" height="208" style="width: 279px; height: 208px;"></p><h2><strong><br></strong></h2><h2><strong>SOFTWARE</strong></h2><ul><li>Madwick fusion: send Euler angles or Quaternions</li><li>ifttt recipes with our python/kivy interface</li><li>experimentation using swift, noble</li><li>open source: <a href="https://upverter.com/honnet/36b930acf1b9264c">hardware</a> / <a href="https://github.com/tangibledisplay/twiz-fw">firmware</a> / <a title="github" href="http://github.com/tangibledisplay/twiz" target="_blank">software</a>:</li></ul><p><img src="http://www.tangibledisplay.org/wp-content/uploads/twiz_manager.png" width="458" height="343" style="width: 458px; height: 343px;"></p><p>Software architecture:</p><p><img src="http://www.tangibledisplay.org/wp-content/uploads/Twiz_soft_arch.png"></p>
