                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>Drones are just like old phones – with no brain. The limited hardware and the lack of a unified platform do not allow creativity and aggregating  innovation on the market. Developers have to spend most of their time on basic drone functionality and thus they  never have the ability to test their new ideas.</p><p>Flyver enables developers to write their own apps as easy as they would do it for a smartphone. We provide all necessary tools, framework and an SDK for that. It is all open source.&nbsp;</p>
                                
                            </p>

                            <h2><a href="/project/1828/log/15923-serving-a-webpage-on-the-drone">Serving a webpage on the drone</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">5 months ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/1828/log/15923#discussion-list">
                                0 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>Flyver SDK allows you to serve a webpage directly from your smartphone.</p><p>To do so, you must put all your webpage files(.html, .css, .js, images) in the FlyverCore/src/main/assets/webpage/ folder(only plain files, folder structure is not supported for now) and the page will be served on the device's IP address. The files in this folder are deployed automatically with the .apk of the application</p><p>The HTTP server used for this is <a href="https://github.com/NanoHttpd/nanohttpd">NanoHTTPD</a>, which is subclassed in utils/java/co.flyver.utils/NanoHTTPDServer, which implements its serve method</p><p>Any questions? Ask in the comments below.</p><p>This article was originally posted on the <a href="https://github.com/flyver/Flyver-SDK/wiki/-10--Webpage-Server">Flyver Github</a></p><img src="http://flyver.co/wp-content/uploads/bfi_thumb/flyver_04-m3gpueqrum9bsfc44mk2i8tra4wziqqzryxuyp3cmk.jpg">
