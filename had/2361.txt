                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Modified USB Hi Power module to include an inverter providing -3.3V rail for ECG and similar analog bio-sensors.  Further improved with new logic for i2c management and OTG.</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>This board is part of Fyber Labs <a href="http://hackaday.io/project/2236-Flex-Modules">Flex Module</a> project.</p><p>The  current design is a TPS62086 driven from a USB 2.0 micro A/B connector.   USB lines are filtered through a <span class="component-content">ECMF02-2AMX6           </span><span class="redactor-invisible-space">and <span class="component-content">TPD1E05U06DPYT            </span></span> for ESD and a <span class="component-content">TPD1S514-1YZR            </span>for high voltage  supression.   The data lines are further processed by a  <span class="component-content">TSU6721YFPR</span> which will run BCD 1.2 negotiations and communicates over i2c. The power of the TPS62086 is driven from USB VBUS or VBAT from an external battery source.  The <span class="component-content">TPS2115ADRBRG4            </span><span class="redactor-invisible-space">decides which power input to select and will also power the new negative 300mA SMPS <span class="component-content">LMR70503TM/NOPB            </span> (replacing the -3.3V charge pump).</span></p><p>Provides standard USB supplied +5V, +3.3V, GND, -3.3V, USB D+/- , ID.  </p><p>OTG is supported directly with the <span class="component-content">MAX3353EEBP-T            </span>charge pump.  This optional component can negotiate OTG with an OTG supporting microcontroller or by itself controlled over i2c.</p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>

I need to research D+/D- orientation.  It may require changing out the USB connector and/or the TSU6721.  The power supplies run well, I just need to pick a solution that doesn't get NRND'ed the second I finish the board.</p>
