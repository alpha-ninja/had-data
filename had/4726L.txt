                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-18451"><p>Long due update. </p><p>The Filament extruder I had designed and built was not completely up to the task of extruding nylon 6/6 at first I was not able to acquire reliable or consistent.</p><p>The solution attempted will be to rework the nozzle and try a different morphology that is concave after the break filter and to move to a 80 mesh filter brass.  The nozzle is a solid brass 3/4" plug drilled with a 5/8 inch drill bit to a depth of 10mm into the plug.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/8854681432541849127.JPG"></p><p>In addition the feed ramp has been revamped and is now printing it looks like so </p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/5556951432541971394.jpg"></p><p>And here it is printing&gt;</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7298041432542037244.JPG"></p><p>I am printing in Nylon for its strength and will probably print it in multiple parts as it was peeling off the bed and I would rather not bend another e3d heatbreak because they are 17 dollars and I only have one spare.  Nylon needs to be printed in an enclosed printer as it shrinks after being extruded or you can just print the sides out and plastic weld everything together.</p><p>Pictures of the extruder hopefully working tommorow.</p></div>

                            <small class="authorship">3 months ago  &bull;

                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-18475"><p>Ok so I didn't get as far as I would have liked today due to the pellet hopper not printing as I would have liked it the second or third time,  Ideally you need a heated build chamber for your 3d printer kept above the glass transistion for nylon IE 50 degrees C for most nylons. Or you need a garolite board to print on, I have neither so I increased the brim by alot and used alot of hairspray and the part I am getting now is semi acceptable, and it will work. </p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4057851432610621964.JPG"></p><p>Its not nearly as beautiful as most of my prints because I am overextruding for strength because I do not want to have to print this again.  </p><p>Here is the extruder on its last and hopefully final rebuild.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/8674441432610743699.JPG"></p><p>Those two extra fans will be going in tommorow to attempt to cool the nylon as quickly as possible.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/5312551432610814230.JPG"></p><p>Here you can see the PID and relay for regulating hotend temperature.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7387051432610891339.JPG"></p><p>Here is the auger and motor with bearing, which will drive pellets fed by the hopper down into the hotend.  I am printing the riser for the hopper now.</p></div>
