                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-20137"><p>Before designing the actual contraption to hold the toilet paper roll, it is important to study the anatomy of a toilet paper roll. As we all probably know, most toilet paper rolls are comprised of two parts:</p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">1. The cardboard roll in the centre, which creates the structure for holding the roll onto a mechanical contraption</p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">2. A long, soft, fluffy sheet of paper, with perforations at pre-determined intervals, rolled around the card board roll. This is the toilet paper.</p><p>Although toilet paper rolls are generally similar, there are still small dimensional differences between each brand. After a bit of research, I stumbled upon a web page <a href="http://www.irishlace.net/thisandthat/toiletpaperrolldimensions.html" target="_blank">here</a>, laid nicely into a table, which I've copied below:</p><table>

The one data point that is missing is the diameter of the hole created by the centre card board, so I had to measure one of my toilet paper rolls to get a rough idea. I also measured the other dimensions to confirm the correctness of the research results I found previously.</p><p>In summary, the dimensions of the roll we will be working with are as follows:</p><p>Inner diameter: ~39.5cm</p><p>Outer diameter: ~10cm - 13cm</p><p>Width: ~10cm - 11.5cm</p><p>My toilet paper roll falls nicely into this range, which gives enough data to work on a mechanical prototype.</p></div>
