A variant on vertical gardening, it addresses multiple problems found in current methods of food supply. For example, it would enable grocery shops to grow their own food, meaning less transportation is necessary to provide food. This approach to farming also reduces pollution by reducing the necessity for pesticides.

My variant on vertical farms will be based on modules that store the plants, water them, give them light, and monitor both their plant and their surroundings. With the addition of either workers or robotic implements, the plants can additionally be given fertilizer and monitored.

By making these containers modular, different plant sizes can be accommodated in the same rack regardless of the plant type, shifting based on current demand, while still optimizing the space needed. Having the plants flat against a wall - or slightly protruding from it - simplifies the use of movable racks.">

                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">This is a project aiming to simplify the process of growing plants anywhere. A variant on vertical gardening, it addresses multiple problems found in current methods of food supply. For example, it would enable grocery shops to grow their own food, meaning less transportation is necessary to provide food. This approach to farming also reduces pollution by reducing the necessity for pesticides.<br /><br />My variant on vertical farms will be based on modules that store the plants, water them, give them light, and monitor both their plant and their surroundings. With the addition of either workers or robotic implements, the plants can additionally be given fertilizer and monitored.<br /><br />By making these containers modular, different plant sizes can be accommodated in the same rack regardless of the plant type, shifting based on current demand, while still optimizing the space needed. Having the plants flat against a wall - or slightly protruding from it - simplifies the use of movable racks.</div>

                            <p><h1>System Design Document</h1><p>I&apos;m building toward the following:<br></p><ul><li>Modules with grow light strips, solenoid valves, docks.<br>Design finished, construction will start soon.</li><li>Communication between Modules, Controllers, and Servers.<ul><li>Communication between Modules and Controllers done, Controller-Server communication is currently in progress.</li></ul></li><li>A graphical interface that shows the status of the system.<ul><li>Mockup has been made, coding is in progress.</li></ul></li><li>Temperature sensors in controllers<ul><li>Work hasn&apos;t started on this.</li></ul></li></ul><p>Please see the components section for a preliminary list of components.</p><h1><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/776561439767900210.png">Information:</h1><p>This is a project aiming to:</p><ul><li>Enable growing food at sale locations (&#x2192; supermarkets, etc)<ul><li>Aims:<br>Reduce pollution caused by transportation</li></ul></li><li>Enable automation of growing food<ul><li>Aims:<br>Permit use of analytics to maximize growing efficiency</li></ul></li><li>Facilitate use of robots in plant growing process<ul><li>Aims:<br>Reduce needed space<br>Achieve consistent quality</li></ul></li><li>Reduce wasted water by eliminating runoff problems and reducing evapotranspiration losses by means of catching runoff and dehumidifying, then filtering water and reusing it.<ul><li>Aims:<br>Use &amp; waste less water.</li></ul></li><li>Accommodate plants of different sizes.<ul><li>Aims:<br>Allow use of a single frame to grow different plants (especially useful in smaller commercial contexts)</li></ul></li></ul><p>In conclusion, these are the major goals I am trying to achieve.</p><ul><li>Grow food where it&apos;s needed (at local supermarkets, etc...)<ul><li>Give customers access to fresher food<ul><li>Improve health by reducing the cost of vegetables &amp; fruit in general and making them a more attractive option</li></ul></li><li>Simplify growing of food in remote regions, especially those that have arid climates</li></ul></li><li>Reduce the environmental impact of growing plants, especially in large scale<ul><li>Use less water<ul><li>Stop releasing pesticides / herbicides into groundwater &amp; nature</li></ul></li></ul></li></ul><h1>Licenses</h1><p>Wire library (c) 2006 Nicholas Zambetti, GNU LGPL 2.1+. Not modified by me.</p><p>Other Arduino.cc Software used (GPL and/or LGPL), but also not modified by me.<br></p>

                            <p><p>To begin with, I'm sorry for not posting in a long time—I hope I will be able to make a lot of updates over the next few days. Regardless, this won't be a long project log.</p><hr><p>I've decided to use the Teensy-LC I got as a prize a few weeks back as the section controller.</p><p>The main reason is because of its USB capabilities. In fact, it turns out it's <a href="https://www.pjrc.com/teensy/rawhid.html">relatively</a> easy to use as a raw HID, meaning that sending 64-byte packets between any computer and the teensy is a breeze.</p><p>It also has a relatively high clock speed, so that's great.</p><p>I'll be doing some testing &amp; coding for this over the next few days and will keep you updated.</p><p>Goals for software: (I'll most likely be using python)</p><ul><li>Figure out some kind of protocol</li><li>Get that protocol implemented<ul><li>Both controller and computer-side<ul><li>Run a simulation on the teensy for testing</li></ul></li></ul></li><li>Get some basic GUI going computer-side</li></ul><p>I will eventually get a π set up, connected to the teensy, connected to a few modules.<br></p>
