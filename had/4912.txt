                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Human urine is a great source of nitrogen, phosphorous, potassium and trace elements for plants. Not only that, we all have a constant, year-round supply of it - and it's free!  Urine has electrolytes "It's got what plants crave."<br /><br />I'll show you how to create a system that can accept grey water from showers, dish washing, and urine, in order to grow massive plants easily without worrying about over/under watering or fertilizing. <br /><br />For now I will have to use 3d models to show how it works, my equipment is at the Cabin which is still unreachable due to drifted snow.  I will upload pictures of the actual Tupperware boxes when I can reach them.  <br /><br />Example of yeild from commercial earth box <a href='http://amazingtomatoes.com/earthbox/tomatoes-in-earthboxes-a-readers-perspective/' target="_blank">http://amazingtomatoes.com/earthbox/tomatoes-in-earthboxes-a-readers-perspective/</a> my boxes actually grow larger and thicker plants.<br /><br />Math example for how much fertilizing you can do with just human urine <a href='http://farmerscrub.blogspot.com/2007/12/nitrogen-fixers-vs-liquid-gold.html' target="_blank">http://farmerscrub.blogspot.com/2007/12/nitrogen-fixers-vs-liquid-gold.html</a></div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p><iframe width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/vXCndFy9RN4" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe>You will need two rubber made or similar containers for this project,  one must be able to sit upside down inside the other.  You will also need a length of PVC pipe and various drills.</p><p>You will also need some good gardening dirt with some perlite, vermiculite, or coco-coir added to lighten the soil and allow more air to infiltrate it.  Oxygen grows healthy roots and roots are the most important part of your plants.  You have to get oxygen to your roots while maintaining a moisture level conducive to growth.</p><p>Drill a bunch of small holes in the smaller containers bottom these holes will allow oxygen to permeate the soil and allow the roots to reach through to the water reservoir below. Drill the holes small enough that the dirt will not fall through into your water resevoir.</p><p>Drill a hole in the side of the larger container just big enough for the PVC pipe to fit into.  Stick the PVC into the larger container and with the smaller container on the inside mark where the PVC will insert into it, Drill a hole in the smaller container so the PVC can slide through it.  Make two more holes so the PVC can slide all the way through your growing box.</p><p>Drill some holes through the PVC pipe so water and air can enter the center of the growing box.  This will also allow excess water to drain out to more boxes or onto the ground.</p><p>Next fill the space around the smaller container that is upside down in the larger box and has the PVC pipe inserted into it with some pea gravel.  The pea gravel should fill up to around 2 inches below the PVC pipe.  Then fill the rest of the space with your gardening medium.  </p><p>Plant your plants in the top of the box.  DO NOT WATER FROM THE TOP!!!! This will compact the soil and ruin all your hard work.  Only water into the PVC pipe as the dirt that is touching the top of the water will wick moisture to your plants roots.  You will generally want to take the cover that came with the larger container and cut some holes into it to just fit your plants,  this way you can keep any rain that may fall on the boxes from compacting your soil.  </p><p>The best part of this growing system is that it eliminates the guess work as far as when to water and how much.  Until your plants get huge you can also leave them for a month or so and they will continue to thrive as the water resevoir will continue to provide what your plants need.</p><p>You will find that near the end of your growing season you will need to stake your plants and provide support to the box. With the easy access to nutrients and oxygen for the roots, the plants will get bigger than you can even imagine.  </p>
                                
                            </p>
