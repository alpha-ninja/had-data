                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Are you tired of classifying your waste youself? Or do you want to know how much waste you produce everyday? Dusty is the solution to all your problems! Dusty is designed to solve our world's garbage pollution problem and is an all in one solution incorporating several aspects as listed below. Dusty is able to classify recyclables from non recyclables and separate cans,bottles,paper and trash preventing cross contamination between recyclables and non-recyclables and speed the recycling process. By implementing sensors and an interface into Dusty, it can perform waste monitoring so we can analyse our daily waste output and change our lifestyles to become more waste efficient and helping the environment in the process. Dusty can also perform networked garbage collection allow Dusty to redirect you to another Dusty in the vicinity if it is full. This makes Dusty the world's first smart dustbin which is aware of its surroundings and can react to situations like being full.</div>

                            <p><h2>The Problem</h2><p>Dusty is combating a serious problem in the world right now. All over the world,we can see landfills filled to the brim with waste that we produce. These waste will be left there over decades to slowly decompose polluting our environment with these pollutants. As seen from the picture below, our waste has been left to accumulate for all these years and has a severe impact on the environment up. We need to solve this problem now!</p><p><img src="http://personal.psu.edu/mrs33/eled/WQ_pollution_files/image355.jpg" style="width: 235px; height: 152.058823529412px;"></p><p>Studies show that America's waste consists of 40.4% paper, 17.6% yard trimmings, 8.5% metals, 8.0% plastic, 7.4% food scraps, 7.0% glass and 11.6% other waste.In total, around 63.0% of our waste can be recycled and 25% can be decomposed into compost. If this waste is properly disposed of, we will reduce the amount of waste in our landfills by 88%. This is a very significant percentage of our waste and I believe that if we start now,we can fix this problem.</p><p><img src="http://www.epa.gov/osw/wycd/catbook/images/what.gif" alt=" a pie chart showing percentages of trash" style="width: 276px; height: 176.068965517241px;"></p><hr><h2>                                                    The Solution</h2><p>Dusty is an all in one solution for this problem.By implementing three main functionalities into the dustbin,we can effectively combat the problem at hand. The first main functionality is that it is able to differentiate between different types of recyclable or non-recyclable waste. The main four categories of recyclable waste we will be targeting is paper, metal, plastic and glass. To differentiate between that various items mentioned above, Dusty will first have to have different sections inside itself so that it can be an all in one solution. The ratio of the space allocated each of the materials is based on the statistics above. The division of Dusty can be seen below.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/822101428533085745.png"></p><p>All of the sections shown here except for the inorganic and organic section will be covered by a separate lid operated via servos to open and close them and restrict access to them so that cross contamination of materials does not occur as that can lower the efficiency of the bin. Now, how will Dusty detect the rubbish to open the right lid.If the user is trying to dispose organic or non-recyclable waste,he can proceed as the inorganic and organic  section is always open. If the user wants to dispose one of the recyclable materials as shown in the diagram colored in light green, he needs to tap the material on a sensor as shown below. This sensor is none other than a sound sensor which detects the sound given off by the material when it is tapped on it and opens the correct lid for disposal. This idea is the most energy efficient idea to classify recyclables and is slightly adapted from a team who made it for a hackathon. While that team uses this to identify recyclables from non-recyclables, I have polished up the idea and used it to classify waste within recyclables.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7240391428534491050.png"></p>That takes care of most of the autonomous material classification in Dusty. Do keep your eyes peeled for more updates on it in my project log as I may change several things in the process of making it. Next let's look at the waste monitoring aspect of Dusty. Dusty will use a combination of IR sensors and Force sensors to determine the weight and number of waste we insert into each of the sections above. This allows to to effectively carry out waste monitoring and display the results either on an application on a nearby device such as a smartphone or a onboard screen. The interfacing of all the sensors and waste monitoring software will be on a raspberry pi which is the brains of Dusty. The Raspberry Pi will consolidate all the sensor values and manipulate it in different manners to get a effective waste monitoring solution. The exact way to display the waste monitoring information will be decided later along the project's development. The Raspberry Pi will also probably handle the opening and closing of the lids though I might need to use a separate microcontroller for that since the Pi is a microprocessor.                             <h2><a href="/project/5152/log/17174-design-overhaul">Design Overhaul</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">4 months ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5152/log/17174#discussion-list">
                                0 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>After some consideration, I have decided that I will build a foam prototype of Dusty to kickstart things off. The prototype will use an Arduino Uno as its primary controller and a bluetooth module. This is where the LightBlue Bean might come in handy.The design has also been changed dramatically. Instead of using a trapdoor mechanism with servos to pull up lids,  I have simplified the system to use just one motor. This works by dividing a circular bin into sectors with each sector corresponding to a certain material. Then we can use a motor to spin a lid which is on a bin and this lid only has one sector exposed. When we spin this lid with a motor, it we open only one sector of the bin at anytime allowing us to accomplish the classification using just one motor.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6865721430440952030.jpg" style="width: 534px; height: 221.890410958904px;"></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">Electronics wise, I will be using an arduino Uno connected to a custom shield which drives the single motor. The bluetooth module will be connected to the arduino and I will be making an app for android phones to interface with Dusty. The app should allow me to monitor waste using several sensors connected to the arduino as well such as a laser gate and force sensor. This should give me a reasonable working prototype that has basic functionality.</p>

                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/5152/log/16286-raspberry-pi-vs-intel-edison">Raspberry Pi vs Intel Edison</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">4 months ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5152/log/16286#discussion-list">
                                0 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>Today, I will be comparing the Raspberry Pi against the Intel Edison and figuring out what is more appropriate for Dusty to use. The Raspberry Pi is an common microprocessor used widely in education and DIY communities.The intel Edison is an newly released board which is specifically designed for the Internet of Things (Iot).</p><p><img src="http://img.pandawhale.com/post-50521-How-Intel-s-Edison-Stacks-Up-A-NjGr.png"></p><p>Looking at the table above,we can see that the Edicon is more powerful than both the Raspberry Pi B+ and the Arduino Uno. It also comes with both Bluetooth 4.0 and Dual Band Wifi on the board itself. However, the Edison does not have a display output unlike the Raspberry Pi. This means that we cannot have any on-board display on Dusty itself and all the interfacing with Dusty will be through an external device connected to the internet. This could be through an app or an webpage. This could be quite more difficult to use that the simple on screen instructions that the Raspberry Pi can produce on an on-board display. However, the Raspberry Pi has its own share of disadvantages involved. It is quite power-hungry and on of the primary aims of Dusty is to have a long enough battery life. Assuming that the garbage collection happens once a week, the battery on board Dusty needs to have a minimum battery life of at least a week. The Intel Edison however has a very low power consumption and is also more compact to occupy less space in Dusty although there needs to be additions of custom breakout boards which will be designed by me. Furthermore,since the Intel Edison has an on-board wifi and Bluetooth, it can connect to any external peripherals extremely easily. There is also no need to have any ethernet cables to connect Dusty to the internet as it can connect to any near wifi hotspots. However,in remote locations, ethernet cables will be necessary but in populated areas this should not be a problem. I think that comparing the two of them,the intel Edison will be a better choice for Dusty. What do you think? I would greatly appreciate it if you could leave some advice below.Thanks!</p>
                                
                            </p>
