                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">A solar powered LED light to keep you, your kids or pets, visible and safe at night. It has a really lame name at the moment but I can't think of anything else, suggestions welcome!</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>A friend of mine recently took on a massive 52 mile walk over 24 hours and since part of it was in the dark he wore a head torch. I thought about how people wear reflective strips so that cars can see them but they were not wearing anything else to keep them visible.</p><p>I thought it would be cool to build a cheap and long-lasting solar powered light to keep the wearer visible at night.</p><p>The project took inspiration from cheap garden solar lights but toned down the need for bight illumination and focussed instead on low level light that can last a very long time on a small solar charge. </p><p>The project is currently working in the 0.6ma range from 2 rechargeable AA batteries, the lower the voltage the lower the current draw. This should give an extremely long life to the batteries on a minimal charge from the sun.</p><p><strong>Project overview (2 minute video):</strong></p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/cZt3z_feiaw" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p><strong>My first prototype:</strong></p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/gX8Z3Q3lYLE" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p><strong>Initial experimentation:</strong></p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/pxBz30tfa_s" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe>
