                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">The Pulsar is a wearable electronic device that allows you to wirelessly control bright, colorful LEDs from any computer or mobile!  It includes a rechargeable, long-lasting, pocket-sized battery, a tiny microcontroller with Bluetooth LE, a real-time clock, and 24 smart, multi-colored (RGB) LEDs.</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>To be honest, we didn&apos;t design the <strong>Pulsar</strong> with a specific use in mind.  We simply wanted a way to include multi-colored lights in our projects without too much hassle, and be able to control them wirelessly.</p><p>To give you a taste for what we&apos;ve built so far, here are a few examples:</p><h2>Iron Man Costume</h2><p>From Comic-Con to Maker Faire, the <strong>Pulsar</strong> allows us to build costumes of varying degrees of complexity.  An Iron Man t-shirt works great when you&apos;re in a pinch for a costume party!</p><p>The <strong>Pulsar</strong> also makes a great addition to our 3d-printed repulsor arm! Check out the CAD model files courtesy of our neighbors at <a data-mention-user-id="19629" href="/hacker/19629">@Deezmaker</a>!</p><h2>International Space Station (ISS) Monitor</h2><p>In collaboration with the creator of <a href="https://hackaday.io/project/3201-iss-above-hd" target="_blank">ISS Above</a> (<a data-mention-user-id="21245" href="/hacker/21245">@Liam Kennedy</a>), we developed custom firmware so that the <strong>Pulsar</strong> would display a countdown until the next pass of the ISS using beautiful LED animations!</p><p>The data are loaded wirelessly onto the <strong>Pulsar</strong> from a Raspberry Pi equipped with a USB Bluetooth LED adapter! </p><h2>3D-Printed Night Lamp</h2><p>In collaboration with our awesome neighbors over at <a data-mention-user-id="19629" href="/hacker/19629">@Deezmaker</a> 3D-Printers we developed a 3d-printed night lamp that can be programmed as a soundless alarm clock from any bluetooth-capable device!</p><p>We&apos;re excited to see what you&apos;ll create using this neat little device!</p>
