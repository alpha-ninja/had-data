                            <small class="authorship">5 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4603/log/15816#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-15816"><p>I ran into issues with the targeted Inverter, have to switch to a 74HCT3G04DP,125 to support TTL out of the WS2811.</p><p>I am also developed another design that eliminates the inverter and uses 3 4.7K pull-up resistors along with 3 2N3904 transistors.  The WS2811 is capable of directly driving the PT4115 but the signals would be reversed making sequencing much more difficult</p><p>The choice is how comfortable are you with SMD soldering of the TSS0P8 form factor at .65 pitch.  Plus the transistor version is a few cents cheaper for the BOM depending on part source.  The 2N3904 can run as cheap as $0.02 each vs the $0.53 single inverter.  Throw in the resistors run the total to .09 vs .53.</p><p>I have PCB available for both versions.</p></div>
                            </div>
