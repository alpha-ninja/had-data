                            <h2><a href="/project/5200/log/16555-cv">CV</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">4 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5200/log/16555#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-16555"><p>We are finishing up on the CV part of our project. We were using some really complicated matrix algebra, but we are now trying out color recognition. We've painted the needle on the pick head, and from that we are able to find the needle. We then enclose that area in a rectangle and say we are interested in the bottom middle part of the rectangle. This gives us the tip of the needle where we will be picking up parts. </p><p>We do the same thing to the board we are placing parts on, and the parts we are placing. Nothing is painted here. We recognize the area the part will be place, and the part before we pick it up. Once the part is over the area it gets placed we correct for any error caused by rotating and the part is then placed.</p><p>So far this route has been a lot less complicated, but we will update after some testing and let you see the results!<br></p></div>
                            </div>

                            <small class="authorship">3 months ago  &bull;

                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-17505"><p>We covered Auto Tool Changing in the last video. In that video we were using new cameras, and unfortunately once we got those new camera attached we discovered that the new cameras would crash into the tool changing wells. Because of this we had to leave every other well empty, so the camera had somewhere to go. After a few days of planning how to fix this problem someone sat up and said, "can't we do this", and with the turn of 2 screws the problem was fixed. Aren't those 10 second fixes the best! </p><p>Now that we have resolved that issue I have made a new video showing the head changing in all active wells. </p><p>You may notice in this video that the head goes over to the PCB and touches it. This is to set the Z axis 0. This is needed because the length of the tools vary. Enjoy!</p><p><iframe src="//www.youtube.com/embed/syRGDpmOsxs" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" width="500"></iframe></p></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/5200/log/18297-speed-testing-and-breakthroughs">Speed testing and breakthroughs!</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">3 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5200/log/18297#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-18297"><p>Hey everyone! Yesterday was a pretty exciting day. We made a few tweaks, and were able to get our speed up considerably. Currently we are close to 1,000 CPH, but in the next few weeks we are very confident that we can increase this number. A few things that we hope will help us get there are: variable (lower) safe heights, new screw for Z-axis (currently using all thread), 24V power supply (currently using 12V). </p><p>The machine is really awesome to watch at its current speed. We are really excited about how far we've come in such a short time. I will try to make a video tomorrow showing the new speed of the machine if I can remember to bring in my camera. </p><p>Until then we are working as fast as we can to get IC placements down. This has been challenging to do with our camera set up; we are working on doing it without an upward facing camera. We are sure it can be done, and we think we are getting close. I hope to have something to show you all on the IC's soon. Our goal is to have IC placements down to a science by the time of the Cleavland Mini Maker Faire on June 6th. We were just invited to show our machine there! If you are in the Cleavland Ohio area come check us out!<br></p></div>
