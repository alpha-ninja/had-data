                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>Is it possible to build a production electric passenger car in under a year.?  </p><p>Here are the key ingredients.</p><p>Design Bodywork&gt;</p><p>Too expensive to hire <span class="st">Pininfarina</span>.  That would cost millions...  So, we will take a pretty car from the world of gaming, with full files in .3DS &amp; try to convert the file to Solid works....3ds designs usually cost about USD100 to buy.</p><p>Design Chassis. &gt;</p><p>There is plenty of information about street legal chassis available.  Lets take the basic info &amp; design  chassis to fit the body.</p><p>Keep it Simple &gt;</p><p>It has to be all electric &amp; let add a twist &amp; use in wheel hub motors.. This means we do not need many complex parts &amp; we can keep the weight low &amp; reduce complexity.</p><p>Keep it Light &gt;</p><p>Weight is critical !.  Power requires has a direct relationship to weight.  Less weight = less power required = less batteries &amp; smaller motors = LESS WEIGHT.  A virtuous circle.</p><p>Deign for mass production &gt;</p><p>If any process can not be done in mass production, do not do it in the prototype. </p><p>Stick by the project aims &gt;</p><p>No deviation from the stated project aims.<br></p>
                                
                            </p>

                            <h2><a href="/project/5066/log/22495-august-2015">August 2015</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">7 days ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5066/log/22495#discussion-list">
                                6 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>This project log is just to say we are still working on the project &amp; recap where we are &amp; what we have to do next...   Our main focus is now on LUKA MK II. </p><p>LUKA 1 has very much done what it was supposed to do.  It gave us proof of concept.  The original project aims were a top speed of 130km/h.  Luka has a top speed of 134km/h with just 2 motors.</p><p>The second aim was for the wight to be under 750kg.  Luka is 721kg.  Even with 4 motors, LUKA II will be well under 750kg because we have made various changes that save weight in Version II.</p><p>EU certification by Sept 2015.  We got certified months ago so that box is ticked too..</p><p>Range of 300km on a single charge.  This Luka definitely goes 300km on a single charge.  But, range is like a piece of string.  There are a multitude of variables.  Huge compromises need to be made to make the car go 300km.  Driving normally with a mix of highways &amp; usual traffic in towns or cities, I think a realistic range is 250km with the 19.2kwh bat pack.  With range, we also need to look at price.  In physical terms, we can fit a bat pack of up to 60kwh in the car &amp; that would obviously have a very long range.  But, as a ballpark number, each additional 100km range will add about EUR3K to the price of the car.  </p><p>Retail price.  Under EUR20K.</p><p>There was confusion about this target.  People seemed to think this would be the price for a &apos;kit&apos; that people have to build themselves &amp; get certified themselves.  What we meant was that the price would be for a complete car, certified &amp; ready to drive.  The only caveat was that this price would be ex works our workshop (Czech republic) so it would cost some money to get it to an end customer... Without doubt, we could sell a complete car configured with the 19.2kwh bat pack &amp; 4 hub motors for under EUR20K &amp; make a profit...(if anyone wants one, let me know !)</p><p>So, we can say we met all the project aims.  We did not expect to.  We expected something to be a show stopper.  But, just because we met the aims does not mean we built a good car.  Unfortunately, the car does not pass the &apos;wife&apos; test.  My wife thinks it looks lovely but she will not drive it because it is too &quot;different&apos;.  It has no noise insulation, this makes driving much more &apos;real&apos;.  You hear the suspension, the brakes, the road noise..  Our brakes are &apos;rally car&apos; brakes (apply a lot of force &amp; they work well).  Normal folk are used to something very different.  We do not need power steering because the car is so light but normal people are not used to this type of steering.  The car does how have a proper interior...  Even &apos;one pedal driving&apos; is weird to people.  Because regen braking starts once you take your foot off the accelerator you usually do not need a brake..This sort of freaks some people out..  &amp;, even for me, it is just too hot to drive any car what does not have proper air conditioning... Here in Czech (not an especially hot country), we hit 40 degrees last week.  This week is high 30s.  A car without proper air con is useless... </p><p>We also learned that open sourcing a project is hard.  The key components for Luka II are not the same as those for Luka I &amp; they are not the same as our original 3D drawings.  All the millimeter changes have been done &apos;as we build&apos; the new chassis &amp; as we made small changes to the body.  So, we have a situation where our tooling will not match our 3D plans. This is far from ideal if our hope is that others can just download things &amp; make their own version of the car..  It has also become clear that even if people could download all the plans, they would still need a huge amount of (various) skills to have any chance of building a car themselves.  Making the chassis would be easy enough, the body almost impossible.  Even if we had a wonderful wiring diagram, only very experienced folk would have any chance of following it.....&amp; then there are all the other systems like brakes, suspension,...</p>

                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/5066/log/20004-novel-charging-concept">Novel Charging concept</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">2 months ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5066/log/20004#discussion-list">
                                27 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>On board chargers are expensive.  They are also slow.  As EV fast charge infrastructure is still crap we are definitely maintaining our 'plug into any socket' capability.  Plugging into any socket should allow people drive to work &amp; back &amp; plug in at both places.  A least at home, getting a 3 phase socket installed will not be a big deal.</p><p>A 3.6kw charger seems to cost about USD1K.  An 8kw charger is more like USD2K.  This is big money for a car supposed to retail for USD22K.  The little 2kw one we have on board now cost about USD500..</p><p>So, we set about thinking.  In fact, we already have 2 x 8KW chargers on the car.  Each of our hub motors is, in effect, an electric generator.  Plug these into the mains, turn the motor controllers to 'regenerate" &amp; hey presto, we have 2 x 'free' 8KW chargers.  It means we could remove the crappy 2kw charger we have now, saving about 25kilos in weight &amp; we can save loads of  money!.  </p><p>If plugged into a normal socket, no matter what charger we have, we will be limited to 16A/230V so about 3.7kw.  With a 20kw bat pack, this means about 5 hours charging.  But, with 3 phase, these limitation do not apply.  I think it gives us 3 x 25a x 230v = so 17kw..  3 phase 'should' allow for us to use 2 x 8kw motors as on board generators.  A 16kw charger would fully charge us in just over an hour. </p><p>This idea is only in the formative stage.  We think this might be another word first.  We do not believe anything like this has been done before.  The solution is probably unique to cars driven by hub motors.  We hope to start testing next week.  We will need to play with the motor controllers a little.  It is unlikely they will support this idea without substantial tampering.  There is every possibility lots of things can get fried as we test the idea.  But, wow, it is such a cool idea what frying some electronics would be more than worthwhile if we could actually make it work.   The charger represents a serious % of the overall cost of the vehicle &amp; if we could remove that cost completely &amp; get close to 1 hour charging it would represent a major breakthrough for LUKA.   Obviously, sharing an early concept like this with no testing means people can rip us apart as we might be totally wrong !.  But, the LUKA project is all about putting it out there with no bells or whistles so lets see what happens..  </p><p>In other developments, we did a lot of work on the future interior of the car.  We  also re-calibrated the BMS.  It is now very conservative.  It is set do it should show 'empty' when we can still dive about 30km. This week, we set a new top speed of 118.5km/h.  On a 130km journey, the BMS showed that we used 60% of the battery (our SOC 40%).  This 130km drive was done with no attempt to be economical &amp; with regeneration not functioning correctly. We will now state with confidence that the 'real world' range of the LUKA EV (using its smaller battery pack) is 250km.  We are confident our target range of 300km will be reached with the bigger pack (40 cells x 3.2V, 200ah). this bigger pack is just one extra set of 4 cells in the back &amp; in the front. </p><p>we hope to do work on suspension next week &amp; possibly do a video tour of the car  next Friday.<br></p>
                                
                            </p>

                            <h2><a href="/project/5066/log/19361-luka-is-registered">LUKA is registered !</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">2 months ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5066/log/19361#discussion-list">
                                33 comments
                            </a>

                            <p><p>15th June 2015.</p><p>Unfortunately, we had to withdraw from the WAVE &amp; bring the car back to Czech.  The car is really low to the ground.  The night before we left, we welded metal plates below the battery pack. These were about 5cm below the chassis of the car.  This reduced our road clearance from 15cm to 10cm... 10cm road clearance was just jot enough !.  While driving on motorways &amp; 'A' roads was OK, we could not get through towns well.  We could not get the car over simple objects like speed ramps.  If we had taken it to an underground car park, we never would have got out again!..  It is a very simple thing to fix but it is something that must be fixed at home, not on a European road trip..  Here we are at the start of the WAVE.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3842571434359612824.jpg"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3254141434359724834.jpg"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/981031434359840600.jpg"></p><p>&amp; here are the official videos of Day 1 &amp; day 2 of the wave.</p><p><iframe src="//www.youtube.com/embed/w-AJRp7ASnE" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" width="500"></iframe></p><p><iframe src="//www.youtube.com/embed/V8ZOQULOa9A" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" width="500"></iframe>The general public love this car!..  Everyone presumed that it was an old English or Italian sports car that we had converted.  It was difficult to explain to everyone that this is a new design, build at home from 'nothing'.  There was much head scratching going on as no one could find the engine !  The looked in the front, the looked in the rear but could see no engine.  Anyone who was knowledgeable about cars was shocked that 2 x 8kw motors hub motors could power a car.  They said things like 'so it only drives 10km per hour?'.... They simply did not believe that it could be possible to do highway driving with such low power...</p><p>The nicest thing was that the car made everyone smile !.  Cars on motorways drove beside us to wave at us.!</p><p>The overall performance of the car was amazing.  We had no range problems.  The car actually seems to go forever &amp; break the laws of physics.  This test car has the smaller 19.2Kwh battery pack.  We were driving relatively fast.  We had 2 heavy men in the car &amp; another 50kilos of luggage &amp; 'stuff'.  Based on day 1 (getting to the wave) &amp; day 2 (going Plauen-Zwickau-Chemnitz-Leipzig) the range of the car might be 400km?.. The BMS could be lying to us but we do not think so as our Voltage suggests the BMS is telling the truth.   But, over the coming weeks, we will do range &amp; speed tests.  Testing will be easy now that we can legally drive it on any road in Europe..</p><p>We were stopped by the police in Germany !.  We got a full interrogation ! They checked all our paperwork, the chassis numbers etc.  They wanted to see the engine number &amp; did not really know what to do when there was no engine in the engine bay !.  It is rare to be stopped by the German police &amp; for them not to be able to find 'something' so they can give you a penalty..  They just told us everything was in order..  I think we even saw then smiling at the car...  </p><p>The WAVE is NOT a holiday !.  It is all about schedules &amp; time keeping.  Each car has to arrive to each location at a specific time (like 0922hrs).  You must leave at a specific time like 0946hrs.  The organizers get angry if you are a minute late !.  The people are really nice, very helpful.  We stopped outside one town as we noticed our hazard lights were not working (Again).  Every wave car that passed us by stopped to ask if they could help us..(it took us about 20 minutes to identify &amp; fix the second problem with the hazard lights)   We will definitely take a 'real' LUKA EV on the wave next year....</p><p>We are pretty disappointed we had to withdraw so early.  We really wanted to do the Berlin to Bern stage (1100km).  With the apparent range of our car, we would have easily done this stage in the allotted time.  We would have loved to take the car up the ALPS..  This really would have tested the climbing ability of hub motors.  </p><p>On one hand, we are sad because the problem that stopped us had nothing to do with our technology or concept.  We just set the ride on the car far too low.  It was quite a basic error.  On the other hand, we are delighted that it was not a case that the hub motors failed, the motor controllers blew up or the battery pack only giving us a range of 20km..  The thing that...</p>

                    <p>Yes this could work but I would be concerned with the pump turning on too much and wasting power. The unit does specify a vacuum switch but with any sort of leakage the pump will operate a lot more often than really needed and it is hard to get a perfect vacuum connection. I can't think of any units off the top of my head but there is electrically assisted brakes that use no vacuum. I'm not sure how they are in terms of pricing but efficiency wise they should be much better since you are not converting electric to vacuum to force on the diaphram inside the booster, you are going electric straight to brake boost. If you have a pressurized air source on board in use for something else you could use it to power the brake booster. Instead of having vacuum on one side and the atmosphere putting 14 psi of force you can create a higher "atmosphere" by connecting pressure to that side and leaving vacuum open to standard air if that makes sense. Pretty much creating an artifical pressure differential for the brake booster that mimicks the 14 psi difference between vacuum and atmosphere side.&nbsp;You can also look into&nbsp;racing brake systems, those do not use a booster but have a smaller diameter master cylinder with longer travel, I'm not sure how soft/hard they feel though. If you have any questions don't hesitate to shoot me a message. I used to build electric cars at school until work took too much time ;( but I'm more than happy to help with you project.
