                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Produces hydrogen fuel on demand by controlled chemical means automatically for small generators and pumps in devastated storm and remote areas for emergency relief. </div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>This project was conceived to provide a highly mobile portable fuel system adaptable to running small gasoline engines(single cylinder) like those found on generators, on hydrogen fuel, produced in a controlled, safe manner. One pound of calcium hydride will produce 20-28 cubic feet of gas(higher depending on grade), and run a small engine for many hours depending on engine size and load demand. A pound of dry chemical can be stored nearly anyplace and even stockpiled. You can easily carry several pounds with you without even noticing it.</p><p>The prototype has three chambers-One which holds calcium hydride, a chemical which when mixed with water(any water source) produces hydrogen gas suitable for use as a substitute for gasoline engines. The second chamber holds water-which can be seawater, non-potable water, or any found on location.</p><p>The third chamber is the reaction chamber. It is where the two ingredients are brought together in microcontroller(Microchip PIC) controlled amounts, to provide a safe usable pressure, and constant flow of gas to keep the engine running. This fuel volume is regulated to 1.5-2 psi depending on engine demand. Because it is very small(both reaction chamber and gas volume), there is little danger due to hazard from explosion, fire or danger in operation.</p><p>Calcium hydride can be shipped through normal venues. Its current cost varies depending on quantity ordered(large to get reasonable pricing), grade and mesh size, Lab grades can be very expensive as they are highly refined for accurate analysis experiments. This unit can make use of much lower grades, </p><p>Because it can be stored safely for years, even decades, it makes an excellent fuel source for preppers. Gasoline has a definite shelf life and not always available when the power is out. Propane and other gases require sturdy heavy tanks which greatly impede mobility.</p><p>This project is highly scaleable. It can be made in larger sizes for larger installations that are remote, and/or larger engines. The intentions for this projects are for the high mobility ability of the fuel, storage in preparation , and on demand production yielding maximum performance with little waste. It only produces gas when demand exists.</p><p>Engines that run on hydrogen gas run cooler, produce much less emissions(when leaned properly), use less oil, and greatly increase the time required between oil changes.</p><p>Exhaust manifold temperatures for gasoline engines are near 1200 degrees, which the hydrogen engine manifold temps measure just above 850.</p><p>The electronics for this project are "off the shelf" common components. The tank itself can be reproduced by pourable casting molds(present) for 16$ in materials, or 3D printed(abs) once STL files get made. A small emergency fuel tank for a small engine(2-4 horsepower) is typically about 10 inches square.</p><p>This entire fuel tank concept is a transparent, embedded system-the user need only switch it on and start the engine. There is no fussing. The gasoline fuel replacement adapters take care of the fuel conversion requirements(External type, or predesigned for specific engines).</p><p>The use of hydrogen fuel in small single cylinder engines is very forgiving. Unlike multi-cylinders engines(auto and other) they are tolerant in fuel adaptations, and while they do produce less horsepower output running than if running on gasoline, this can be designed around and planned for in engine selection.</p>
                                
                            </p>

                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/5917/log/18810-upgrades-to-the-binary-fuel-tank">Upgrades to the Binary fuel Tank</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">3 months ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5917/log/18810#discussion-list">
                                0 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>Thanks to the OSH park boards I won during the weekly contests, I was able to redesign some circuitry for the fuel tank controller and really provide a great upgrade in respect to safety measures, monitoring and fuel production. The LCD menu display is also making the unit much more user friendly. Here is a look at the new system module:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/2750891433206924444.jpg"></p><p>It can also be noted I got all the closure panels on the top of the big yellow box now!</p><p> The new module is the user interface that allows the pressure and flowrate to be programmed in for a specific engine being used(in this case my lawnmower engine/generator) and only requires the unit to be turned on and the engine started. Before, the pressure had to be set each time the tank was turned on and fiddled with a bit.</p><p> Thanks to the PIC controller and lots of crazy coding I was able to make it simple enough for anyone to use, which would be desirable for an end product. I chose the PIC for this(and almost ALL my other projects) as they are stable, reliable and have a ton of pins that can do magic. They have never failed me and for this project, the choice was a no brainer.</p><p>The tank controller has a stepped menu for the two line display which allows changes for other apps, so it's a "one size fits all" thing now. There is also a manual over ride to still allow the use of the manual system in case an unknown or previously unused engine type is used, and of course in this case for experimenting.</p><p> Once the system is up and running the LCD scroll through the readout settings so you can tell at a glance what's happening inside the system(razzle/dazzle!).</p><p>I still need to make some upgrades and revisions to the OSH boards so I will wait to post the Gerbers until everything is all in place.</p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </li>
                    
                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/5917/log/18741-the-hydrogen-adapter-safety-modifications">The hydrogen adapter safety modifications</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">3 months ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5917/log/18741#discussion-list">
                                0 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>To add really bulletproof safety measures to the hydrogen adapter it was necessary to run a cable from the binary fuel tank that supported a remote valve shutoff, as well as a sensor that detects leaking hydrogen.</p><p>This is what the adapter looks like now. The white tube is the new fuel inlet point:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7241201433089293265.jpg"></p><p>This is a look at the hydrogen sensor:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/8310401433089308346.jpg"></p><p>This is how it looks attached to the engine:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/9032341433089323310.jpg"></p><p>And yet another view:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/8955441433089341579.jpg"></p><p>Note the additional jacks on the adapter plate-These carry power from the binary fuel tank or generator output to power the sensor circuit. In case of a leak or errant gas detection, the solenoid valve shuts off the fuel supply to the engine and activates the engine kill switch. Despite adding these features, the adapter is still inexpensive to make.</p>
