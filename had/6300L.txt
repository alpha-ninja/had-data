                            <h2><a href="/project/6300/log/19776-thoughts-on-pcbs-for-the-leds">Thoughts on PCBs for the LEDs</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">2 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6300/log/19776#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-19776"><p><strong>Abstract:</strong><strong></strong> Just thinking out loud. Also please comment if you know any low quantity PCB <span class="redactor-invisible-space">manufacture (like OSH park or dirty pcbs) that also makes aluminium base PCBs or have any experience with those manufactures.</span><br></p><p></p><p>Star bases are probably not the <em>best</em> way to mount LED to other materials, so I googled about making a PCB for high power LEDs. That <em>led</em><span class="redactor-invisible-space"> (pun intended) me to eBay where I found out, that there are cheaper and better options than star bases for mounting LEDs to aluminium.</span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space"><br></span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space"><a href="http://www.ebay.com/itm/257mm-x-47mm-Aluminium-PCB-Circuit-Board-for-14PCS-x-1W-3W-5W-LED-In-Series-/261507409277">$2.8 for 14 LEDs</a>, for example would be sufficient for my experiments, easier to mount and cheaper than the star bases I bought.</span></p><p><img src="http://i.ebayimg.com/00/s/NjY3WDEwMDA=/z/sZUAAOSwoQ1Tnj~B/$_57.JPG">And than there is <a href="http://www.ebay.com/itm/50W-10x5-225-175MM-Aluminium-PCB-Circuit-Board-for-50pcs-x-1W-3W-High-Power-LED-/221252831386">this board.</a> At $6.50 with shipping, it's more expensive than the star bases but at least it's smaller and easier to mount.</p><p><img src="http://i.ebayimg.com/00/s/MTI1MVgxNjAw/z/wTIAAOSwRLZT4azI/$_57.JPG">Anyway, I am not sure if I will put only 50 3W LEDs on the LED pcb. It would probably be a good idea to also have 30W of blue LEDs mounted on the PCB with a switch that would switch between the string of blue LEDs or another string of Full spectrum LEDs. </p><p>The thing is, that plants need more blue light during there grow stage, and  that I think that the full spectrum ones provide too much red light for the growing stage (but enough for the flowering stage).</p><p>I searched around for a cheap manufacture of metal core pcbs and found a <a href="http://www.eevblog.com/wiki/index.php?title=PCB_Manufacturers">page</a> on the EEVblog wiki. There are some and I decided to not worry about that too much, but I am not sure if I would even need a meta core PCB, because I looked at a <a href="http://www.cree.com/~/media/Files/Cree/LED%20Components%20and%20Modules/XLamp/XLamp%20Application%20Notes/XLamp_PCB_Thermal.pdf">cree application note</a> where it said that it can also be done with just a FR-4 PCB if you transfer the heat to a heat sink. I would need to do some calculation but there is some data missing. Maybe the Chinese seller will help me but I doubt it.</p><p>I am planing to go to a university near my high school on Wednesday with a LED and a supply for it, and hopefully get the LED tested with a spectrometer in the light laboratory.</p></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/6300/log/21566-cleaning-the-hydro-system-timelapse">Cleaning the hydro system, timelapse</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">23 days ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6300/log/21566#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-21566"><p><strong>Abstract</strong>: I decided to clean the bucket and change the solution anyway. I also switched back to the full spectrum LEDs. </p><p>I&apos;ve changed the lights back to the full spectrum LEDs, since the 90W&apos;s of white LEDs gave off to much heat. </p><p>I taped some aluminium foil to the document file folder after I removed the mechanism with my dremle. <br></p><p><img src="http://shrani.si/f/40/Iy/348H6iig/ezgif-3288406072.gif"></p><p>I have also cleaned the bucket end everything, even my medium to hold all the roots in the net pot. I cleaned it with hot water and 12% hydrogen peroxide cream (for dying hair, but I guess it should also work as a cleaning agent).</p><p><img src="http://shrani.si/f/f/E8/JkHAIjS/ezgifcom-maker-1.gif"></p><p>Then I taped the bucket with aluminium foil so very little light would get into the bucket. I also glued down the air stone, so it would be under the roots all the time and wouldn&apos;t move.</p><p><img src="http://shrani.si/f/2G/nh/2mnjJEre/ezgifcom-maker-2.gif"></p><p>I added 2 litres (about 0.53 gallons) of water, then I mixed in (5ml of) HESI TNT Complex fertilizer (NPK is 3 2 3). It&apos;s supposed to be for soil only (they also make a separate one for hydroponics) but this is what I have by hand.
