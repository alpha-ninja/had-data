                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>Pan and Tilt Camera steppers and Arduino</p><p>100 to 1 geared stepper and extra worm gears to increase torque and reduce speed.</p><p>Using Arduino and Motor Shield V1.2 for control via a joystick</p><p>The steppers are from eBay :-</p><p><i>DC5V 2-phase 4-wire Gear stepper motor</i></p><p><i>Full Metal Gear Stepper Motor</i></p><p>Arduino Leonardo and</p><p>Adafruit Motor/Stepper/Servo Shield for Arduino kit - v1.2</p><p>These Motor Shield are discontinued but may still be available on eBay or the Adafruit Motor/Stepper/Servo Shield for Arduino v2 Kit - v2.3 could be used with suitable changes to the code</p><p>Files:</p><p> Stepper_Shield_Worm_100_1.zip:-</p><p>Download here:-</p><p><a href="http://1drv.ms/1H4jUi4">http://1drv.ms/1H4jUi4</a></p><p> Stepper_Shield_Worm_100_1.ino</p><p>Stepper_Shield_Worm_100_1 Ardiuno wiring.jpg</p><p>USE AFMotor Modified folder with Leonardo by Michael Margolis</p><p>Notes:</p><p>USE AFMotor Modified</p><p>( Modified for the Leonardo by Michael Margolis)</p><p>Control both Stepper motors at the same time with different speeds if needed</p><p>For use with v1 shields</p><p>100-1 metal gear stepper. With further Worm reduction</p><p>Tilt, Up and Down 100-1 metal gear stepper.</p><p>With further Worm reduction and to increase Torque</p><p>Using Maplin Worm Gear, a bit bigger diameter than the one off eBay</p><p>But using the same bigger gear wheel off eBay</p><p>Using Analogue Joystick for control, with the Joystick buttonused to slow down movement using a delay.</p><p>Step Angle: 18 ° / 100 so after geared moves 0.18 ° per step</p><p>20 Steps per 360°</p><p>Reduction ratio: 100:1</p><p>2000 steps on output shaft after the 100:1 reduction</p><p>Actual motor Step Angle: 18 ° / 100 = 20 Steps per 360°</p><p>Public domain!</p><p>SINGLE - One coil is energized at a time.</p><p>DOUBLE - Two coils are energized at a time for more torque.</p><p>INTERLEAVE - Alternate between single and double to create a half-step in between.This can result in smoother operation, but because of the extra half-step, the speed is reduced by half too.</p><p>MICROSTEP - Adjacent coils are ramped up and down to create a number of 'micro-steps' between each full step.This results in finer resolution and smoother rotation, but with a loss in torque.</p><p>Note: Step is a synchronous command and will not return until all steps have completed.For concurrent motion of two motors, you must handle the step timing for both motors and use the "onestep()" function below.</p><p>Speed - the speed in RPM</p><p>Note: The resulting step speed is based on the 'steps' parameter in the constructor.If this does not match the number of steps for your motor, you actual speed will be off as well.</p><p>Adafruit motor shield V1.0</p><p><a href="https://learn.adafruit.com/adafruit-motor-shield">https://learn.adafruit.com/adafruit-motor-shield</a></p><p>AF_Stepper Class Modified for the Leonardo by Michael Margolis</p><p><a href="https://learn.adafruit.com/afmotor-library-reference/af-stepper-class">https://learn.adafruit.com/afmotor-library-reference/af-stepper-class</a></p><p>Modified for the Leonardo by Michael Margolis</p><p>Basic Analogue Joystick</p><p>Ref : <a href="http://arduino.cc/en/Tutorial/AnalogInput">http://arduino.cc/en/Tutorial/AnalogInput</a></p><p>Build from:-</p><p>AFMotor_MultiStepper.pde</p><p><a href="http://www.airspayce.com/mikem/arduino/AccelStepper/AFMotor_MultiStepper_8pde-example.html">http://www.airspayce.com/mikem/arduino/AccelStepper/AFMotor_MultiStepper_8pde-example.html</a></p><p>IMPORTANT:</p><p>Works with all Arduino including Leonardo, Mega using modified AFMotor for the Leonardo by Michael Margolis</p><p>Version 1 of the motor shield</p><p>Michael Margolis more information</p><p><a href="http://www.oreilly.com/pub/au/4466">http://www.oreilly.com/pub/au/4466</a></p><p> and also requires:-</p><p>The AccelStepper library for Arduino</p><p><a href="http://www.airspayce.com/mikem/arduino/AccelStepper/index.html">http://www.airspayce.com/mikem/arduino/AccelStepper/index.html</a></p><p>Control both Stepper motors at the same time</p><p>Public domain! </p><p>Video is use as a test</p><p><a href="http://www.dailymotion.com/video/x2pjbhc_pan-and-tilt-camera-steppers-and-arduino_tech">http://www.dailymotion.com/video/x2pjbhc_pan-and-tilt-camera-steppers-and-arduino_tech</a></p>
