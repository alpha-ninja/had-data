                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-21737"><p>7/12:  Ran across a description of a Sample &amp; Hold; 13 lines of 
code later, I have my 15th effect. I&apos;ll have to play with the hold time 
to find the best sound.</p><p>7/22: Well, after a few days tracking down
 a bug caused by combined misreading of the PIC datasheet AND leaving 
some test code un-commented-out, I finally saw my waveforms change 
smoothly in response to the pedal magnet. YES!</p><p>Tonight I&apos;m off to the local makerspace (Sask TechWorks) to see about printing a case and components.</p><p>7/28:
 after various fits and starts, I got a &apos;learning prototype&apos; printed - 
as in &apos;&quot;now i get to see how bad my Sketchup skills are&quot;.  :)</p><p>Being
 that it was a flat box, the corners pulled up - oops, lesson learned. 
And I STILL  can&apos;t figure out how the Makerbot printed several 
centimeters of  (millimeter-sized) horizontal beams WITH NO SUPPORT 
STRUCTURE! How&apos;dtheydothat?</p>Anyhow, in spite of the pulling, the important areas printed fine, and showed me some changes that need to be<p> made for ergonomics. All in all, for a first pass, I&apos;m satisfied. </p><p>Tonight it&apos;s back to the &apos;space for more...</p></div>
                            </div>

                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/6410/log/21801-the-hunt-continues">The Hunt Continues</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">19 days ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6410/log/21801#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-21801"><p>7/29: I printed up a small jig to test pedal sensors. It lets me turn a magnet by hand over the  sensor; this helped me pinpoint a not-bug in the position sensor coding. It turns out the AS5055A output is 0-1FFF (13 bits) over 360 degrees; the datasheet must be referring to 12 bits over 180 degrees. Anyhoo, the position sensor is tracking fine now.</p><p>7/30: The last bug has been obliterated, all scope waveforms track on all effects - time for the acid test! I hooked up a guitar to the input, and the output to the Aux In jack of a cheapo countertop stereo, and ... a split-second of sound, and &lt;pop&gt; no audio. Power off/on, a split -second of sound, and &lt;pop&gt;. WTF?</p><p>I hooked in the oscillator I was testing with, everything&apos;s back to normal; at least nothing blew. &apos;Sample/Hold&apos; and &apos;Theremin&apos; sound totally *awesome* as you move the magnet! Most of the rest work as expected, but Chorus seems a bit odd; then again, it could be that I&apos;m getting cancelling effects from a single-frequency input tone.</p><p>After that, I tried the guitar again - NOW it decides to behave! Maybe I&apos;m sitting too close to the speaker, and feedback tripped something.<br></p><p>All in all, it&apos;s working, just not reliably. More testing to come.</p><p>8/6: Came across a description of something called &apos;Track &amp; Hold&apos; in an essay on sample &amp; hold circuits. It is now effect number 16 on the device. Given that the selector is a 12-position encoder, that&apos;s 1-1/3 turns of the knob to scroll through the list. I&apos;m declaring a moratorium on new effects until I get some opinions on what to cull. My Carpal Tunnels will thank me later. Plus, as Brian mentioned in the review, I need to focus on the mechanical side of things. </p><p>8/11: My first print of a foot pedal mechanism came out with a weak layer through everything; the plastic is pale, with visible gaps between layers. Pieces fall off at the merest touch. Still, it gives me some insight into what might and might not work.<br></p></div>
