                            <p><p>Step 1....</p><p>Capture an overall RGB value for the video and use that to help discriminate against &quot;natural&quot; objects.  It is far from perfect but seems to do the job for now.</p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/OUfW6OpO8jY" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" width="500"></iframe><table><tbody><tr><td>No filter so every object detected is visible.<img style="width: 401px; height: 601.988px;" src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7776281433882117097.png"></td><td>With background AVG (top left is avg BGR)<img style="width: 352px; height: 599.106px;" src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7036971433882127233.png"></td></tr></tbody></table><p>2.  Building the arm</p><p>  Starting off with the shoulder joint.</p><ul><li>First make or buy a servo bracket.  I made one out of some HDPE cutting board and attempted to flame bend it.  Bad idea, looks like crap but works.  I will post the specs so you can easily replicate with a saw and drills.</li><li>Next take (4) 4-40 machine screws and (4) 6-32 machine screws (trimming was required) and bolted to the bracket.</li><li>Assemble the mess and admire.</li></ul><table><tbody><tr><td><img style="width: 362px; height: 482px;" src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1927421435191081296.jpg" height="482" width="362"></td><td><img style="width: 368px; height: 491px;" src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7101721435190976985.jpg" height="491" width="368"></td></tr><tr><td><p><img style="width: 322px; height: 464px;" src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3584851435192381282.jpg" height="464" width="322"></p></td><td><br>Almost completed shoulder joint. I figure for this prototype it will work, at least the prototype.</p><p>The rough dimensions are 6&quot; and 12&quot;.  I decided on this based on a crab diagram and since my truck is 35&quot; wide divide that by 2 and then factor in the joints.  About 18&quot; of total arm is halfway to the mouth.  The small metal piece was milled to insert into the tube and allow for attaching to the up/down servo.<br></p><p><img style="width: 560px; height: 419px;" src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6412781435192415754.jpg" height="419" width="560"></p><p>Final arm complete.
