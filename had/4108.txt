            <div>
                <div id="comment-20989-content">
                    <p>It is an interesting idea, and I am doing something pretty much like this myself. I would recommend you the idea of, instead of looking for 1 PIC MCU to generate everything, separate tasks between 2 of them (or more if you feel the need to). I am building a PIC computer myself with the intention of learning about those devices and systems enough to build a larger project that I have with possible commercial applications.</p><p>Try it like this: Use a PIC18F4550 for the display and for connectivity (since it has a USB Full Speed 2.0 port built in, which is extremely useful, and still has a UART feature for other uses), and maybe for the firmware, since this PIC model has a boot-loading feature. And then for the main tasks you can use another PIC MCU (or other brand for that matter), a MCU that has its strong points on processing power rather than connectivity.</p><p>Using PIC18F4550 and PIC16F877A, for example, gives you 1 USB connection and at least 3 others for USART communication, with around 30+MHz of processing speed combined. With creativity (which you appear to have a lot), you can go long distances with this project.</p><p>Good luck!<br></p>
