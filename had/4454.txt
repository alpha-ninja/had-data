                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">One hub to rule them all!<br /><br />Home automation is a growing space and every device has it's own way to interact with it and your phone is starting to get cluttered with all the different applications for each company. The Open Smart Hub allows you combine them in one place, make new ones, and let your home handle itself based on your preferences automatically.<br /><br />Imagine you're sitting at home on your sofa and it gets way too warm due to the afternoon sun shining through your window. No need to get up, because your window begins to open by itself in order to cool it down. An hour later it starts to drizzle (a summer storm) but luckily your home hub knew that would happen and it closed your windows for you!<br /><br />That's just one example of what the Home Hub could do for you.<br /><br />More information at: <a href='http://www.hackster.io/anthony-ngu/open-source-home-hub' target="_blank">http://www.hackster.io/anthony-ngu/open-source-home-hub</a></div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>There are three components. The online-hub, local-hub, and devices.</p><ul><li>The online hub lives in an Azure web app with WebSockets turned on.</li><li>The local hub connects to this online hub and runs on a local machine with Node.js. It will communicate to your devices through the internet or local network.</li><li>The devices are your smart home devices. They can be anything from a NEST thermostat, WeMo switches, SmartThings devices, or even your own self-made projects.</li></ul>Currently Supported Devices:<ul><li>Spark maker projects (Button, Motion, RGB Strip)</li><li>WeMo</li><li>WeatherUnderground APIs for local astronomical and weather forecasts</li><li>Clock for time related triggers and data</li></ul><p>Currently Being Developed:</p><ul><li>Yamaha Receiver</li><li>Z-Wave Devices</li><li>Amazon Echo Voice Integration</li></ul><p>Want to see a device supported? You have the power to add it yourself!</p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>

                    <p>The idea is the same, the execution is different.<br><br>The purpose behind building&nbsp;OpenSmartHub was to make a solution that was less complicated, easy to contribute to, and had a server component based in the cloud to allow you access from anywhere.</p><p><br>While OpenHAB does a great job with&nbsp;a&nbsp;Java based solution and distribution add-ons for device types,&nbsp;the generic usage is a webserver hosted on your local network and&nbsp;it&nbsp;didn't seem a suitable base for me to grow my solution on top of.</p><p>That's why I created&nbsp;OpenSmartHub.
