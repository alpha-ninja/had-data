                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Unfortunately smartphones are not adapted for the visually impaired people and users had to rely on Siri or any other personal assistants to communicate with, ask for info and find their way through the world. A smartphone isn't the best choice for a blind person because it's hard to work with. <br /><br />That's why we need to look at the problem from a different perspective and bring up a solution. Intella Watch is designed to do just that. It helps people find their way, find out what the time is or get directions. <br /><br />This watch is specially designed for vision impaired people because it gives them the possibility to read texts or to communicate with others. The watch can be carried around with ease on your wrist because it's very light and the size resembles other watches. In other words, vision impaired people won't have to carry a bulky & ugly terminal with them or heavy Braille books.<br /></div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p><strong></strong>The idea is simple. </p><p><strong>The problem.</strong></p><p><strong><br></strong></p><p>What can vision impaired people do when they want to travel somewhere and they need directions ? How could they find out what the time is ? How can they get in contact with someone else &amp; if somebody is calling them, how will they answer ?</p><p><strong>The solution.</strong></p><p><strong><br></strong></p><p>Vision impaired people need a smartwatch of their own. If they want to travel somewhere they need directions. If they want to know what the time is, they need a clock to tell them that. If they get a message, they want to read it. If somebody is calling them, they want to know it.</p><p>Basically, a smartwatch can do all of that.</p><p><strong>Project overview.</strong></p><p><strong><br></strong></p><p>The project is divided in a couple of stages:</p><ul><li><strong>Stage 1</strong>  - Answer basic questions like how will the watch look like, how it will communicate, how the user will interact,  how it will work &amp; how it will impact the life of vision impaired people.</li></ul><span></span><ul><li><strong>Stage 2</strong> - Build a working smartwatch prototype &amp; show basic functions.</li></ul><span></span><ul><li><strong>Stage 3</strong> - Detail the technology behind the smartwatch. How it works, how it will communicate using Braille.</li></ul><span></span><ul><li><strong>Stage 4</strong> - Make it easy &amp; accessible for everyone to use. Publish the results, documentation &amp; get everyone excited about it.</li></ul><p>The watch will look no different than today&apos;s smartwatches, be it round or rectangular shaped. It will communicate with the user through a Braille type display (which is different than a graphical display) because it involves a couple of electromechanical systems or MEMS for short. The way the user will interact will be through a couple of gestures and selecting appropriate functions like &quot;Give me directions&quot; or &quot;Read me the message I received&quot;.</p><p>The way it will work will be not much different than today&apos;s smartwatches. Intella Watch is going to be run by a low power microcontroller unit and a Li-ion battery. The smartwatch will exchange information with the outside world through a Bluetooth interface like getting weather information or retrieving messages. Besides that, the smartwatch is capable of delivering entire texts and that could be a huge plus if the person doesn&apos;t want to carry heavy Braille books with them.</p><p>In order to prove that a smartwatch can be built, a prototype has to be done that shows basic functions like &quot;time&quot;, &quot;date&quot;, &quot;notifications&quot; and &quot;interactions&quot;. Future development revolves around those 4 functions and is crucial to underline them.</p><p>The technology behind Intella Watch is pretty straightforward and the only complicated part that needs extensive research is the Braille display. </p><p><strong>Licence.</strong></p><p>Intella Watch is released as Open Source, under <a href="http://www.gnu.org/licenses/lgpl-3.0.en.html" target="_blank">LGPL v3 license</a>, covering code, circuit and enclosure design, <a href="https://github.com/robertcotofana/Intella-Watch" target="_blank">all available on Github</a>.<br></p>
                                
                            </p>
