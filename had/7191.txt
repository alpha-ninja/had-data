                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Hydrogen fuel cells would be an incredible alternative to fossil fuels and lithium ion batteries for providing power to vehicles but they are plagued by the cost of Platinum electrodes.<br /><br />Wouldn't it be great if we could build a vehicle, without the use of fossil fuels, noble metals, or dwindling supplies of lithium metal?  Using non-polluting hydrogen electrolytically made with solar power we can reduce the strain on the environment that our desire to move about freely has developed.<br /><br />I think its possible and I have determined what materials will allow me to do so, I will attempt to build this thing in under four days as well. </div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/-kmfRGJFM-U" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p>Hydrogen fuel cells up till now have been expensive to produce requiring Pt electrodes. Following the discovery of Zhu Chen and crew at Waterloo Institute for Nanotechnology I will build a low cost robust hydrogen fuel cell with Nitrogen Doped Multi Walled Carbon Nanotubes as the reduction active layer.</p><p>Asphaltenes being a waste product from Tar Sands processing are an excellent candidate for this technology. Containing both vanadium and nickel in trace amounts they should oxidize hydrogen readily. </p><p>The use of Sulfonated Para-Aramids as a proton carrier will make this an incredibly robust device capable of withstanding impact and shock, suitable for use in a vehicle.</p><p>I will have to 3d print the prototype but will eventually make an entirely composite fuel cell. Making the operation questionable, however this cell utilizing a sulfonated aromatic amide polymer should operate at as low a temperature as 70 degrees Centigrade.</p><p><span></span>N doped CNT catalyze oxygen reduction better than Pt electrodes so why not build one if I already have the materials on hand, this will be a weekend hackathon to finish proof of concept on all three of my hackaday 2015 prize entries. </p><p>Using aluminum waste and HCl to make hydrogen, combined with N-CNT/asphaltene electrodes and sulfonated Kevlar I will attempt to build a fuel cell this weekend. Nominally it would use hydrogen from a solar power electrolysis process.</p><p>I will sulfonate the para-aramid with sulfuric acid, dry it and then epoxy my electrodes on both sides, slide it into a matrix that delivers hydrogen from the HCl and Aluminum reaction and Oxygen from a fan run by the cell itself.</p><p>Nominal power output at .9V and 200mA per cm^2, with 20 cells output should be 400A at .9V or run in series 20A at 18V.  Obviously it will be less than this due to amature building but eventually that is the target.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6344631439809857133.jpg"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4272931439814530917.jpg"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6324961439816082384.jpg"></p>

                        <h5>Build instructions</h5>
                        
                        
                            <ul class="post-content section-instructions-list">
                                
                                    <li>
                                        <span class="instruction-number">1</span>
                                        <div class="instruction-list-item"><p>Obtain a sufficient quantity 200g or more of chinese super critically extracted asphaltenes from alibaba.</p><p>Add asphaltenes to a container, a mason jar may be used.</p><p>Add acetone to the container with the asphaltenes, enough to fully cover and wet the dry chemical</p><p>Sonicate the asphaltene acetone mixture for 2 hours stirring the mixture from time to time to allow the acetone to dissolve any excess hydrocarbons and parrafin.<span class="redactor-invisible-space"><br></span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space">Filter the asphaltenes using a bucher funnel, filter flask, and vacuum pump.  Rinse the asphaltenes with warm acetone to remove any excess contaminates.<span class="redactor-invisible-space"><br></span></span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space"><span class="redactor-invisible-space">Place the filtered asphaltenes on an aluminum foil boat for drying in a vacuum oven.  Dry at 100 degress celcius and 27 inches of mercury for 1 hour.  Relieve vacuum slowly as to not blow the dry powder about. Place aside.<span class="redactor-invisible-space"><br></span></span></span></p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6191631439840187494.JPG"></div>
                                    </li>
                                
                                    <li>
                                        <span class="instruction-number">2</span>
                                        <div class="instruction-list-item"><p>Place 20g of iMWCNT obtained from cheaptubes.com in a mason jar. Add 200mL of concentrated Nitric Acid.  Sonicate for 2 hours stirring periodically. Filter the N-functionalized iMWCNT using a buchner, filter flask, and vacuum.  Rinse the ox iMWCNT with DI water.</p><p>Place on an aluminum boat and dry in a vacuum oven for 2 hours, take care not to release vacuum too quickly or the dry ox iMWCNT will be blown about in a fine smoke like powder.</p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/2583961439840151854.JPG"></div>
                                    </li>
                                
                                    <li>
                                        <span class="instruction-number">3</span>
                                        <div class="instruction-list-item"><p>Dry 100g of Urea in a vacuum oven for 1 hour.</p><p>Combine by weight either  Cathode or Anode Nanomaterials and 3% Urea.  </p><p>Place the ingredients into a ball mill and tumble for 8 hours.</p><p>This dry material will constitute the active layer for oxidation and reduction as well as electron transport to the electron carrier aluminum mesh.  It must be reduced before it will be effective but will be reduced after the electrodes are formed.  </p><p>As of now the nitrogen groups are attached laterally to the iMWCNT upon microwave urea assisted reduction they will be reincorporated into the sides of the iMWCNT making them work as a oxygen hydrogen reduction catalyst.</p><p>Place ball milled ingredients into a standard 1000W microwave and microwave for 20 minutes at a setting of 2 power level.</p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/5082031439840257227.JPG"></div>
                                    </li>
                                
                            </ul>
