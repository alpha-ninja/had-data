                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Plated vias and through holes remain one of the last frontiers in home PCB production. This projects intends to provide a means to cross this frontier safely, with cheap and readily available materials.<br />People tend to think it's not feasible as there are rather nasty/proprietary/expensive/hard to come by chemicals involved, which used to be the case, at least for the proprietary/expensive part. The nastiness is (at least IMHO) a bit overrated, since the main chemicals involved are copper sulphate and dilute sulphuric acid, which is no more or less nasty than your usual etching chemistry (assuming you're using cupric chloride).<br />To overcome the expensive/proprietary part, Reverse Pulse Current is used. The idea being that if the plating current is pulsed and/or reversed, the same (or even better) results as with traditional chemical additive systems (brighteners, levellers and carriers) can be achieved.<br /><br />The project consists of two parts, the process tank and the power supply.</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>The process tank is made with an off the shelf &quot;Lock&apos;n&apos;Lock&quot; brand food container, a few laser cut acrylic parts, a piece of copper heating pipe and a bit of PVC aquarium tube.</p><p>The power supply is a hybrid of a AVR controlled constant current source and an H-Bridge. It&apos;s powered by an ATX power supply, as these are ubiquitous and<span class="redactor-invisible-space"> </span>capable of providing a lot of power.</p>
                                
                            </p>
