                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">I was inspired by my friend's R2D2. I saw how much fun he had entertaining people with his droid. I didn't want to steal any of his thunder with a Star Wars droid and I was always partial to K-9 from Doctor Who. I decided to build my own semi-replica of the Tin Dog. I say it's a semi-replica because I have personalized a few external parts so it's not exactly the same. I've nicknamed him Flux.  It's a completed project in the way any project is really complete. I'll be upgrading and altering parts so they make K9 easier to use.</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>K9's brain is an Arduino Uno that receives commands from a PS2 controller. The drive motors are running in tank mode via a Sabertooth 2X12. A Syren 10 runs a salvaged wiper motor  from an Astro van to raise and lower his head. Sounds are generated by an MP3trigger and amplified by a Sure 2X25. </p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/6670/log/21272-k-9s-upgrades">K-9&#39;s upgrades</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a month ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6670/log/21272#discussion-list">
                                0 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>K-9&apos;s rebuild began with a sanding and filling over the first paint job. I embedded a magnet in K-9 so I could attach things such as 3d printed celery or a Starfleet commbadge. </p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6208591437494991819.jpg"><p>I also moved the button panel up slightly. This kept the buttons from hitting the body and gave me room to hide a voltage regulator and Arduino Micro for the LED s</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/2908161437492629208.jpg"></p><p>The pipe connecting the head to the neck motor was upgraded from plastic to metal. My father cut a metal tube on his work&apos;s mill based on my template.  <br></p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </li>
                    
                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/6670/log/20931-k-9s-outing">K-9&#39;s outing</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a month ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6670/log/20931#discussion-list">
                                0 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>K-9&apos;s first convention taught me a few lessons. The convention was in August and the AC in the building stopped working. The glue I used to keep K-9&apos;s head attached to his body weakened to the point where his head just fell off. I jammed a pocket knife into the two ends to keep it from happening again. </p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/2765231436900810778.jpg"><p>K-9&apos;s ears stopped functioning, hence the fez. My 5v step down transformer board blew due to the heat.  </p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/8300571436900797331.jpg"></p><p>I decided that I didn&apos;t like K-9&apos;s eyes, body color, or keypad lighting sequencer. I built a flux capacitor circuit to &quot;randomize&quot; the blinking but it just wasn&apos;t good enough. Right after I tore K-9 down expecting to have until February the next year to have him fully upgraded he was invited to a local expo in November as a &quot;guest&quot;. <br></p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </li>
