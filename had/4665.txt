                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><h2>Introduction:</h2><p>&apos;Aleph&apos; is first letter belonging to the oldest verified alphabet, the Phoenician alphabet. The Phoenician Alphabet replaced the symbols and drawings with a dynamic and easily manipulated letters. Exactly like that, Aleph aims to replace the static paper receipts with a digital dynamic copy that can be easily converted or manipulated.</p><h2>The Problems:</h2><ol><li>Everyone gets hundreds of paper receipts monthly. These papers have a chemical coating making them impossible to recycle. They all end up in our garbage bins, making tons upon tons of un-treatable garbage globally. </li><li>The coating chemicals are not environmentally friendly, and some studies showed that they are not even healthy for the human body. Don&apos;t take my words for it just Google BPA Thermal Papers.</li><li>In general, paper production is bad for the environment. No matter how harsh the laws are to protect the forest areas, there will always be people bypassing and stretching the laws. Less paper production will hopefully account to less wood generation, which in turn will help in saving green areas. </li><li>Paper receipts are very hard to manipulate and edit. If the consumer needs to generate a report, he will have to go through the process of scanning all the receipts. Even after scanning the result is just a still image that is not possible to use for automated reports or calculation software.</li></ol><h2>The Solution:</h2><p>On the hardware level Aleph is a small electronic device that will show up as a printer when plugged to the PC. The key concept is to send a printing command to Aleph and it will be able to generate a digital receipt and transfer it to the user using NFC.</p><p>The basic operation model is that the store owner (Restaurant - Supermarket - ATM ...) will replace (or use them together) the traditional Thermal Receipt Printer with Aleph Device. When checking out, the customer can then just tap his phone and his receipt will be transferred to the phone directly. On the phone the Aleph App (or 3rd party app) can get the data and offer some automated calculations and reports.</p><p>In addition Aleph will upload a PDF copy of the receipt to our secured server. At a later time the customer can download the copy using the mobile application. </p>What differentiates Aleph from other solutions that tried to solve this problem is the ease of integration. Aleph does not require any additional software to work with existing POS or ATM. The same process that is required to install a paper printer will be used to integrate Aleph with the system<ol><li>Plug the device to the PC using either USB, LAN or WiFi.</li><li> Install the printer driver on the PC</li><li>Print to Aleph from any software</li></ol><h2>The Benefits:</h2><p>The benefits are numeral for both the consumer and the store owner. Below is a short list but this does not mean that this is the end of it. As the work progresses, the list will be expanded.</p><p><strong>Consumer Benefits:</strong></p><ul><li>Digital editable copy of all the receipt, text based</li><li>Sort, Search and Share any receipt</li><li>Detailed real-time expense management </li><li>Generate reports based on time </li><li>No more extra vouchers and coupons</li><li>Optional cloud backup and online editing and more available reports</li></ul><p><strong>Store Owner Benefits:</strong></p><ul><li>Analytics based on anonymous consumer information</li><li>Push notifications for consumers that opt-in for it</li><li>Loyalty programs based on consumer trends</li><li>Voucher and discounts over the air</li><li>Added value service</li><li>Cost reduction for paper, ink and maintenance</li></ul><p><strong>Environmental Benefits:</strong></p><ul><li>Significantly reduce paper waste globally</li><li>Reduce deforestation caused by paper production</li><li>Reduce air pollution due to use of coating chemicals</li><li>Reduce health risks based on the contacts with thermal receipts<br></li></ul><h2>The Ultimate Goal:</h2><p>The ultimate goal for this project would be to have Aleph integrated inside &apos;Tap to pay&apos; devices. Like this, when checking out in the store, the consumer can tap to pay and then tap again to receive the digital receipt.</p>

                            <h2><a href="/project/4665/log/23281-documentation">Documentation</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">20 hours ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4665/log/23281#discussion-list">

                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>I would usually make a separate external document for this part. But since Hackaday.io has all the documentation tools included, I will just collect everything in this project so things don&apos;t get duplicated.</p><h2>Brief Description</h2><p>Aleph is an NFC printer aimed to work with any existing POS system without the need of changing anything on the POS side. The POS will treat Aleph just like any other paper printer, Aleph software and hardware will manage the rest. As <a data-mention-user-id="7" href="/hacker/7">@Benchoff</a> puts it &quot;IT&#x2019;S LIKE APPLE PAY, BUT FOR RECEIPTS&quot; [<a href="http://hackaday.com/2015/07/06/hackaday-prize-entry-its-like-apple-pay-but-for-receipts/" target="_blank">link</a>]</p><h2>Milestones</h2><p>Based on a lot of feedback from this community, Aleph milestones have changed many times during the design process. I still expect it to change as the design continues. The image below describes the achieved and still to be achieved goals for Aleph.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3400411439801735051.png"></p><h2>Hardware Design</h2><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6803471439803011576.png"></p>The hardware is responsible for transporting the JSON object generated by the software to the mobile app. It will receive an HTTP request parse it and send it using NFC to the smartphone.<p>The hardware design is done using KiCad. All the project files are open and available in the GIT repo. For those that do not have access to KiCad, the documents are also available in PDF and Gerber formats. For more information please visit the following <a href="https://bitbucket.org/HishamDaou/aleph-lan-dev/src/b89331b75f9365e26b0905dd69c82323182710a3/aleph-lan-hardware/Electronics/Design/?at=master" target="_blank">link</a>. </p><p>In order to make things easier here are the most important files in the repo : <a href="https://bitbucket.org/HishamDaou/aleph-lan-dev/raw/b89331b75f9365e26b0905dd69c82323182710a3/aleph-lan-hardware/Electronics/Design/aleph-lan-hardware.pdf" target="_blank">PDF Schematic</a> , <a href="https://bitbucket.org/HishamDaou/aleph-lan-dev/raw/b89331b75f9365e26b0905dd69c82323182710a3/aleph-lan-hardware/Electronics/Design/aleph-lan-hardware-gerber.pdf" target="_blank">PDF Gerber</a> , <a href="https://bitbucket.org/HishamDaou/aleph-lan-dev/raw/b89331b75f9365e26b0905dd69c82323182710a3/aleph-lan-hardware/Electronics/Design/aleph-lan-hardware.csv" target="_blank">CSV BOM</a> .</p><h2>Software Development</h2><p>The software side of Aleph is responsible of two main things. The first one is parsing the incoming print command and generating a JSON object from it. The second one is generating a PDF file from the print command to give an exact replica of the paper receipt. The JSON is then sent using LAN to the Aleph hardware. The PDF is uploaded to a secured cloud location for later access by the customer.</p><p>All the PC side software development was done using Java. This is to make it cross platform in the future. For the time being the PC software relies on two ready software that work only on windows, this will be changed in the future when all the hardware part is done. As with the hardware, the software is open source and available in the same repo. Here is a <a href="https://bitbucket.org/HishamDaou/aleph-lan-dev/src/b89331b75f9365e26b0905dd69c82323182710a3/aleph-lan-pc-print/?at=master" target="_blank">quick link</a> to the software folder. </p><h2>Firmware Development</h2><p>The firmware is built using Eclipse CDT and GCC on Ubuntu. But since the code is <a href="https://bitbucket.org/HishamDaou/aleph-lan-dev/src/b89331b75f9365e26b0905dd69c82323182710a3/aleph-lan-embedded/?at=master" target="_blank">published openly</a>, anyone can fork the repo and make his own version of Aleph firmware using any desired platform. </p><h2>Mobile App Development</h2><p>Currently, and since the transportation is done using NFC only, the mobile application is developed for Android. The mobile app has the minimal features to function and the UI was done quickly just to show the functionality. Later on the application should get a lift off and implement material design guidelines, also get a lot of more features. [<a href="https://bitbucket.org/HishamDaou/aleph-lan-dev/src/b89331b75f9365e26b0905dd69c82323182710a3/aleph-lan-mobile/?at=master" target="_blank">link</a>]</p><h2>Ultimate Goals</h2><p>The first goal is to make Aleph integrate inside already existing &apos;Tap to pay&apos; devices. This will allow the user to pay and get the receipt by a tapping on the same device. I am already working to achieve this, I will post more details when something solid comes up.</p><p>The next goal of Aleph is to allow users without access to smartphones to benefit from it. Again a course of action has been set, more details will be published once the work is proved.</p><h2>Licenses</h2><p>This version of Aleph is based on free software:</p><p>1- Redmon:  Redirection Port Monitor [<a href="https://bitbucket.org/HishamDaou/aleph-lan-dev/raw/b89331b75f9365e26b0905dd69c82323182710a3/Setup/redmon/LICENCE" target="_blank">GNU GPL3</a><span class="redactor-invisible-space">]</span></p><p>2- Ghostscript : A suite of software based on an interpreter for Adobe Systems&apos; PostScript and Portable Document Format (PDF). [<a href="http://www.artifex.com/page/licensing-information.html" target="_blank">LICENSE</a>]</p><p>The next version will be more robust and less dependent on 3rd party applications.</p>
