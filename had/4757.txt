The "caterpillar" drives will be attached to this pipe. Yes, we want more than one drive...<br /><br />3) Capture the hydrogen and chlorine produced at the electrodes and the sodium hydroxide coming out of the bottom.<br /><br />4) Use the hydrogen (in fuel cells!). Produce more electricity.<br /><br />5) And you get clean water from the fuel cells...<br /><br />Well at least that's the plan...<br /></div>

The first run at about the 10s mark has 10 drives in series and gives an emf of about 18mV.                             <p><p>Back of the envelope calculations:</p><p>Pump flow rate = 200l/h = 0.2m3/h = 5.56x10-5m3/s</p><p>Cross sectional area of tubing = (pi)r2 = (0.005)2=7.85 x 105m2</p><p>Flow velocity = 5.56x10-5 / 7.85 x 105= 0.708 m/s</p><p>So for experimental set up, </p><p>B = 1.0T</p><p>l = 0.01m</p><p>u = 0.708m/s</p> and e should be 1x0.01x0.708= 7.08x10-3V across each pair of electrodes…<p>Now to get the monkeys working...</p>

Adding more magnets increases the area, but keeps the same density.)</p><p>Any idea what the current would be in your setup?
