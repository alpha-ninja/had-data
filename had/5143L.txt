                            <h2><a href="/project/5143/log/16932-generic-pcb">generic PCB</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">4 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5143/log/16932#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-16932"><p>I've been using a pretty generic "breadboard" PCB design for most of my projects for several years, now... Every time I get an order, I fill all the empty-space with this design. It's pretty simple, but with a few features I find handy...</p><p>The basic idea is standard .1inch spacing, two-layer, and a ground/power plane.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/370201429968754335.png"></p><p>It pushes some limits, using absolute-minimum spacings guaranteed by the manufacturer... Relying on accurate solder-masks, etc. So far, I haven't had any trouble. My <a data-mention-project-id="3602" href="/project/3602">#audioThing - ephemeralEventRecorder</a> submission, for the HaD Trinket Everyday-Carry contest, was built *entirely* on this breadboard, as have been many of my other projects.</p><p>There're a couple things to point out... Note the silkscreened lines. These are on both sides, in alternating rows of vias... The silkscreen shows which rows are connected to which "plane." (Shown here, the Red plane is "Top", the blue-plane is bottom, but hardly visible as the two are nearly identical except at the thermal-reliefs to the corresponding vias).</p><p>With this design, except at the top/bottom edges of the board, every pin can easily be solder-blobbed on the "solder-side" to either power/ground (or whatever purpose one might use the planes for).</p><p>There're a few other nicities of this design... Discrete 0804(?) SMTs can easily be placed between the vias and a pin, making for really easy capacitor-supply-decoupling... and probably quite a bit I'm forgetting. (connecting four discretes to the same via and all the surrounding pins was an interesting challenge, once!)</p><p>For the sake of a "new" design, there may be some more considerations... I'd have to sit down and really think about it, but a few off-hand: </p><p>Plausibly break-up the planes into sections which could easily be solder-blobbed together... Thus there could be an "analog" and "digital" plane on different halves of the board. <br></p><p>Other SMT (.05in e.g. DIPs) can be used pretty easily with this board, with a tiny bit of pin-bending and point-to-pin wiring... No biggie, but maybe something more sophisticated could be accomplished by, e.g., making the vias detachable from their planes...</p><p>(would it make more sense to stagger those vias in diagonal-rows instead? There's not much path for high-current in the horizontal direction)</p><p>Might think about decreasing the annular-rings on the regular .1in holes... since they're through-hole-plated, there's really not much need for them to be too large, which would increase the planes' "trace" widths.. (Never worked with non-circular pads, but maybe that's an option, as well).</p><p>Anyways, I'm sure there's more I could-think/have-thought of... As it stands, it's basically just a 'pcb' part I grab and throw-in to unused-space. But it sure would be handy to have bigger and better!</p><p>NOTE: This is my own design... I don't *think* I borrowed it from anywhere besides *years* of experience... If you choose to use this design for your own purposes, I'd sure appreciate a shout-out. And if you plan to profit from it, PLEASE contact me first!<br>  </p></div>

                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-23030"><p>Summer came early this year, and fuller-force than any I remember. Combined with the lack of airflow in this apartment, that means: The fan<em>s</em> in <em>the</em> window have been running full-blast basically 24/7 for quite some time.</p><p>And <em>that</em> means I&apos;d almost completely forgotten my necessity for this project; the fans do a pretty good job covering up the fridge-noise, or at least reducing the ability for those moments of silence.</p><p>That said, this project may&apos;ve gone forgotten for some time, but it&apos;s not <em>gone</em>. I foresee its necessity again in the cooler months (though, not sure how I survived all those months with the airflow concern...)</p><p>Anyways, thanks <a data-mention-user-id="16981" href="/hacker/16981">@Blecky</a> for reminding me of this project!</p><p>It doesn&apos;t seem to have progressed far enough for the 2015 quarter-finals, maybe 2016!</p><p>Oh, and thanks to all the comments!
