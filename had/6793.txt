                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">JUDGES: there's no way this is complete enough for quarter-finals. I suggest you use the time you would've otherwise spent checking this project for requirements to instead take a moment for yourself. You deserve it! Thank you for being you! And thank you SO MUCH for the lasering-order! Believe me, it'll be put to good use! (Maybe next year, for this one, eh?)<br /><br />Vacuum Fluorescent Displays are pretty awesome.. They lend themselves well to color-filtration, so why not add *selective* color-filtration using an old LCD panel as an overlay? And since there's so much extra space on the LCD maybe throw in some other groovy instruments, gauges, indicator-lights, and maybe even an oscilloscope.</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p><strong>VFD + LCD = (sectionally-) selectable color filtration</strong></p><p>Yeah, Got this ancient VFD, 40x2. I do like the nice glow these things have, and the sharp contrast... And the ability to use color-filters...</p><p>I tried it out once, throwing an old laptop panel in front, with its backlight/back-panel removed... it seemed to do quite nicely. I kinda dug the 3D aspect of it, as well... the &quot;display&quot; being more of a window... the overall shape being somewhat more &quot;square&quot; and hiding the fact that the VFD is distinctly rectangualar... Could throw other things back there, too, LEDs, whatnot. Even stencilled lightbulb covers, and selectable color-filtration for those as well... even lighted circuitry... Who knows, maybe color-filtered Nixies... or those 7-segment incandescents... The list goes on. The depth... I kinda dig it. The irony(?) of the ancient and new technologies; the new being capable of *all that* the old is doing, yet being used for little more than could be done with a simple color-wheel. Awesome. </p><p>(And, I&apos;ve already been using an ATtiny85 to drive laptop displays with nothing more than a couple TTL XORs, its limited memory and slow-refresh lends itself perfectly to a project like this. <a data-mention-project-id="3898" href="/project/3898">#my very first binary-clock</a>)</p><p>Also, found some recent inspiration from <a data-mention-project-id="5800" href="/project/5800">#HD44780 LCD Graphics</a> that *might* be implementable on this VFD. This cat... holy schmoly.</p><p>As far as *what* this UI will be for...? That remains to be determined.</p><p>(oh, and maybe something about a clear-capacitive-touch-overlay? Or a light-pen!)</p><p>Additional considerations:</p><p>Have been wondering what&apos;d happen if an LCD is cut horizontally (e.g. with a glass-cutter), might lend itself well to better-fitting the VFD. I think some displays could handle it, depending on the design)</p><p>Possibly have a lit/&quot;white&quot; background (somewhere a little below the LCD for 3D/floating-effect) and use a *portion* of the display for text/graphics... not sure.<br></p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>
