Most of ATMega1284 features are still unused, and potential functions can be, medical sensor, climate sensor and more, and those can be connected with I2C, SPI and sensored values can be transmit through Wi-Fi. Total cost is less than $20 and affordable for most of the people in the world, also presenting a solution for digital divide problem by providing an opportunity for computer program.  I would present the basement for it!<br /><br /><br />Currently supporting:<br />ATMega1284P version<br />128x64 SPI OLED display<br />160x128 SPI Color LCD<br />SD card, WiFi by Flashair<br />Twitter message compose and sending through FlashAir<br /><br />Full source code, schematic, board information are released on Github,<br /><a href='https://github.com/kodera2t/TinyBasicPlus_OLED_support' target="_blank">https://github.com/kodera2t/TinyBasicPlus_OLED_support</a></div>

So I made a new board containing all additional components as following pictures.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/711761434189642039.jpg"></p><p>It looks similar to previous one,,,, yes certainly. but behind OLED....</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/240871434189704910.jpg"></p><p>RTC (PCF2129AT, crystal embedded single chip RTC),  23K256 SPI SRAM, SPI Kanji-ROM are arranged on the frontside. As same as previous one, Hardware-serial (fortunately ATMega1284P has two hardware serials, it is quite useful!) is put on top-left for camera connection and D0/D1 terminal is put for thermal printer interface (working by software serial,,, This tiny computer has total three serial terminals!!)</p><p>You may think,, "where is CPU?"</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/2134631434189935921.jpg"></p><p>Yes, it is backside with RTC back-up battery. The five un-touched terminals are A/D converter inputs of ATMega1284 and not yet populated.</p><p>I am not sure the board and schematic information is useful, but I have already uploaded at</p><p><a href="https://github.com/kodera2t/TinyBasicPlus_OLED_support/tree/master/atmega1284p_v4_gerber">github....</a> have fun!</p>

                        <h5>Build instructions</h5>
                        
                        
                            <ul class="post-content section-instructions-list">
                                
                                    <li>
                                        <span class="instruction-number">1</span>
                                        <div class="instruction-list-item"><p>Step 1: Prepare to install arduino boot loader to "bare" ATMega1284P. Detailed can be found in "<a href="https://www.google.co.jp/search?client=safari&amp;rls=en&amp;q=Mighty1284&amp;ie=UTF-8&amp;oe=UTF-8&amp;gfe_rd=cr&amp;ei=BFnTVPaKD_P98we2oYGgAw">Mighty1284</a>" of google search</p><p>Step 2: Prepare your circuit board. I am using CNC but etching or board company can make it. The circuit is simple ( I omit even by-pass capacitor, but 16MHz computer seems to work without them. Just draw in Eagle may be enough to make your own board pattern. Please carefully connect between ATMega1284P with OLED if you want to use my code without modification..</p><p>Step 3: Please make your own new BASIC command in TinyBasicPlus. I believe this point is most interesting...</p></div>
                                    </li>
                                
                                    <li>
                                        <span class="instruction-number">2</span>
                                        <div class="instruction-list-item"><p>Step 4: Prepare your keyboard. As previously presented in <a href="http://hackaday.io/project/1260-single-chip-avr-basic-computer" target="_blank">http://hackaday.io/project/1260-single-chip-avr-basic-computer</a><br>, usage of PS/2 can be a good choice. But unfortunately small keyboard such as chatpad cannot be found, as far as I know. So I follow the Ben Heck's way,</p><p><iframe width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/Hjdj14C_jAI" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><span></span></p><p>This movie is well organized and self-consistent. I learned the way of re-programming of Xbox360 chatpad from this movie.</p><p>One thing to know: Chinese compatible (not Microsoft Genuine) chatpad does not use PIC (or OTP) and no way to re-programming. (no programming terminal at all.) Please avoid them for this purpose.</p><p>(Sorry for my confusion, the chip marked "microsoft" can be programmed by Pickit3! )</p><p>One additional TIPS for keyboard: Chatpad has a lot of LED init, and it is really power-eater. In my case, I don't want to feed them and removed all of LED on board to save power  consumption.</p></div>
                                    </li>
                                
                                    <li>
                                        <span class="instruction-number">3</span>
                                        <div class="instruction-list-item"><p>Step 5: Please design your case as you wish!!</p></div>
