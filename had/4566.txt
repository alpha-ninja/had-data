                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">About 1.6 billion people on earth don´t have access to electricity - resulting in problems with conserving food, light in the night hours, radio/tv and wireless communication.<br />However, they have access to biomass (vegetation and animal excrements) and solar energy. Grass vegetation has  to be burned regularly to prevent big fires (when too much flammable material grows, a ligtning strike can cause a big fire and therefore a big damage)<br />These natural heat sources are used for cooking anyway, so they can provide electricity simultaneously. <br />The FireNice will be capable of generating and storing energy from heat sources like open fire, biogas or a solar concentrator. <br />The small version of the system will be capable of delivering 200W of power, scalable to more - even by night.<br />It will provide a 230V/50Hz supply for most common devices like laptops, LED-lights, TVs, CB-Radios and tools.<br />A cooler with a foldable thermal chamber will provide cooling of food.</div>
