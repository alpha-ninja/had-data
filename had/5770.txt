                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">The market is flooded with so many power banks from the East. One of the problem of all the power banks I have is that they have horribly slow charging. Takes overnight to charge one of those 10000mAh+ packs - linear charging controllers. Also some of them can show "4/4 points" of battery, and out of juice half an hour - wait, my phone was just 30% charged?<br /><br />The solution? - Laptop replacement battery packs. Abundant 6 cell batteries can be found for cheap - have intelligence built in to gauge their health. Circuit consisting of a SMBus battery charger (fast charging), a supervisor (STM32F1), a display and high efficiency Buck/Boost converters depending as per requirements.<br /><br />Use cases: Charge your phones, tablets, act as an UPS for your always-on-the-internet BeagleBone, Pi or your favorite SBC, power your hacks, custom power train for robots, and as a laptop emergency power source (if it is identical to your laptop make).<br /><br />Aiming to become a universal DC source to power gadgets.</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>The project aims to build a power pack solution that</p><ul><li>Charges fast / allows devices to charge even while charging itself</li><li>Provide consistent charging to devices connected to it</li><li>Be as energy efficient as possible</li><li>Provide Consistent information about its own state (hours charge remaining etc)</li><li>Power small computers like the Pi, the BeagleBone Black reliably for long periods like an online UPS</li><li>[In the long term] Provide support for renewable energy harvesting using solar cells / MPPT with firmware upgrades.</li></ul><p><strong></strong></p><p>I see this project move towards a standard for powering almost any portable gadget, and can be expanded in the future to support LED lighting and BLDC fans to create a completely DC-powered room run by a battery pack.</p><p><strong>Licenses</strong></p><p>Firmware will be licensed under MIT License.</p><p>Hardware under a CERN Open Hardware License.</p><p>This project makes use of the Smart Battery Specification given at <a href="http://sbs-forum.org/specs/sbdat110.pdf">http://sbs-forum.org/specs/sbdat110.pdf</a> which is copyrighted by the Smart Battery Implementers Forum.</p>

                            <h2><a href="/project/5770/log/22166-choice-of-microcontroller">Choice of Microcontroller</a></h2>

                            </small>
                            <p><p>A long idle period and I thought a lot over this and eventually decided to go for a STM32F103C8T6 microcontroller to control the system. I had initially thought of using an Arduino Nano but integrating a few libraries tends to fill up code space really fast, so there might not be enough room for expansion later. The IC itself is also available cheaply due to it being used in a lot of low cost dev boards from China, and I have a few spares lying so it&apos;d be a good idea to engage them to work here.</p><p>I&apos;m now beginning work over board layout of the charger section, although I&apos;d seek some feedback on how many outputs you&apos;d like to see from this power pack, after which I will complete the board and send it off to the fab.</p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </li>
                    
                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/5770/log/20226-the-bq24725a-schematic">The bq24725a schematic</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">2 months ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5770/log/20226#discussion-list">
                                2 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>The TI bq24725a is a SMBus Smart Battery Charger, with integrated synchronous buck controller. It can be used to charge upto 4-cell Li batteries. It also has dynamic power path management, which dynamically switches the load between AC and battery power. It can also monitor current drawn from the AC source as well as the battery which can be measured by an ADC on the host side. </p><p>For this design I am using the BQ24725a evaluation board schematics, but that&apos;d be redrawn in KiCAD. Almost done, and this is how it looks as of now.</p><p>[Fix schematic]</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/2116721438786032128.png"></p><p>Now to design the MCU side of things.<br></p>
