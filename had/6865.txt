                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Imagine a classroom where students, parents, and teachers were always present. A classroom where students were driven to achieve more, and made excited by new ideas. Imagine a system that allowed parents to constantly stay in the loop of their child's academic career. Imagine a system that keeps parents, students, and teachers in constant contact. Imagine a system that allowed even the student who is sick and home away from school, be involved in the classroom. <br /><br />That is the goal behind Athena; to be a software based aid to the classroom environment. To allow seamless communication between parents, students, and teachers. To organize teacher and student notes on a server access able outside of the classroom. Ect.<br /><br />The idea is: <br />A Java app that is to be organized via unique tabs, representing a different facet of the classroom experience(i.e. Notes, Tests, ect.). The tabs are also to be limited in functionality by the user's roll in the classroom(i.e. parent, student, teacher).</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>The general idea is to create an open sourced platform to aid the classroom by improving the flow of information between parent, student, and teacher; with potential to become a source for anonymous large scale data collection in the classroom.</p><p>The program will organize different facets of the classroom, via the use of several tabs, such as &quot;Grades&quot; and &quot;Homework.&quot;</p><p>The program will access a schools dedicated server to pull/store pertinent information. Example: student wants to access his/her notes. Athena will access the schools server and pull the requested notes. They then later decide to save changes to said notes. The program will then, again, access the school&apos;s server and replace the original file.</p><p>It should be noted, I am currently looking into adding a messaging service as well as developing hardware to compliment the program.</p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>
