                            <h2><a href="/project/6418/log/20394-ehhh">Ehhh...</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">2 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6418/log/20394#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-20394"><p>I attempted something similar to the lunchbox RasPi build I linked to earlier, but I only managed to waste a lot of time and materials (I used something similar to a cooler).  The screen and the microcontroller could just barely fit together on the lid, but trying to get the HDMI cable and power cord in as well was a whole other story, and I ended up dislodging the supports for the HDMI port.  Luckily none of the important connectors were broken, just the supports, and I was able to solder one back into place- I wasn&apos;t touching the other one, it was WAY too close to a capacitor for comfort, plus my hand holding the soldering iron was shaking pretty badly (no clue why, and don&apos;t worry, I don&apos;t intend on becoming a surgeon).  The one support is, thankfully, strong enough.</p><p>New plan of attack: forget the portable version, code everything on a separate computer (I love Ubuntu, by the way) then transfer the files later.  At least that way I&apos;m less likely to break something and a coding fix will be easier on a regular computer.</p><p>One last thing; the &quot;face&quot; designs will be based off &quot;chibi&quot; designs in part to bring the creepiness factor down since it&apos;s not realistic, thus avoiding the Uncanny Valley (<a href="http://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/UncannyValley">http://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/UncannyValley</a>) and making the robot seem more friendly.  I may provide an option to decide which face to go with, but I will only be working on one personality for this project to save time, and I figure it&apos;s better to go with one well-developed personality than a bunch of poorly-developed ones to choose from. </p><p>Thanks for reading, I appreciate the support!</p><p>- Mister Malware<br></p></div>
