                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">RainBerry case is a compact, aesthetically designed, weatherproof case for Raspberry Pi and Pi camera. This project was born out of a need to monitor and protect the garden using a smart Pi camera with computer vision. There is no affordable case available for such purposes. <br /><br />RainBerry intend to provide a weatherproof case for a small cost to large pool of makers, who want to use Raspberry Pi in outdoor conditions. Many makers expressed huge interest on social media and echoed their needs, expecting RainBerry to be manufactured.  </div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/5283551439508982136.png"></p><p>For most Raspberry Pi and Pi camera users, it is impossible to find a case which has the following features in it. </p><ul><li>Compact</li><li>Weatherproof</li><li>Houses both Pi and Pi Camera together</li><li>Looks neat and clean (no wire clutter)</li><li>Wireless</li><li>Mount freely at any angle</li><li>Affordable </li></ul><p>RainBerry case was born out of this need with a simple sketch on the back of scratch paper. </p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1312691439508530346.jpg"></p><p>RainBerry case addresses each of the above problem and brings out a nice looking product to community. It is compact, aesthetically pleasing, houses all parts together and works in rain easily. It is a product born out of this necessity and community voices on Raspberry Pi forums. </p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/969061439508544240.jpg"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/5861131439508295491.jpg"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3584251439508367637.jpg"></p><p>During the thunderstorm</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4531481439508689499.jpg"></p><p>RainBerry after the rain stopped</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1974091439508384855.jpg">Look Ma, no water inside the case !</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6176081439508667781.jpg"></p><p>RainBerry case has successfully been used for the following purposes and programs are shared on the blog freely. </p><ul><li>Create timelapse video of the day<span></span></li></ul><p><iframe width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/CFkkasZFQ4k" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe></p><ul><li><a href="https://tinkererblog.wordpress.com/2015/07/22/raspberry-pi-camera-face-recognition-and-email-alert/" target="_blank">Human face recognition in backyard and email alert</a></li></ul><img src="https://tinkererblog.files.wordpress.com/2015/07/blog2.png"><ul><li>Streaming video to your phone with vlc on iPhone and Android (pet, garden and family member monitor)</li></ul><p><span></span>Performance of RainBerry during a 2.5 hour thunderstorm in Detroit, MI</p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/bq-tpEqPFo4" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p>RainBerry received a very good feedback in Detroit Maker 2015. Many makers wanted to buy it immediately and said it just looks like one in the store. It also received a huge response on <a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/raspberry_pi/comments/3fqo2e/designed_a_compact_weatherproof_case_rainberry/" target="_blank">social</a> media. A detailed blog post explaining full project details is available <a href="https://tinkererblog.wordpress.com/2015/07/28/how-i-designed-a-compact-weatherproof-raspberry-pi-case/">here</a>. </p><p>RainBerry case is highly useful for users who want to get introduced easily to Raspberry Pi, especially kids. <br></p><p>Many more uses are possible with RainBerry case.</p><ul><li>Night vision for nature trail camera</li><li>PIR for motion sensing and email for a DIY security camera</li><li>Weather monitoring</li><li>Bird watching</li><li>Video and sound streaming projects</li><li>Computer vision researchers (face, object, animal recognition, deep learning)</li><li>Plant growth monitoring</li><li>Star gazing</li></ul>
                                
                            </p>
