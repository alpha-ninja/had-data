                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">I am going to make a bicycle that can tell you the temperature, wind speed and direction, speed of your bike, and keep track of trip distance like a normal bike speedometer. I am going to use an arduino to process the data, a couple piezos to detect wind direction, a temperature sensor, a couple tilt sensors to know if you are going uphill or downhill, and a motor to switch gears. I will mount it all in a 3d printed case and hopefully it won't be too big.</div>
                </div>

                            <small class="authorship">3 months ago  &bull;

                                0 comments

                            <p><p>I am using a 16x2 lcd display with 16 pins. I hooked it up to the arduino (I am using an arduino uno for now but my finished product will use an arduino mini pro). There are going to be four different displays and a button to switch through them. </p><h2>Display 1  </h2><p>This display will show you how fast you are going(MPH or KPH). It will also show you what gear you are on (It will switch gears for you so your normal thing won't be accurate) and the wind speed and direction.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/8614781432122520674.JPG"></p><h2>Display 2</h2><p>This display will show you your trip information. How far you have gone and how long it took you. You will be able to reset the information by holding down the set button for three seconds.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/966961432122590678.JPG"></p><h2>Display 3</h2><p>This display will show you the time and if the gear three setting is on or off. When the gear 3 setting is off, the bike won't switch you to gear 3 (front gears). You will be able to press the set button to toggle Gear_3 on or off.  It will also show you the temperature (I couldn't use the degrease symbol so I had to substitute it with ^). </p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4222491432122675885.JPG"></p><h2>Display 4</h2><p>This display show you the total distance you have traveled on the bike.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7155331432122817643.JPG"></p><br>
