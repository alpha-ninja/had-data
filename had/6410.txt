                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">I'm using a TI TLV320AIC3204 codec and a PIC32MX150 to create a multifunction guitar effects unit. An OLED and rotary encoder provide menu selection. So far I have 13 effects programmed (pitch shift, Flanger, echo, chorus, etc.) and an E2 tuning tone, and I still haven't explored the TLV320's filters and onboard goodies. The '3204 is a stereo device, so the second channel can be a mic/aux input, a noise gate input, a pre-programmed sample output ... I'm open to suggestions.<br /><br />When I get this thing boxed up, I'll have to get some guitarist acquaintances together, and get opinions on which effects work and which ones suck! Until then, this thing will be one big scrollable menu of guitar effects.</div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>The TLV320 is currently running at 64K 32-bit samples/sec; samples are passed via SPI.  The PIC32 controls the TLV (via I2C), monitors the pedal position sensor (AS5055) once every 64 samples, and sends graphics to the OLED. </p><p>I chose the AS5055 magnetic position sensor for two reasons: 1) I wanted a non-contact solution to avoid wear, and 2) I found I needed better resolution than I could get out of a potentiometer and a 10-bit ADC. The AS5055 works as advertised, and the magnet alignment issue I was having was solved by using a different type of magnet.</p><p>As of now, the 13 effects I have are: 2 octave Pitch Shift, 1 octave Pitch Shift (for finer control), Flanger, Clipping, Soft Clip, &quot;Sag&quot;, Chorus (dry plus 1-3 voices), Echo, Harmony(dry+2 voices), Harmony(dry+1 voice), BitCrush, Static, FeedBack Comb filter. </p><p>I&apos;m torn over what feature to use when you push in  the encoder shaft; I can have it switch-on/switch-off an 82.45Hz tone for E2 tuning, or just Mute the outputs until it&apos;s released.<br></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6401671435177330458.jpg"></p><p>The image shows the working unit; my rev1 board has the encoder and pedal sensor all on one pcb; I just received updated pcb&apos;s with the encoder and sensor separate. The 5-pin ICSP header along the bottom was glued on for prototyping.</p><p>So for now, I have 3 tasks left: AS5055 magnet alignment, getting my &apos;theremin&apos; effect working, and designing a case.</p><p>6/25: Forget what I said about the AS5055 having a small sensing area; i just tried a small cabinet latch magnet I had laying around, and it works MUCH better than the bar magnets I was trying before. </p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3488441435268546871.jpg"></p><p>One problem down!</p><p>7/12:  Ran across a description of a Sample &amp; Hold; 13 lines of code later, I have my 15th effect. I&apos;ll have to play with the hold time to find the best sound.</p><p>7/22: Well, after a few days tracking down a bug caused by combined misreading of the PIC datasheet AND leaving some test code un-commented-out, I finally saw my waveforms change smoothly in response to the pedal magnet. YES!</p><p>Tonight I&apos;m off to the local makerspace (Sask TechWorks) to see about printing a case and components.</p><p>7/28: after various fits and starts, I got a &apos;learning prototype&apos; printed - as in &apos;&quot;now i get to see how bad my Sketchup skills are&quot;.  :)</p><p>Being that it was a flat box, the corners pulled up - oops, lesson learned. And I STILL  can&apos;t figure out how the Makerbot printed several centimeters of  (millimeter-sized) horizontal beams WITH NO SUPPORT STRUCTURE! How&apos;dtheydothat?</p><p>Anyhow, in spite of the pulling, the important areas printed fine, and showed me some changes that need to be made for ergonomics. All in all, for a first pass, I&apos;m satisfied. Tonight it&apos;s back to the &apos;space for more...<br></p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>

                    <h5>Project logs</h5>
                    
                    <ul class="buillogs-list post-content details-content">
                    
                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/6410/log/21801-the-hunt-continues">The Hunt Continues</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">19 days ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6410/log/21801#discussion-list">
                                0 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>7/29: I printed up a small jig to test pedal sensors. It lets me turn a magnet by hand over the  sensor; this helped me pinpoint a not-bug in the position sensor coding. It turns out the AS5055A output is 0-1FFF (13 bits) over 360 degrees; the datasheet must be referring to 12 bits over 180 degrees. Anyhoo, the position sensor is tracking fine now.</p><p>7/30: The last bug has been obliterated, all scope waveforms track on all effects - time for the acid test! I hooked up a guitar to the input, and the output to the Aux In jack of a cheapo countertop stereo, and ... a split-second of sound, and &lt;pop&gt; no audio. Power off/on, a split -second of sound, and &lt;pop&gt;. WTF?</p><p>I hooked in the oscillator I was testing with, everything&apos;s back to normal; at least nothing blew. &apos;Sample/Hold&apos; and &apos;Theremin&apos; sound totally *awesome* as you move the magnet! Most of the rest work as expected, but Chorus seems a bit odd; then again, it could be that I&apos;m getting cancelling effects from a single-frequency input tone.</p><p>After that, I tried the guitar again - NOW it decides to behave!
