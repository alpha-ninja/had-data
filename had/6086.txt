Portable, low-power thermoelectric systems are seen on the market today, but I was looking for one that would power an entire tiny house or at least supplement a larger one. The FireVolt does that, while providing all the domestic hot water you need along with it.

- The prototype has been in operation &gt; 7 months 
- A InStove 60L boils 15 gal. of water, using a small armload of sticks, in 35 minutes. 
- Fuels are wood, biomass briquettes, and/or pellets.
- The generator runs silently.
- The system is visible-smoke free (there are of course smoke emissions, but greatly reduced)
- TEGs, (the most costly component of the system), have a 200,000 hour service life!
- The pumps that drive the system consume &lt; 15 watts.">

                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Built upon the InStove 60L rocket stove (see <a href='http://instove.org' target="_blank">http://instove.org</a> ), the FireVolt Hydronic Thermoelectric Core is a scalable, solid state, power production system rated at 250+ Watts DC Electrical output, (with 500+ Watts DC capable on the InStove 100L).  Portable, low-power thermoelectric systems are seen on the market today, but I was looking for one that would power an entire tiny house or at least supplement a larger one. The FireVolt does that, while providing all the domestic hot water you need along with it.<br /><br />- The prototype has been in operation > 7 months <br />- A InStove 60L boils 15 gal. of water, using a small armload of sticks, in 35 minutes. <br />- Fuels are wood, biomass briquettes, and/or pellets.<br />- The generator runs silently.<br />- The system is visible-smoke free (there are of course smoke emissions, but greatly reduced)<br />- TEGs, (the most costly component of the system), have a 200,000 hour service life!<br />- The pumps that drive the system consume < 15 watts.</div>

                    <div class="section section-instructions">
                        <h5>Build instructions</h5>
                        
                        
                            <ul class="post-content section-instructions-list">
                                
                                    <li>
                                        <span class="instruction-number">1</span>
                                        <div class="instruction-list-item"><p>Step one is to get yourself an <a href="http://instove.org/60-100-liter-cookstove" target="_blank">InStove 60L</a> and get familiar with it. Boil some water, try different fuels, play around. Everything related to the build is downstream from this. At worst, if this is as far as you get,  you'll be able to cook huge amounts of food and heat 15 gallons of water on a very small carbon-neutral fuel budget. But of course, that's not the only end goal. We're here to generate Electricity. So... step 2</p></div>
                                    </li>
                                
                                    <li>
                                        <span class="instruction-number">2</span>
                                        <div class="instruction-list-item"><p>Figure out your TEG budget.  The FireVolt System is <em>scalable</em>.  You can invest as little as $350 in the TEG side of a viable build with room to grow as your budget allows.  In such a configuration your max output would be around 35 Watts, matched load at 9v DC, with a TEG block occupying only 1/6th of the core.  The remainder of the Core walls could be water block / heat harvesters only, or simply insulated dummy panels made of 1/4&quot; steel. <br><br>p.s. this is a no-weld build! (believe it or not) - more on that later - but going forward the instructions assume you are building a full 6-sided TEG core.</p></div>
                                    </li>
                                
                                    <li>
                                        <span class="instruction-number">3</span>
                                        <div class="instruction-list-item"><p>Cut 1/4&quot; Aluminum T-beam to 10&quot; lengths, de-burr any sharp edges. With 300 grit paper, sand the face you&apos;re mounting your TEG block to so that it is well-polished.</p></div>
                                    </li>
                                
                            </ul>
