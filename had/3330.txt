                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">A while back Warren and I designed a breakout / dev board for the ATMega32u2 (a very nice microcontroller which has native USB functionality, but does not have a through hole part for easy prototyping).  The original uBBB was also a test for SMD soldering, as we had not done that before.<br /><br />Fast forward to now: the uBBB boards have been used in various projects, and the itch for something new is here.  More importantly, the 32u4 is now available in TQFP packaging!  In addition to having more I/O pins, it also has an ADC, and is only $1 or so more.<br /><br />This project documents the process of this dev board build.<br /><br />(uBBB stands for 'Micro Bare Bones Board; this is a play off of an early Arduino-compatible board that I used in the past and liked for its simplicity: the RBBB - Really Bare Bones Board -  from <a href='http://moderndevice.com)' target="_blank">http://moderndevice.com)</a></div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>As with most of my dev boards, I try to keep things simple but functional.  This board includes:</p><ul><li>a Mini USB B connector along with all required USB hardware (just plug it in, and you are good to go)</li><li>a RGB LED for status / debugging</li><li>all required components such as pullups on /RST and /HWB, decoupling capacitors, crystal, etc</li><li>800 mil wide, 100 mil pitch, breadboard compatible holes for pins</li></ul><div>That's it.  No frills, no 50 mil spaced headers, just raw USB-compatible goodness.</div> 
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>
