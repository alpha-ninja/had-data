                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">Meet Andy, a multi-purpose humanoid robot built with a Raspberry Pi and miscellaneous components. Andy can walk,  take pictures, record video, and answer questions. Andy is voice controlled, so there is no need for a computer or smartphone to send him commands. Just say "Andy" wait for the beep, say your command, and Andy will do the rest. <br /><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /></div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><strong>System Design</strong><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7973141412014993659.png"></p><p>Andy has a SPST switch on his side that will power him on. When this switch is flipped, power from the lipo battery is converted to 5v and then sent to the Raspberry Pi and servos. The Raspberry Pi will start up and <a href="https://github.com/spadgenske/ANDY" target="_blank">Andy's software</a> will start. Andy is now operational. A more detailed overview of the hardware can be found in this project log. [link coming soon]</p><p>Once the Pi has booted up, you can start giving Andy commands. To give Andy a command, it is as simple as saying "Andy" waiting for the high pitched beep, and saying your command. If you give Andy the "shutdown" command, the Pi will log off and you can safely turn off the SPST switch. A more detailed overview of the software can be found <a href="http://hackaday.io/project/1205/log/4466">here</a>.</p><p>Andy can also be logged on over a network. If Andy is connected to the internet, you can log into Andy's client program and send him commands via text.</p><p>

                            <h2><a href="/project/1205/log/13284-rebuilding-andy-again">Rebuilding Andy Again...</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">7 months ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/1205/log/13284#discussion-list">
                                2 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>Well,  I haven't been on Hackaday for awhile so I thought I would write about the progress I am having with Andy. I dismantled him again, because I wasn't liking the DIY look and the resistors I used in between the Raspberry Pi GPIO and the servo control line were messing things up more than they were helping. So, now Andy looks something like this:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4797811422052613979.JPG"></p><p>The MG996R Servos used to make Andy walk were drawing more current than the 1K resistors could handle. So, I removed them. Then, I made Andy walk faster, and then it happened.</p><p>Andy walked right off the table, and the perfboard cracked. The metal legs and electronics survived, but instead of re soldering everything to a new perfboard, I decided to make a Andy 3.0. But, I stalled and built a <a href="http://hackaday.io/project/3721">Game Show Console</a>. Now, I plan on continuing work on Andy. The new design will consist of a Raspberry Pi A+ or B+ (most likely the A+ because it is cheaper) and a <a href="https://www.adafruit.com/product/2327" target="_blank">servo hat</a>. The torso and head will most likely be 3D printed instead of perfboard to make it look nicer and stronger. I don't know when I will get to the rebuilding, hopefully I can get to it this spring or summer.  So stay tuned! EDIT: If you can, because I have no idea on how you tune a web browser.</p>
                                
                            </p>

                            <p><p>A final working version of Andy 2.0 is still on hold, because it takes a couple weeks to get parts from China. While I'm waiting, all I can do is work on software, so here is what I've done.&nbsp;</p><p>When you give Andy the Take Picture or take video command, Andy will take the picture or video, and save it to his SD card. Normally, if you would want to view the picture/video you would have to either:</p><ul><li><span></span><span>Shutdown Andy, connect a mouse and keyboard and HDMI screen, log in, start X, open picture file.</span><span></span></li><li><span></span><span>Log into him via</span><span>&nbsp;SSH, start Xming, open image in Midori.&nbsp;</span><span></span></li></ul><p>Not any more. Now, after Andy takes a picture, if he is connected to the Internet, he will automatically upload the picture to dropbox, so&nbsp;any computer connected to it can view and copy the file.&nbsp;</p><p>Once the file is transferred, Dropbox will give you a handy desktop notification.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1475381412014692304.PNG"></p><p>Just click, and you will be able to view the picture you just took.</p>
