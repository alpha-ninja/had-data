                            <small class="authorship">18 days ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4260/log/21896#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-21896"><p>Natural Language Processing (NLP) deals with deciphering spoken language grammar and  our understanding of the same. So far my app had two or three words commands. And coincidentally they followed a pattern. Like &quot;play movie batman&quot; or &quot;switch lights on&quot;. I broke it down as:</p><ol><li>The device that should handle the command</li><li>The function</li><li>Optional additional data required to perform the function</li></ol><p>However, for mood light, this pattern breaks down. One is more likely to say &quot;color device red&quot; or &quot;color all green&quot;. Got to spend some time thinking of a sensible algorithm to handle this possibly using NLP. </p></div>
