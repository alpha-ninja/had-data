                <div class="section section-description">
                    <h5>Description</h5>
                    <div class="description">I was working on another project which can be found here:<br /><br /><a href='https://hackaday.io/project/3944-multirotor-camera-platform-for-extreme-sports' target="_blank">https://hackaday.io/project/3944-multirotor-camera-platform-for-extreme-sports</a> <br /><br />That project was to take a quadcopter frame and see how I could modify it and tweak it to fulfil a need.<br /><br />I decided to start another project to build an agile AP platform from scratch. I started designing and intended to cut most of the frame parts and motor mounts from either glass or carbon fibre.<br /><br />It occurred to me that glass/carbon fibre plate is very expensive and before I let my CNC machine start chewing it up I should cut the parts from something cheaper to make sure everything fits together.<br /><br />I looked around my workshop and my eye caught a sheet of 3mm MDF (Medium Density Fibreboard).<br /><br />It's about 1mm thicker than the carbon plate but I figured what the hell. Once the first few pieces were cut I bolted them together and realised that they were actually pretty strong... so much so I built the whole copter out of it!!<br /></div>
                </div>
            

            
                <div class="section section-details">
                    <h5>
                        
                            Details
                        
                    </h5>
                    

                    
                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><p>The main benefit to this material is obviously the cost.</p>  <p>MDF Sheet 3mm Thick x 912mm Length x 607mm Height<span class="redactor-invisible-space">= £3.49</span></p>  <p><span class="redactor-invisible-space">I worked out that making the frame and motor mounts from one of these sheets would give me enough to build 3 copters... that £1.17 per copter!!</span></p>  <p><span class="redactor-invisible-space">The second benefit is that the fibreboard does a fantastic job of absorbing vibrations (the bane of flight controllers everywhere!!!)</span></p>  <p><span class="redactor-invisible-space">This was made very apparent as the first version of the copter had 1mm carbon fibre motor mounts and the vibrations were terrible, so much so at certain RPM's the copter was un-flyable. Now they are MDF the copter flies with no vibration issues at all.</span></p>
                                
                            </p>
                        </div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/6644/log/20635-version-3">Version 3</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a month ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6644/log/20635#discussion-list">
                                0 comments
                            </a>
                            </small>
                            <p><p>After much testing it turns out that long arms and 9" props in a Y4 configuration has almost no yaw authority at all!!</p>  <p>It made the copter practically useless for AP as you couldn't keep it straight let alone pan a shot.</p>  <p>So I decided to go with a standard tricopter and got myself one of these:</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3808471436342204464.jpg"></p><p>It's a ToughTilt mechanism from flitetest.com and it's rock solid. It has an almost hydraulic feel to the movement.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/8985701436342343800.jpg"><span class="redactor-invisible-space">After a test with the 9" props it was obvious I needed something bigger.<br></span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space"><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/5964071436342647794.JPG"><span class="redactor-invisible-space">So I went with 850kv motors and 11x5" props. This gives me a nice 50% hover:<br></span></span></p><p><span class="redactor-invisible-space"><span class="redactor-invisible-space"><br></span></span></p><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/nX1do9V36qI" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe>
                                
                            </p>
                        </li>
                    
                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/6644/log/20483-cut-paint-repeat">Cut, Paint, Repeat</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">a month ago  &bull;
                            <a class="gray-link" href="/project/6644/log/20483#discussion-list">
                                0 comments
                            </a>
