                            <h2><a href="/project/5152/log/16286-raspberry-pi-vs-intel-edison">Raspberry Pi vs Intel Edison</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">4 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5152/log/16286#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-16286"><p>Today, I will be comparing the Raspberry Pi against the Intel Edison and figuring out what is more appropriate for Dusty to use. The Raspberry Pi is an common microprocessor used widely in education and DIY communities.The intel Edison is an newly released board which is specifically designed for the Internet of Things (Iot).</p><p><img src="http://img.pandawhale.com/post-50521-How-Intel-s-Edison-Stacks-Up-A-NjGr.png"></p><p>Looking at the table above,we can see that the Edicon is more powerful than both the Raspberry Pi B+ and the Arduino Uno. It also comes with both Bluetooth 4.0 and Dual Band Wifi on the board itself. However, the Edison does not have a display output unlike the Raspberry Pi. This means that we cannot have any on-board display on Dusty itself and all the interfacing with Dusty will be through an external device connected to the internet. This could be through an app or an webpage. This could be quite more difficult to use that the simple on screen instructions that the Raspberry Pi can produce on an on-board display. However, the Raspberry Pi has its own share of disadvantages involved. It is quite power-hungry and on of the primary aims of Dusty is to have a long enough battery life. Assuming that the garbage collection happens once a week, the battery on board Dusty needs to have a minimum battery life of at least a week. The Intel Edison however has a very low power consumption and is also more compact to occupy less space in Dusty although there needs to be additions of custom breakout boards which will be designed by me. Furthermore,since the Intel Edison has an on-board wifi and Bluetooth, it can connect to any external peripherals extremely easily. There is also no need to have any ethernet cables to connect Dusty to the internet as it can connect to any near wifi hotspots. However,in remote locations, ethernet cables will be necessary but in populated areas this should not be a problem. I think that comparing the two of them,the intel Edison will be a better choice for Dusty. What do you think? I would greatly appreciate it if you could leave some advice below.Thanks!</p></div>
                            </div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/5152/log/17174-design-overhaul">Design Overhaul</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">4 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/5152/log/17174#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-17174"><p>After some consideration, I have decided that I will build a foam prototype of Dusty to kickstart things off. The prototype will use an Arduino Uno as its primary controller and a bluetooth module. This is where the LightBlue Bean might come in handy.The design has also been changed dramatically. Instead of using a trapdoor mechanism with servos to pull up lids,  I have simplified the system to use just one motor. This works by dividing a circular bin into sectors with each sector corresponding to a certain material. Then we can use a motor to spin a lid which is on a bin and this lid only has one sector exposed. When we spin this lid with a motor, it we open only one sector of the bin at anytime allowing us to accomplish the classification using just one motor.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6865721430440952030.jpg" style="width: 534px; height: 221.890410958904px;"></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;">Electronics wise, I will be using an arduino Uno connected to a custom shield which drives the single motor. The bluetooth module will be connected to the arduino and I will be making an app for android phones to interface with Dusty. The app should allow me to monitor waste using several sensors connected to the arduino as well such as a laser gate and force sensor. This should give me a reasonable working prototype that has basic functionality.</p></div>
