                            <h2><a href="/project/4900/log/15588-concept">Concept</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">5 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4900/log/15588#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-15588"><p>Problem:</p><p>As people get older, they tend to need more medications. Unfortunately, some elderly people begin having difficulty self-administering medications. Focusing on pills, here are some of the difficulties encountered:</p> <ul><li>Pills are too small to separate</li> <li>Medication regimes can be difficult to understand</li> <li>Forgetting to take pills</li> <li>Taking too many doses in a day</li> <li>Losing track of the day/date/AM or PM/time</li> <li>Forgetting to re-order pills</li> <li>Opening bottles</li></ul> <p>The goal of this project is to mitigate as many pill-taking issues as possible. The end product should try to address as many of the following goals as possible:</p> <ul><li>Easy to use</li> <li>No fine motor control necessary</li> <li>Understand or help interpret medication directions</li> <li>Dispense pills at correct times</li> <li>Remind user to take dispensed pills</li> <li>Display time/date info in easy to read/understand way</li> <li>Re-order or remind to re-order pills</li> <li>Help open the bottle</li></ul><p><br><span></span></p> <p>Ideas:</p> <ul><li>Make the system modular by having a separate container/unit for each pill type</li> <li>Use a rotating disk (at an angle) with holes in it for pill dispensing</li> <li>(or) use a tube with insert for pill holding</li> <li>Make the modular system provide power to the next module</li> <li>Make the modular system provide force to the next module</li> <li>Use a combination lock style mechanism to open/close a dispensing gate for each module</li> <li>Use a base station that has all the motors/electronics</li> <li>Sense a new module with some sort of cascade circuitry</li> <li>Have a "top" module for inserting pills</li></ul><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/2288911427215247642.png"></p></div>
                            </div>

                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-15627"><p>After much thinking, I've narrowed down the dispensing mechanism to 2 ideas - a rotating plate (with holes) and a tube with plate.</p><p>The rotating plate is easy to load (just dump in the pills), but it unfortunately takes up a lot of space. The tube is very compact, but it is hard to load.</p><p><span></span><br></p><p>I looked at some other methods, such as a shaker plate or a conveyor, but these were eliminated because of size/noise/impracticality.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/1241571427295915000.png"></p></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/4900/log/15832-concept-rotating-plate">Concept - Rotating Plate</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">5 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4900/log/15832#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-15832"><p>I spent about a week fleshing out the plate concept idea. Some things that had to be figured out were:</p><ul><li>How to add pills</li><li>How to remove pills</li><li>How to make it as efficient as possible</li></ul><p>I chose to combine the pill adding and subtracting together - using the same mechanism for both. To make this efficient, a combination lock mechanism is used to select the orientation of each "plug" individually using only one motor. The plugs would have to click into place so they don't get dislodged. <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CZ8WRDVgKrk">Here's a project (video:youtube) by Matthias Wandel that shows the basic mechanism</a>. Below is a sketch of my ideas.</p><p><span></span><br></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/8377311427739265405.png"></p><p>The whole mechanism would have to be tilted to make the plate system work, so I make a sketch of the unit.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4360951427740529114.png"></p>Not satisfied with just sketches, I modeled the concept. Below are some renderings.<p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/7639831427740592933.png"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3972721427740610597.png"></p><p>Now to evaluate the concept.</p><p>Pros:</p><ul><li>(Hopefully) easy to load</li><li>Only requires 2 motors</li></ul><p>Cons:</p><ul><li>The more compartments, the longer and wider it gets</li><li>Rotating plates need to be changed per pill type</li></ul><p>Things that aren't quite figured out yet:</p><ul><li>Rotating plate changing</li><li>Load and pass-through holes can't be at 180 degrees from each other</li></ul></div>

                            <small class="authorship">5 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4900/log/15989#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-15989"><p>Another concept involves tubes loaded with pills. </p><p>If a pharmacy were to build their own home pill dispenser, tube "cartridges" would probably be the best method for dispensing. The cartridges would be a standard size, but the internal diameter and dispensing mechanism would be tailored for the pills. Each cartridge could come with an NFC tag containing dosing instructions.</p><p>Unfortunately, we have to deal will arbitrary pill sizes and load the tubes ourselves. The concept has a place to add pills, and mechanism to auto-load tubes, and a dispensing mechanism.</p><p><span></span><br></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/5451851427988685542.png"></p><p>The tubes would have to match the individual pill diameters. Several dispensing plates of different thicknesses could be added to the bottom, allowing a carousel to rotate the correct tube and dispense one pill at a time.</p><p>Below are concept renderings of the mechanism.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3346941427988910785.png"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/8027761427988924141.png"></p><p>And here is a quick video of the dispensing mechanism. There is a rotating cam with a spring loaded following pin. The cam rotates one direction to select the correct dispensing plate, then rotates the other direction to dispense a pill.</p><iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/hrgLGCHFlJk" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p>Pros:</p><ul><li>(Hopefully) easy to load</li><li>High density storage</li><li>Easy to clean tubes</li></ul><p>Cons:</p><ul><li>Tubes need to be changed out for each pill type</li><li>Loading pills and dispensing might be inconsistent</li></ul><p>Things that aren't quite figured out yet:</p><ul><li>Exact dispensing mechanism</li><li>Exact pill loading method</li></ul></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/4900/log/15990-concept-containers-cascading">Concept - Containers (Cascading)</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">5 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4900/log/15990#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-15990"><p>This concept departs from the "store pills and dispense them one at a time" theme. Instead, doses (one or more pills) are held in individual containers. By making a rotating carousel of fins, and clever top/bottom plates, a high-density cascading system can be created.</p><p><span></span><br></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4992671427990165663.png"></p><p>Unfortunately, the system can only be indexed one way, so if the system is filled half way (and then dispenses) the system will have to empty it's entire inventory before more pills can be added (or else there will be a pill gap). </p><p>To facilitate multiple pill types, more systems will have to be added (possibly in a carousal). Below are some renderings of what that would look like.</p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/5534451427990272295.png"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/8362951427990285553.png"></p><p>Pros:</p><ul><li>High density storage</li><li>Easy to dispense</li></ul><p>Cons:</p><ul><li>Possibly hard to clean</li><li>Hard to combine multiple systems</li></ul><p>Things that aren't quite figured out yet:</p><ul><li>Combining multiple systems</li><li>Loading</li><li>Dispensing efficiently</li><li>Adding a collection spot</li></ul></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/4900/log/15994-concept-containers-stacked">Concept - Containers (Stacked)</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">5 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4900/log/15994#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-15994"><p>The last log details a tall, skinny, cascading storage system. This concept is a fat, short system that does not cascade. Each pill type is stored on a different level (in a veined carousel), with a shared shaft to add/remove pills. Unfortunately, this adds complexity, with a "shuttle" to access different levels (for adding pills) and possibly a second shuttle to rotate individual levels.</p><p>I was also toying with the idea of a shake conveyor, in this case vibrating a piece of sloped angle aluminum. Below are some renderings.</p><p><span></span><br></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3295521427991439984.png"></p><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6157381427991460166.png"></p><p>If I can control the orientation of the pills, I can make the containers more efficient. In this case, I made them about 1"x0.5"x0.5". In a 12" cube I'm fitting 500 containers. I was also experimenting with an idea to get the pill delivery shuttle out of the shaft at it's lowest position.</p><p>Pros:</p><ul><li>(Hopefully) easy to load</li><li>High density storage</li><li>Somewhat universal system (no changing parts)</li></ul><p>Cons:</p><ul><li>Complex (with tight mechanical tolerances)</li><li>Loading pills and dispensing will be slow</li></ul><p>Things that aren't quite figured out yet:</p><ul><li>Loading</li><li>Sensor locations</li></ul></div>

                        <li>
                            <h2><a href="/project/4900/log/15998-concept-what-to-choose">Concept - What to choose?</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">5 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4900/log/15998#discussion-list">
                                    5 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-15998"><p>Up to this point, four virtual prototypes have been made.</p><ol><li>Rotating Plate</li><li>Tube</li><li>Cascading Container</li><li>Stacked Container</li></ol><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/3871721427999995527.png"></p><p><span></span>I've learned a lot making these prototypes - more than I can possibly write about. Let my try to give a general outline of what the machine should do.</p><ul><li>Accept Pills<ul><li>Separate, catalog and verify if necessary</li><li>With as much automation and as little required dexterity as possible</li></ul></li><li>Store Pills<ul><li>Pills can either be stored  in an orderly manor or in bunches; in containers or tubes</li></ul></li><li>Dispense Pills<br>
<ul><li>Separate, verify if necessary</li><li>Verify pills have been taken</li><li>Easy to use/take pills</li></ul></li></ul><table>     <tbody><tr> <td>#</td> <td>1</td> <td>2</td> <td>3</td> <td>4</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Name</td> <td>Rotating Plate</td> <td>Tube</td> <td>Cascading Container</td> <td>Stacked Container</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Accept Pills</td> <td>TBD,<br> Requires verification</td> <td>TBD,<br> Does not require verification</td> <td>TBD,<br> Requires verification</td> <td>TBD,<br> Requires verification</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Store Pills</td> <td>Stored in containers,<br> Not orderly</td> <td>Stored in tubes,<br> Tube must match pill type,<br> Orderly</td> <td>Stored in container,<br> Orderly</td> <td>Stored in container,<br> Orderly</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Dispense Pills</td> <td>Rotating plate,<br> Plates must match pill type</td> <td>Reciprocating plate (Probably),<br> Plate must match pill type</td> <td>Rotate storage mechanism</td> <td>Rotate storage mechanism</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Notes</td> <td>Simple but requires mechanism changes</td> <td>Simple but requires mechanism changes</td> <td>Complex</td> <td>Complex</td></tr></tbody></table><p>In working with these designs, I've (probably) narrowed it down to two choices: the tubes (#2) and the stacked containers (#4). I'm not sure I can properly explain the design decisions, but I believe these mechanisms represent the best choice for two competing strategies.</p><p>If we assume that people buy this device, bring it home, and use it with their existing medication, a stacked container (#4) is best. If we go with the ink-jet model where people buy "cartridges" from a pharmacy or online. the tube (#2) is best.</p><p>I envision the stacked containers (#4) to be quite complex, but versatile (able to accept pills from prescription bottles). Pills need to be separated, verified, and added to a container. Dosage data also needs to be imputed. Pills will then be dispensed and verified again.</p><p>Using concept #2 (the tubes) bypasses a lot of steps. Pills will be put in the tubes at the factory/pharmacy and verified. Placing a cartridge (tube) in the machine would be easy, and the dispense mechanism would be built into the individual tube (haven't figured that out yet, but I envision something similar to a pen or mechanical pencil). The dosage data could be pre-loaded onto the tube (NFC). The machine would only have to keep track of the tubes and activate the dispense mechanisms at the correct time - a considerably simpler process.</p><p>Now, the question remains - #2 or #4?</p></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/4900/log/16177-project-direction-an-appeal-for-input">Project Direction - An appeal for input</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">4 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4900/log/16177#discussion-list">
                                    2 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-16177"><p>I have a choice to make regarding the direction of the project, and I'd like to solicit feedback from the community. As I see it, my pill dispensing project can now go one of two ways:</p><ol><li>Universal solution (Accept most pills, from pill bottles - complex and possibly unreliable)</li><li>Cartridge style (Only accept "Pill Minder" cartridges - simple, but possibly proprietary)</li></ol><p>Currently, I'm only planning on making a few "finished" machines - one for my grandfather (who has Alzheimer's) and one or two for family friends. But, I want to make the best product I can make, which leads me to an interesting hypothetical.</p><p>Who is my target market, and what do I want to do with this machine? Do I want to partner with a large company like Walgreens and have my product only work with Walgreens branded cartridges? This would probably impact the largest number of people. Do I want a universal pill solution - as-seen-on-TV? My target demographic definitely overlaps with theirs. There are clear pros and cons of each choice. Please tell me what you think.</p></div>

                            <h2><a href="/project/4900/log/16533-project-direction-resolved">Project Direction - Resolved</a></h2>
                            <small class="authorship">4 months ago  &bull;
                                <a class="gray-link" href="/project/4900/log/16533#discussion-list">
                                    0 comments
                                </a>
                                </small>
                            <div style="position:relative">
                                <div id="post-body-16533"><p>After an in depth discussion, we've decided to go with the cartridge style (option 2) design.
