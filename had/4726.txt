                        <div class="post-content details-content">
                            <p><h1>Turn Your Headphones Down Before Watching Video</h1><iframe style="width: 500px; height: 281px;" width="500" height="281" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/j2qYdlXqHxs" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><h1><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/6881101439817826157.jpg"></h1><p><img src="https://cdn.hackaday.io/images/4932641439818154824.jpg"></p><p>I live in Wyoming, an area of the USthat&apos;s entire economy is based on energy, energy that often isn&apos;tvery environmentally friendly.  From doing lab work for a companythat sells uranium to the Japanese TEPCO that more than likely endedup being apart of the fukushima disaster, to working in the oilexploration industry that lead to recent shale oil directionaldrilling exploitation, I have been intrinsically involved in thenegative effects of energy production.  With the majority of coalbeing mined in Wyoming and the recent discovery of a large lithiumdeposit in the western area of the state more environmental impactwill be attributed to energy production and utilization in the state.   We have also been developing largeamounts of wind energy do to the wide open expanses and near constantwind, wind energy that mars our skylines and kills our birds of preywithout being a clear alternative to conventional energy production. Being recently unemployed and being urged by friends and family toreturn to energy production of the conventional type, I have decidedto reject that idea completely.</p><p>&#x201C;Doing something that matters&#x201D; hasreally resonated with me in that I feel guilty for participating inthese industries that threaten the future of the planets flora andfauna, as well as humans ability to survive.  </p><p>The temporary solution until a grailtechnology such as cold fusion, or zero point energy can be utilizedI feel is the development of a more energy dense method of energyproduction than wind power with the low environmental impact ofsolar.  Coupled with that is the ability to store energy at low costand even lower environmental impact without the use of rare earthmetals such as neodymium and ever dwindling lithium deposits.  In thesame vein a method of transportation that can utilize the energystored, is paramount.</p><p>A power generator based on solar powered thermal contraction of artificial muscles should provide more power per square meter than does photovoltaics. This energy can then be stored in Ultra-capacitors tied to the grid, or converted into hydrogen via electrolysis of water. </p><p>A hydrogen fuel cell powered vehicle can supply both steam and electricity to actuate the muscles and provide locomotion. Ultra-capacitors can be used in conjunction with an electromagnetic brake to regenerate power that would normally go to waste as heat with brake pads.</p><p>This is why I have begun research onCarbon Nanotube Ultracapacitors and Artificial Muscles with what I believe to posses atheoretical higher energy density and power density than lithium ionbattery&apos;s.  When the barrier of 200 watt hours per kilogram isbreached they will be the defacto energy storage medium as they havea life charge cycle in the 100 of thousands compared to lithium&apos;sions paltry 500-2000.  With artificial muscles that boast a millionactuation cycles, and a power to weight ratio greater than a jetengine coupled with a low cost hydrogen fuel cell viable alternative transportation can be realized. Solar energy production of hydrogen, and utilization in transportation can be greatlyimproved and environmental impact vastly reduced.  </p><p>This is what I am doing that matters.</p><p>Open Hardware Licence GNU</p>

                    <div class="section section-instructions">
                        <h5>Build instructions</h5>
                        
                        
                            <ul class="post-content section-instructions-list">
                                
                                    <li>
                                        <span class="instruction-number">1</span>
                                        <div class="instruction-list-item"><p>Step 1.</p><p>So you have recieved some cheap 90% clean multi walled carbon nanotubes.  That's great but they are pretty useless as is, the first order of business is to clean and functionalize them using nitric acid , 30% hydrogen peroxide and a microwave.  </p><p>Cover your work area with disposable materials that you can throw away, don long gloves, safety glasses, lab coat, covered toe boots and a respirator before cracking the container that contains the nanotubes.  The respirator should be able to filter organics and fine particulates.  It would be best to do this in a filtered fume hood but it is not neccessary if you are careful and don't create alot of dust.  </p><p>Put any amount into a tared mason jar and record the weight.  Cover the mason jar with microwavable stretch plastic. Microwave them without any addition for 15 seconds.  They will spark and sputter this is the iron left over being conglomerated.  Take a neodymium n52 magnet glued to a string and swish it around inside the jar to grab any of the conglomerated iron.  <br></p><p>Nitric acid is pretty benign but H202 can start fires very easily. Have a fire extinguisher on hand.</p><p>Add either nitric acid or h202 to the jar just covering the nanotubes.  The nitrogen functionalized tubes will be for the ultracapacitors and the carboxylized(oxidized) tubes will be used in the muscles.  Cover the jar again with plastic wrap. Poke a few holes in the wrap to relieve pressure.  Microwave again untill the fluid starts to boil.  Let it set and continue to microwave in intervals untill all the fluid is boiled off.</p><p>The nitric acid will fume and you definitely don't want to breathe it so wear the respirator that filters organics.  DONT DO THIS IN YOUR HOUSE.  The H202 will also release oxygen so have no flames present, if a plasma develops in the jar, immediately turn the microwave off.  </p><p>Once all the fluid has boiled off, you can now move on to the next step.</p></div>
